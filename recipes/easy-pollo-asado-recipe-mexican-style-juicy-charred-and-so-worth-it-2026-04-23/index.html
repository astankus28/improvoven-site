Easy Pollo Asado Recipe Mexican Style (Juicy, Charred, and So Worth It)

Published April 23, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy pollo asado recipe Mexican style is the kind of dish that makes your whole block wonder what's going on at your house — smoky, citrusy, and packed with that deep achiote flavor that just hits different. You don't need a fancy grill or a long ingredient list, just some pantry staples and chicken you probably already have. This one's going straight into your regular rotation, trust me.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
40 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs or drumsticks, bone-in skin-on
  • 3 tablespoons achiote paste (or 2 teaspoons ground annatto powder)
  • 3 tablespoons fresh orange juice (about 1 orange)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 1 lime)
  • 3 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano (Mexican oregano if you have it)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Optional for serving: warm tortillas, sliced white onion, fresh cilantro, lime wedges

Instructions

  1. 1Make your marinade: In a bowl, combine the achiote paste, orange juice, lime juice, white vinegar, minced garlic, cumin, oregano, smoked paprika, black pepper, salt, and oil. Whisk it all together until smooth. If you're using achiote paste and it's not breaking down easily, just mash it with a fork first — it'll loosen up once the citrus hits it.
  2. 2Score and coat the chicken: Using a sharp knife, make 2-3 shallow cuts into the thickest parts of each piece of chicken. This lets the marinade get all the way in, not just sit on top. Add the chicken to the bowl or a zip-lock bag, pour the marinade over, and toss to coat every piece really well.
  3. 3Let it marinate: If you have time, let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes at room temperature, or cover and refrigerate for up to 8 hours. Even 30 minutes makes a real difference. The citrus and vinegar are doing serious work here.
  4. 4Cook the chicken — grill method: Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Lightly oil the grates and place chicken skin-side down. Grill for about 12-14 minutes per side, flipping once, until the skin is charred in spots and the internal temperature reaches 165°F. That char is not a mistake — it's the whole point.
  5. 5Cook the chicken — oven method: If you're going oven route, preheat to 425°F. Place chicken on a foil-lined baking sheet and roast for 35-40 minutes, then switch to broil for the last 3-4 minutes to get that gorgeous color on the outside.
  6. 6Rest and serve: Let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before cutting in. Serve with warm tortillas, fresh cilantro, sliced white onion, and extra lime wedges on the side. A little salsa verde or sliced avocado on the table never hurt anybody either.
💡 Improv Tip

Achiote paste is the secret soul of this dish — look for it in the Latin aisle of most grocery stores or at any Latin market for under $2. If you absolutely cannot find it, mix 2 teaspoons of annatto powder with a tiny bit of oil to make a quick paste. It won't be identical but it'll still give you that gorgeous deep red color and earthy flavor that makes pollo asado look and taste like you really know what you're doing.
