Easy Meatless Friday Lunch: Crispy Black Bean Tostadas with Avocado Crema

Published April 1, 2026 · Improv Oven

When you need an easy meatless Friday recipe that actually feels like a treat and not a punishment, these black bean tostadas are your answer. We're talking crispy corn tortillas piled high with smoky refried black beans, fresh pico, and a dreamy avocado crema — all from stuff you probably already have in the pantry. This is the kind of meal that makes you forget there was ever meat on the menu.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
10 mins
Total
25 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1/4 cup sour cream or plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice (about 1 lime)
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
  • 2 Roma tomatoes, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage or iceberg lettuce
  • 1/2 cup crumbled cotija or feta cheese
  • Hot sauce for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Make your pico de gallo first so it has time to come together. In a small bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, red onion, jalapeño, and half the cilantro. Add a pinch of salt and a squeeze of lime, stir, and set aside.
  2. 2Brush both sides of each corn tortilla lightly with olive oil. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium-high heat and toast the tortillas 2 to 3 minutes per side until they're golden, crispy, and have a few dark spots. Work in batches and set them on a wire rack or paper towel — don't stack them or they'll steam and get soggy.
  3. 3In the same skillet, add a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add the black beans, cumin, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and salt. Stir everything together and cook for about 5 minutes, mashing roughly half the beans with the back of a spoon as you go. You want a chunky, spreadable texture — not totally smooth. Add a splash of water if it gets too thick.
  4. 4While the beans are going, make your avocado crema. Scoop the avocados into a bowl, add the sour cream, remaining lime juice, and a pinch of salt. Mash it all together until it's creamy with just a few small chunks. Taste and adjust salt and lime.
  5. 5Now build your tostadas. Spread a generous layer of black beans on each crispy tortilla, then spoon on the avocado crema. Top with shredded cabbage, a big spoonful of pico, and a shower of cotija cheese and fresh cilantro. Finish with your favorite hot sauce and eat immediately while they're still crunchy.
💡 Improv Tip

The number one tostada mistake is building them too early — once the beans and crema hit that tortilla, the clock is ticking. Set everything out assembly-line style and let everyone build their own right before eating. Also, if your avocados are rock hard (we've all been there), just do a chunky guacamole instead and skip the crema — a little extra lime and salt and nobody will know the difference.
