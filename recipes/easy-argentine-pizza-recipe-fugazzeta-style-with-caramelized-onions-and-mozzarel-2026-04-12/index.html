Easy Argentine Pizza Recipe (Fugazzeta Style with Caramelized Onions and Mozzarella)

Published April 12, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Argentine pizza recipe brings Buenos Aires straight to your kitchen — think thick, focaccia-like dough buried under a mountain of sweet caramelized onions and melted mozzarella, no tomato sauce in sight. It sounds fancy but it's one of the most forgiving pizzas you'll ever make, and your wallet is going to thank you. Once you try fugazzeta, you're going to wonder why regular pizza ever had your heart.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
45 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast (one standard packet)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, plus more for the pan
  • 3 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons butter or additional olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt for the onions
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano, plus more for topping
  • 2 cups shredded low-moisture mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Optional: red pepper flakes for serving

Instructions

  1. 1In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Give it a gentle stir and let it sit for about 8 to 10 minutes until it looks foamy and active. If nothing happens, your yeast is old — grab a fresh packet before moving on.
  2. 2In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Pour in the yeast mixture and the olive oil, then stir until a shaggy dough forms. Turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5 to 6 minutes until the dough is smooth and just slightly tacky. If it's sticking to everything, add flour a tablespoon at a time.
  3. 3Drizzle a little olive oil into the bowl, place the dough back in, and turn it to coat. Cover with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm spot for 45 minutes to 1 hour, until roughly doubled. While that's happening, go handle your onions.
  4. 4Heat the butter or olive oil in a large skillet over medium-low heat. Add the sliced onions and the salt for the onions. Cook low and slow, stirring every few minutes, for about 25 to 30 minutes until the onions are deeply golden, sweet, and jammy. Don't rush this — caramelized onions are the whole soul of this pizza. Add the black pepper and set aside.
  5. 5Preheat your oven to 475°F. Generously oil a 9x13 inch baking pan or a large cast iron skillet. Once the dough has risen, press and stretch it into the pan with your fingertips, working it all the way to the edges. If it keeps springing back, let it rest 5 minutes and try again.
  6. 6Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella evenly over the entire surface of the dough, going right to the edges. Pile the caramelized onions on top of the cheese — yes, cheese goes first in fugazzeta, that's the move. Scatter the Parmesan over everything, then finish with a pinch of dried oregano.
  7. 7Bake for 18 to 22 minutes until the edges are golden and puffed, the cheese is bubbling underneath the onions, and the bottom of the crust is crispy when you lift a corner with a spatula. Let it rest for 5 minutes before slicing. Serve with red pepper flakes on the side and try not to eat the whole thing yourself.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to real fugazzeta is not skimping on the onions — three onions sounds like a lot but they cook down to almost nothing and become pure sweet magic. If you're short on time, you can caramelize the onions a day ahead and keep them in the fridge. They actually get even better overnight.
