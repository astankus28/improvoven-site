Simple Banana Bread Recipe (Easy, One-Bowl, No Mixer)

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple banana bread recipe is so easy it almost feels like cheating — one bowl, no fancy equipment, and those sad spotted bananas on your counter are about to become something incredible. We keep it classic but sneak in a little warmth with cinnamon and vanilla, because here at Improv Oven we don't do boring. Perfect for breakfast, a snack, or honestly just standing at the counter eating a slice straight from the pan.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
55 mins
Total
65 mins
Serves
8 slices

Ingredients

  • 3 very ripe bananas (the blacker the better, trust)
  • 1/3 cup melted butter (salted or unsalted both work)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F and grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan with butter or cooking spray. Don't skip this step — nobody wants banana bread stuck to the pan.
  2. 2In a large mixing bowl, mash your ripe bananas with a fork until mostly smooth. A few small lumps are totally fine and give you great texture in every bite.
  3. 3Pour the melted butter into the mashed bananas and stir it all together until combined.
  4. 4Add the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract. Stir until everything is well incorporated and you have a smooth, fragrant mixture.
  5. 5Sprinkle in the baking soda, cinnamon, and salt. Give it a good stir so those dry ingredients are evenly distributed throughout the wet mixture.
  6. 6Add the flour and fold it in gently with a spatula — mix just until you no longer see dry flour streaks. Overmixing is the enemy of tender banana bread, so stop as soon as it comes together.
  7. 7Pour the batter into your prepared loaf pan and smooth out the top. If you want to get fancy, lay a few thin banana slices on top for presentation.
  8. 8Bake for 55 to 65 minutes, until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean or with just a few moist crumbs. If the top starts browning too fast around the 40-minute mark, loosely tent it with aluminum foil.
  9. 9Let the bread cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then turn it out onto a wire rack. Slice it warm — you deserve it.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon for the best banana bread? Those bananas need to be seriously overripe — we're talking almost entirely black on the outside. If yours aren't there yet, throw unpeeled bananas in a 300°F oven for about 20 minutes until the skins turn black and the insides get soft and sweet. Miami abuela-approved shortcut right there.
