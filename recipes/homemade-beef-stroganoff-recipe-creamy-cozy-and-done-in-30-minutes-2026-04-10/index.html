Homemade Beef Stroganoff Recipe (Creamy, Cozy, and Done in 30 Minutes)

Published April 10, 2026 · Improv Oven

This homemade beef stroganoff recipe is the kind of dinner that makes people think you spent way more time in the kitchen than you actually did — rich, creamy, beefy, and deeply satisfying. We're keeping it classic but smart, using ingredients you probably already have sitting around, because that's what we do here. Think of it as your refrigerator whispering 'please, use me' — and this time, you actually listened.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef sirloin or chuck steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 8 oz egg noodles (or whatever pasta you have — penne works too)
  • 8 oz cremini or white button mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1Slice your beef thin — like, really thin, about a quarter inch. The thinner it is, the faster it cooks and the more tender it stays. Season generously with salt and black pepper on both sides and let it sit while you get everything else ready.
  2. 2Cook your egg noodles according to the package directions in well-salted water, drain, and set aside. Don't overcook them — you want a little bite left because they'll hang out in the sauce at the end.
  3. 3Heat your olive oil in a large skillet over high heat until it's almost smoking. Add the beef in a single layer — don't crowd the pan or it'll steam instead of sear. Work in batches if needed, about 1 to 2 minutes per side. You want a good brown crust on that meat. Remove and set aside.
  4. 4Lower the heat to medium and add the butter to the same pan. Toss in your onions and cook for about 3 to 4 minutes until they start to soften. Add the mushrooms and let them cook down for another 4 to 5 minutes until they're golden and most of their liquid has evaporated. Add the garlic and cook for one more minute.
  5. 5Sprinkle the flour over the mushroom and onion mixture and stir it in well — this is what thickens your sauce. Cook the flour for about a minute so it doesn't taste raw, then pour in the beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, and Dijon mustard. Stir everything together and let it simmer for 3 to 4 minutes until the sauce starts to thicken up nicely.
  6. 6Turn the heat down to low — this is important — and stir in the sour cream. Low heat is the move here because if the sauce is too hot, the sour cream will break and you'll get a grainy mess instead of that silky, creamy goodness you came for. Stir until fully combined and smooth.
  7. 7Add the beef back into the pan along with the cooked egg noodles. Toss everything together gently and let it all heat through for about 2 minutes. Taste it, adjust the salt and pepper, and serve hot topped with a good handful of fresh parsley.
💡 Improv Tip

Cheap cuts like chuck steak work beautifully here — just pop it in the freezer for about 20 minutes before slicing so it firms up and you can get those thin, even cuts without fighting it. And if you don't have sour cream, full-fat Greek yogurt is a solid swap that nobody will clock.
