Simple Mexican Beans from Scratch (No Can Required)

Published April 26, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Mexican beans recipe from scratch is the kind of thing your abuela's neighbor knew by heart — humble ingredients, zero fuss, and flavor that'll make you wonder why you ever grabbed a can. We're talking dried pinto beans cooked low and slow with onion, garlic, and a few pantry spices until they're creamy, rich, and deeply savory. Serve them over rice, tucked into a warm tortilla, or straight from the pot with a piece of crusty bread — lunch is officially handled.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
1 hr 45 mins
Total
1 hr 55 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried pinto beans, rinsed and picked over
  • 6 cups water (plus more if needed)
  • 1/2 white onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or lard
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano (Mexican oregano if you have it)
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt (added at the end)
  • Fresh cilantro, for serving (optional but come on)
  • Lime wedges, for serving
  • Warm tortillas or crusty bread, for serving

Instructions

  1. 1No soaking required — we're doing this the easy way. Add your rinsed pinto beans to a large pot with the 6 cups of water, chopped onion, and smashed garlic. Bring everything to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a steady simmer. You want bubbles, not a violent boil.
  2. 2Let the beans cook uncovered for about 1 hour and 30 minutes, stirring occasionally and adding a splash of water if the level drops below the beans. They're done when you can smash one easily between two fingers — soft all the way through, no chalkiness.
  3. 3Once the beans are tender, stir in the olive oil, cumin, oregano, and chili powder. Now — and this is important — add your salt. Salting too early makes the skins tough, so we always wait until the beans are soft. Stir well and let everything simmer together for another 10 to 15 minutes so those spices really settle in.
  4. 4Taste and adjust. Need more salt? Add it. Want more heat? A pinch of cayenne never hurt anybody. If you like your beans a little thicker and creamier, use the back of a spoon or a potato masher to smash about a quarter of the beans right in the pot — this is the move that makes them restaurant-worthy.
  5. 5Ladle into bowls and top with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime. Serve with warm tortillas or bread for scooping. If you've got avocado, slice it on top. If you've got cheese, crumble it on. These beans are your canvas — go live your life.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret flex here is the epazote — if you ever spot it at a Latin market or a well-stocked grocery, throw a sprig in with the beans while they cook. It's a wild herb that Mexicans have been using in beans forever, and it adds this earthy, almost medicinal depth that you can't replicate with anything else. Can't find it? No stress, your beans will still be incredible. But once you try it, there's no going back.
