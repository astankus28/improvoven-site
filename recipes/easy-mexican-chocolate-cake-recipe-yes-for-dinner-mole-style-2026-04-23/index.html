Easy Mexican Chocolate Cake Recipe (Yes, For Dinner — Mole Style)

Published April 23, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Mexican chocolate cake recipe isn't dessert — it's a savory mole-inspired chocolate cake layered with spiced ground turkey and smoky chipotle, the kind of bold, unexpected dinner that makes your whole kitchen smell like a mercado in Oaxaca. Think of it as a showstopper savory torte: rich dark chocolate folded into a spiced meat filling, stacked between tender cornmeal-chocolate rounds, and finished with a glossy ancho chile glaze. It sounds wild but it works — and your fridge probably already has most of what you need.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
40 mins
Total
60 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup fine cornmeal
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 lb ground turkey or ground beef
  • 1 small white onion, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, minced
  • 1 tsp adobo seasoning
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 2 oz dark chocolate (70% cacao or higher), roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup chicken or beef broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro and crumbled cotija cheese for serving
  • 1 ancho chile pepper (dried), seeds removed — for the glaze
  • 1/2 cup warm water — for the glaze
  • 1 tsp honey — for the glaze
  • 1 tsp apple cider vinegar — for the glaze

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F. Lightly grease two 8-inch round cake pans and line the bottoms with parchment paper. This is your savory cake base — those pans are doing dinner duty tonight.
  2. 2In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, cornmeal, cocoa powder, baking powder, cinnamon, cayenne, and salt until fully combined. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs, milk, vegetable oil, and brown sugar. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and stir until just combined — a few lumps are totally fine, don't overwork it.
  3. 3Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared pans and spread it smooth. Bake for 18 to 22 minutes, until the tops are set and a toothpick comes out clean. Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack.
  4. 4While the cakes bake, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 3 to 4 minutes until soft and translucent. Add the garlic and cook one more minute.
  5. 5Add the ground turkey or beef to the skillet, breaking it up as it cooks. Season with adobo, cumin, salt, and black pepper. Cook until browned all the way through, about 7 to 8 minutes.
  6. 6Stir in the minced chipotle peppers, tomato paste, and broth. Let it simmer on medium-low for 5 minutes until the liquid reduces and the mixture looks glossy and thick. Remove from heat and stir in the chopped dark chocolate until fully melted into the meat — this is your mole filling. Taste and adjust salt.
  7. 7Make the ancho glaze: Soak the dried ancho chile in 1/2 cup of warm water for 10 minutes until softened. Blend the chile with its soaking water, honey, and apple cider vinegar until completely smooth. Strain through a fine mesh strainer and season with a pinch of salt. Set aside.
  8. 8To assemble: Place one cornmeal-chocolate cake round on a serving plate or board. Spoon the entire mole meat filling on top, spreading it evenly to the edges. Place the second cake round on top and press down gently.
  9. 9Drizzle the ancho glaze generously over the top of the assembled cake, letting it run down the sides. Finish with a handful of fresh cilantro leaves and a shower of crumbled cotija cheese.
  10. 10Slice into wedges like you would a layer cake and serve immediately. Each slice should hold together beautifully with that layered cornmeal-chocolate base, savory mole center, and glossy chile glaze. Put it on the table and watch people try to figure out what they're looking at — then take a bite and completely understand.
💡 Improv Tip

The dark chocolate in the filling is doing the same job it does in a real mole negro — it's not sweetness you're after, it's depth. Don't skip it and don't swap it for milk chocolate or chips with added sugar. Grab the cheapest 70% dark chocolate bar at the grocery store and break it up by hand. That two-dollar bar is going to make this taste like you spent all day at the stove.
