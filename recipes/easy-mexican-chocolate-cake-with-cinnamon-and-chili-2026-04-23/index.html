Easy Mexican Chocolate Cake with Cinnamon and Chili

Published April 23, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Mexican chocolate cake recipe is the one you make when you want to blow someone's mind without spending more than a few dollars or dirtying every pan in the kitchen. It's got that deep, warming combo of cinnamon and a little heat that makes Mexican chocolate so special — think Abuela's hot cocoa but in cake form. One bowl, simple pantry stuff, and it comes out so moist people will think you ordered it from somewhere.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
35 mins
Total
50 mins
Serves
8

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper (or ancho chili powder for milder heat)
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup brewed coffee, cooled (or water if you don't have coffee)
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar or apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup sour cream or plain yogurt
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips, divided
  • ½ tsp flaky sea salt for topping (optional but worth it)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F and grease a 9-inch round cake pan or an 8x8 square baking dish. If you have parchment paper, line the bottom — it makes your life so much easier when it's time to unmold.
  2. 2In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, cinnamon, cayenne, baking soda, and salt until everything is evenly combined. This is your dry team — make sure they're all hanging out together before the wet ingredients crash the party.
  3. 3In a separate bowl or large measuring cup, whisk together the cooled coffee, vegetable oil, vinegar, vanilla, eggs, and sour cream until smooth. The vinegar reacts with the baking soda to give you that tender, airy crumb — don't skip it.
  4. 4Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir gently with a spatula or wooden spoon until just combined. A few small lumps are totally fine. Overmixing is the enemy of a moist cake, so once you don't see dry flour, stop.
  5. 5Fold in ¾ cup of the chocolate chips, then pour the batter into your prepared pan and spread it out evenly. Scatter the remaining ¼ cup of chocolate chips over the top — they'll melt into little pockets of chocolate heaven.
  6. 6Bake for 32 to 38 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out with just a few moist crumbs attached. Not wet batter — moist crumbs. That's your sweet spot.
  7. 7Let the cake cool in the pan for at least 15 minutes before slicing. If you can wait 30, even better. Sprinkle with flaky sea salt right before serving if you have it — that contrast against the dark chocolate and spice is everything.
  8. 8Serve warm as-is, or top with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, a dollop of whipped cream, or a drizzle of cajeta if you really want to go full Mexican dessert mode. No judgment either way — it's your kitchen.
💡 Improv Tip

The cayenne might feel scary but don't leave it out — it doesn't make the cake spicy, it just wakes up all the chocolate flavor in a way that makes people go 'wait, what IS that?' That's the whole magic of Mexican chocolate. If you only have a Abuelita or Ibarra chocolate tablet in the pantry, melt one down and reduce the sugar by 2 tablespoons, and you've got an even more authentic version. This cake also freezes beautifully — slice it, wrap individual pieces in plastic wrap, and you've got a emergency dessert situation handled for weeks.
