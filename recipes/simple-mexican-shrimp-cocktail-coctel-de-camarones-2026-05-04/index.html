Simple Mexican Shrimp Cocktail (Coctel de Camarones)

Published May 4, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Mexican shrimp cocktail recipe is the kind of thing that makes people think you spent all day in the kitchen — but honestly? You're maybe 30 minutes away from something seriously special. It's cold, tangy, a little spicy, and packed with fresh flavor in every single bite. Think of it like ceviche's saucier cousin, and once you make it, you'll keep the ingredients stocked on rotation.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
5 mins
Total
25 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb medium shrimp, peeled and deveined (fresh or frozen, thawed)
  • 1 cup Clamato juice (or tomato juice in a pinch)
  • 1/3 cup ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons hot sauce (Valentina or Tabasco work great)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 2 limes)
  • 1/2 cup cucumber, peeled and diced small
  • 1/2 cup white onion, finely diced
  • 1 Roma tomato, seeded and diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 1 jalapeño or serrano pepper, finely minced (seeds removed if you want less heat)
  • 1 small avocado, diced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Tostadas or saltine crackers for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Bring a medium pot of salted water to a boil. Add your shrimp and cook just until they turn pink and curl up, about 2 to 3 minutes — don't walk away, overcooked shrimp will ruin the whole vibe. Drain immediately and transfer to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking. Once they're cool, drain and pat them dry.
  2. 2While the shrimp chills out, make your cocktail sauce base. In a large bowl, whisk together the Clamato juice, ketchup, hot sauce, and lime juice. Taste it — it should be tangy, a little sweet, and have some heat. Adjust any of those flavors to your liking right now before everything goes in.
  3. 3Add the cucumber, white onion, Roma tomato, cilantro, and jalapeño to the sauce and stir everything together. The veggies bring freshness and crunch that make this recipe sing, so don't skip any of them if you can help it.
  4. 4Add the cooled shrimp to the bowl and fold everything together gently. Season with salt and pepper, then taste one more time. More lime? More hot sauce? You're the chef — trust your gut.
  5. 5Cover the bowl and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least 10 minutes so all those flavors can get acquainted. Right before serving, fold in the diced avocado so it stays fresh and green.
  6. 6Serve in chilled glasses or small bowls with tostadas or saltine crackers on the side. A wedge of lime and a few sprigs of cilantro on top make it look like you really know what you're doing — because now you do.
💡 Improv Tip

If Clamato isn't in your grocery store, don't stress — just use regular tomato juice and add a tiny splash of Worcestershire sauce to get that savory depth. Also, if you're buying frozen shrimp (which is totally fine and usually cheaper), thaw them overnight in the fridge or run them under cold water for about 15 minutes. Never use the microwave — you'll end up cooking them unevenly and that's a sad shrimp situation nobody wants.
