Simple Mexican Beer Margarita Recipe (The Chelada Cousin You Need)

Published April 14, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Mexican beer margarita recipe — sometimes called a 'beergarita' — is the kind of thing you throw together when company shows up unannounced and you want to look like you planned it all along. We're talking tequila, lime, a splash of triple sec, and a cold Mexican lager to stretch it all out into something dangerously refreshing. It's a crowd drink, a porch drink, a 'why didn't I know about this sooner' drink.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
0 mins
Total
10 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 12 oz Mexican lager beer (Modelo, Corona, or Pacifico all work great)
  • 4 oz silver tequila (nothing fancy — a mid-shelf blanco is perfect)
  • 2 oz triple sec or any orange liqueur you have on hand
  • 4 oz freshly squeezed lime juice (about 4 limes)
  • 2 oz simple syrup or agave nectar (adjust to taste)
  • Coarse salt or Tajín for rimming the glasses
  • 1 lime, sliced into wedges for garnish
  • 2 cups ice

Instructions

  1. 1Start by prepping your glasses — run a lime wedge around the rim of each glass, then press the rim into a plate of coarse salt or Tajín. If you've never used Tajín for this, that's the move. Set the glasses aside.
  2. 2In a large pitcher, combine the tequila, triple sec, fresh lime juice, and simple syrup. Give it a good stir so everything is friendly with each other.
  3. 3Taste the base mix before you add the beer — this is your moment to adjust. Too tart? Add a little more agave. Too sweet? Squeeze in another half a lime. You're the chef here.
  4. 4Fill each prepared glass with ice, then pour the margarita base about halfway up the glass.
  5. 5Top each glass slowly with the cold Mexican lager, pouring over the back of a spoon if you want to keep it from foaming up too fast. The beer is doing the heavy lifting on volume and that light, crisp finish.
  6. 6Give it a gentle stir — just once or twice, you don't want to kill the carbonation — garnish with a lime wedge, and serve immediately. These do not wait around.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon here is keeping your beer ice cold right up until you pour it — warm beer goes flat fast and you'll lose that fizzy lift that makes beergaritas so fun. Pop your bottles in the freezer for 10 minutes before you start prepping everything else. Also, if you batch this for a party, make the margarita base ahead of time and store it in the fridge, then add the beer per glass right before serving so nobody's drinking a flat drink by round two.
