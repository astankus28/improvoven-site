Simple Michelada Recipe for Cinco de Mayo (and Every Other Day Too)

Published April 21, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple michelada recipe is exactly what your Cinco de Mayo spread is missing — cold, spicy, tangy, and dangerously refreshing. Think of it like a Bloody Mary's cooler Latin cousin who showed up to the party in flip flops. Once you make this once, you'll be michelada-ing every Mexican lager that comes near you.

Prep
5 mins
Cook
0 mins
Total
5 mins
Serves
1

Ingredients

  • 1 cold Mexican lager (Modelo, Tecate, or Pacifico all work great)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 1 lime)
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (Valentina, Cholula, or Tabasco — your call)
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • Pinch of black pepper
  • Tajín or coarse salt for the rim
  • 1 lime wedge for garnish
  • Ice cubes
  • Optional: 2 tablespoons Clamato or tomato juice for a fuller body

Instructions

  1. 1Grab a tall glass or a chilled mug — a big 16 oz glass is perfect. Run a lime wedge around the rim so it's nice and wet, then dip or roll it in Tajín or coarse salt. That rim is non-negotiable, okay?
  2. 2Fill the glass about halfway with ice. You want this cold cold — micheladas do not play when it comes to temperature.
  3. 3Add your lime juice, hot sauce, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, and black pepper directly into the glass over the ice. If you're going the Clamato route, add it now too. Give it a quick little stir with a spoon or just swirl the glass.
  4. 4Slowly pour in your cold lager, angling the glass a bit to keep the foam chill. Pour until the glass is full and give it one more gentle stir to bring everything together.
  5. 5Squeeze that lime wedge over the top, drop it in the glass, and serve immediately. If you're making a batch for the crew, just multiply everything and set up a little michelada station so everyone can pour their own beer — it stays fresher that way.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon most people skip is the soy sauce — it adds this deep savory backbone that makes you wonder why your michelada tastes better than the restaurant's. Don't skip it. Also, Valentina hot sauce is the move here if you can find it; it has that perfect vinegary kick without going nuclear, which means you can use more of it freely.
