Simple Lentil Vegetable Soup (Freezer-Friendly & Ready in 30 Minutes)

Published April 27, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple lentil vegetable soup is freezer-friendly, stupid easy to make, and one of those recipes that tastes like you spent way more time on it than you did. We're talking a big, hearty pot of something that warms you from the inside out — built entirely from pantry and fridge staples you probably already have. In Miami or anywhere else, this one earns a permanent spot in your rotation.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups dry green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and cut into small cubes
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, with juices
  • 6 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Juice of half a lime
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Once it shimmers, add the diced onion and cook for about 3-4 minutes until it softens and turns a little translucent. Don't rush this — that's your flavor base right there.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic, carrots, and celery to the pot. Stir everything together and cook another 2-3 minutes until the garlic is fragrant and the veggies start to soften. Your kitchen should be smelling amazing right about now.
  3. 3Sprinkle in the cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, turmeric, and black pepper. Stir to coat all the vegetables in the spices and let them toast for about 30 seconds. That quick bloom in the oil is what takes this from bland to bold.
  4. 4Add the rinsed lentils, diced potato, canned tomatoes with their juices, vegetable broth, and bay leaf. Stir everything together and bring the pot to a boil over high heat.
  5. 5Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium-low, partially cover the pot, and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes — stirring occasionally — until the lentils are completely tender and the potato is soft. If the soup thickens too much, just splash in a little extra broth or water.
  6. 6Remove the bay leaf. Squeeze in the lime juice and taste for salt — adjust as needed. For a creamier texture, use a spoon or ladle to mash about a quarter of the soup against the side of the pot and stir it back in. That's the move.
  7. 7Serve hot with a little fresh cilantro on top. Crusty bread, white rice, or a warm tortilla on the side? Yes, yes, and yes.
💡 Improv Tip

This soup is an absolute freezer champ — let it cool completely, portion it into zip-lock bags or airtight containers, and freeze for up to 3 months. When you're ready to eat, just thaw overnight in the fridge or reheat straight from frozen on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of broth. Future you is going to be so grateful present you made a double batch.
