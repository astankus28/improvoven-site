Easy Homemade Easter Bread (Sweet & Fluffy for the Morning Table)

Published April 4, 2026 · Improv Oven

This homemade Easter bread recipe is easy enough that you can have it warm on the table before the Easter egg hunt even starts — no bakery run required. It's a sweet, slightly enriched bread with colorful dyed eggs baked right in, the kind of thing that makes the whole house smell like a holiday. Think of it as the Latin American rosca meets your Sunday morning, cozy, festive, and made with things you probably already have.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
30 mins
Total
50 mins plus 1 hour rise time
Serves
8

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour, plus a little extra for dusting
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast (1 standard packet)
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup whole milk, warmed to about 110°F
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon anise extract or the zest of one orange (optional but so worth it)
  • 5 raw eggs in the shell, dyed in food coloring (for decorating)
  • 2 tablespoons milk mixed with 1 egg yolk for egg wash
  • 2 tablespoons rainbow sprinkles or colored sugar for topping

Instructions

  1. 1Wake up your yeast first. In a small bowl, stir the warm milk, a pinch of sugar, and the yeast together. Let it sit for 5 to 10 minutes until it looks foamy and smells like bread is coming. If nothing happens, your milk was too hot or your yeast is old — start fresh, it happens to everyone.
  2. 2In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, melted butter, 2 eggs, vanilla, and the anise or orange zest if you're using it. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. 3Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8 minutes until it's smooth, soft, and a little tacky but not sticky. If you have a stand mixer with a dough hook, 5 minutes on medium works great. The dough should feel like a soft earlobe — that's your sign it's ready.
  4. 4Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise somewhere warm for 1 hour until doubled in size. Oven off but with the light on works perfectly in most kitchens.
  5. 5While the dough rises, hard-boil your decorative eggs just until set but not fully cooked — about 7 minutes. Let them cool and then dye them with food coloring in small cups of water. They're going back in the oven so they'll finish cooking inside the bread.
  6. 6Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently and divide it into 3 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a long rope about 18 inches long. Braid the three ropes together on a parchment-lined baking sheet and form it into a round wreath shape, pinching the ends together.
  7. 7Press the dyed eggs evenly into the braid around the wreath, nestling each one in between the folds so it sits snug. Cover loosely and let rise again for 30 minutes while you preheat your oven to 350°F.
  8. 8Brush the dough all over with the egg wash — be gentle around the colored eggs. Scatter sprinkles or colored sugar over the top and slide it into the oven.
  9. 9Bake for 25 to 30 minutes until the bread is deep golden brown and sounds hollow when you tap the bottom. If the top is browning too fast, lay a loose sheet of foil over it after the first 20 minutes. Let it cool for at least 15 minutes before slicing — I know it's hard.
💡 Improv Tip

The dyed eggs go in raw (or just barely set) on purpose — they finish cooking inside the bread as it bakes, which is classic. But if you want to skip the dyeing step on a busy morning, just nestle in some already hard-boiled eggs after baking as decoration. Nobody's checking your method, and it still looks gorgeous on the Easter table.
