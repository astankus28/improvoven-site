Budget-Friendly Sweet Soup Recipes Got Nothing on This Creamy Arroz con Leche Pudding

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

Look, budget-friendly soup recipes are great and all, but when your sweet tooth hits, nothing stretches a dollar like a silky, cinnamon-kissed arroz con leche — Cuban grandma energy in every spoonful. This stovetop rice pudding uses pantry staples you already have and comes together in about 30 minutes, no fancy equipment needed. It's the kind of dessert that makes people think you spent hours in the kitchen, and we are absolutely not correcting them.

Prep
5 mins
Cook
30 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 3 cups whole milk (or evaporated milk for extra richness)
  • 1 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon, for topping
  • Pinch of salt
  • Zest of 1 lime (optional but highly recommended)

Instructions

  1. 1Add the rice, water, cinnamon stick, and a pinch of salt to a medium saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until the water is mostly absorbed, about 12 to 15 minutes.
  2. 2Remove the lid and pour in the whole milk. Stir everything together and bring it back up to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat. You want it bubbling softly, not aggressively — think spa day, not mosh pit.
  3. 3Cook uncovered, stirring frequently with a wooden spoon or heatproof spatula, for about 12 to 15 minutes until the mixture thickens and the rice is very soft and creamy. It will look a little loose — that's perfect, it firms up as it cools.
  4. 4Stir in the sweetened condensed milk, vanilla extract, and lime zest if using. Cook for another 3 to 5 minutes, stirring constantly, until the pudding is thick, glossy, and coats the back of a spoon.
  5. 5Remove the cinnamon stick. Spoon the arroz con leche into cups, ramekins, or bowls. Dust generously with ground cinnamon on top.
  6. 6Serve warm for a cozy vibe, or cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour for a chilled, firmer pudding. Both versions are incredible — it's a personal journey.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret move here is using evaporated milk in place of regular whole milk — it gives you that deep, slightly caramelized richness without any extra effort or cost. Most Latin grocery stores sell it in bulk packs for almost nothing, so grab a few cans and keep them in the pantry. Your future self will thank you at 10pm on a Tuesday.
