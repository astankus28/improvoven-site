Easy Jalapeño Poppers Recipe for Cinco de Mayo (Dinner-Style, Stuffed and Loaded)

Published April 20, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy jalapeño poppers recipe for Cinco de Mayo takes the classic party bite and turns it into a full-on dinner situation — we're talking big, stuffed peppers loaded with seasoned ground beef, cream cheese, and melty cheddar, then baked until golden and bubbling. It's the kind of meal that makes your kitchen smell incredible and your guests think you spent way more time than you did. Very Miami, very festive, very doable on a Tuesday.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
40 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 8 large jalapeños, halved lengthwise and seeded
  • 1/2 lb ground beef (or ground turkey)
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese, divided
  • 1/2 small onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 cup sour cream, for serving
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving
  • Hot sauce, to taste

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 400°F and line a baking sheet with foil or parchment. Slice your jalapeños in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds and membranes with a small spoon — leave a little membrane if you want heat, remove it all if you're cooking for people who say 'I don't do spicy' but always eat three anyway.
  2. 2Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 2-3 minutes until softened and starting to go translucent. Add the garlic and cook another 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. 3Add the ground beef to the skillet and break it up with a wooden spoon. Season with cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Cook until the beef is fully browned, about 6-7 minutes. Drain any excess fat and let it cool for 5 minutes — you don't want to melt the cream cheese on contact.
  4. 4In a mixing bowl, combine the softened cream cheese with half the shredded cheddar. Fold in the cooked seasoned beef mixture until everything is evenly combined. Taste it. Adjust salt if needed. Try not to eat it all standing at the counter.
  5. 5Arrange the jalapeño halves cut-side up on your prepared baking sheet. Spoon the beef and cream cheese filling generously into each pepper — don't be shy, pile it up. Sprinkle the remaining cheddar over the tops of all the stuffed peppers.
  6. 6Bake for 18-22 minutes until the jalapeños are tender, the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the tops are getting those beautiful golden spots. If you want more color, hit them under the broiler for 2-3 minutes at the end — watch them closely.
  7. 7Let them rest for 5 minutes because the filling is molten lava right out of the oven. Serve on a platter with sour cream for dipping, fresh cilantro scattered on top, lime wedges on the side, and hot sauce on the table for the ones who think they can handle more.
💡 Improv Tip

If your jalapeños won't sit flat and keep rolling around on the pan, just slice a tiny sliver off the rounded bottom of each one to give them a flat base — takes 30 seconds and saves you from filling spilling everywhere. Also, wear gloves when seeding the peppers or wash your hands like three times before you touch your face. Ask me how I learned that lesson.
