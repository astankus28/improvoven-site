Arancini Italian Rice Balls

Improv Oven Classic · Originally published 2017

Think of arancini as fried mozzarella sticks in ball form — crispy outside, gooey and cheesy inside. The Italian name means "little oranges," and once you make them you'll understand why people can't stop eating them. The key is using day-old rice from the fridge (it holds together much better), good parmesan, and Italian breadcrumbs. Serve with our homemade marinara and they disappear fast.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
15 mins
Total
35 mins
Makes
8

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked white rice, chilled
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella
  • 8 small cubes mozzarella (for stuffing)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup Italian breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Marinara sauce for serving
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. 1In a large bowl, combine chilled rice, parmesan, shredded mozzarella, and beaten eggs. Season with salt and pepper. Mix well until the mixture holds together when pressed.
  2. 2Take a small handful of the mixture and flatten it in your palm. Place a mozzarella cube in the center. Close your hand and squeeze firmly to form a tight ball around the cheese. Repeat to make 8 arancini.
  3. 3Roll each arancini in Italian breadcrumbs until completely coated on all sides.
  4. 4Heat 2–3 inches of vegetable oil in a deep pot to 350°F. Fry arancini in batches for about 5 minutes, turning occasionally, until deep golden brown all over.
  5. 5Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Serve warm with marinara sauce for dipping.
💡 Improv Tip

Day-old rice from the fridge is essential — freshly cooked rice is too wet and sticky to hold its shape. If you're making rice fresh, spread it on a sheet pan and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before using. Don't skip this step or your arancini will fall apart in the oil.
