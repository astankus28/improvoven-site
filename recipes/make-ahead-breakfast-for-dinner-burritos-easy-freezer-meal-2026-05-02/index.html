Make-Ahead Breakfast-for-Dinner Burritos (Easy Freezer Meal)

Published May 2, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you love make-ahead breakfast recipes, this easy freezer burrito situation is about to become your weeknight hero — wrap them up on Sunday and you've got dinner (or breakfast, no judgment) ready to go all week long. We're talking scrambled eggs, black beans, crispy potatoes, and melty cheese all tucked into a flour tortilla with a little sofrito magic to keep things interesting. Budget-friendly, totally customizable, and honestly way better than anything you'd grab from a drive-through.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 8 large flour tortillas (burrito-size)
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 medium russet potatoes, diced small
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar or Mexican blend cheese
  • 1/2 cup frozen sofrito (or 1/4 cup diced onion + 1/4 cup diced green bell pepper)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Hot sauce or salsa for serving (optional but highly encouraged)

Instructions

  1. 1Start with your potatoes — heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the diced potatoes in a single layer, season with cumin, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Let them cook undisturbed for about 5 minutes so they get a nice golden crust, then flip and cook another 5 minutes until crispy and cooked through. Transfer to a plate.
  2. 2In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add your sofrito (or diced onion and pepper) and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened and fragrant. Add the drained black beans, stir everything together, and cook for another 2 minutes. Season with a pinch of salt and push the mixture to the side of the pan.
  3. 3Crack your eggs into a bowl, season with salt and pepper, and whisk well. Pour the eggs into the empty side of the skillet and scramble them low and slow — pull them off the heat while they still look just slightly underdone because they'll finish cooking when you reheat the burritos. Nobody wants rubbery eggs.
  4. 4Lay out your tortillas on a clean surface. Down the center of each one, layer a scoop of potatoes, a spoonful of the bean mixture, a portion of scrambled eggs, and a good handful of cheese. Don't overfill or you'll fight with the fold — trust the process.
  5. 5Fold the sides of the tortilla in first, then roll from the bottom up tight. Wrap each burrito individually in aluminum foil. At this point you can eat one immediately (quality control is important), refrigerate the rest for up to 4 days, or freeze them for up to 2 months.
  6. 6To reheat from the fridge: unwrap the foil and microwave for 1.5 to 2 minutes, flipping halfway. From frozen: either thaw overnight in the fridge first, or microwave from frozen for 3-4 minutes, flipping halfway through. For extra crispiness, finish in a dry skillet or air fryer for 2-3 minutes after microwaving.
💡 Improv Tip

The move here is to let every component cool down for about 10 minutes before you build the burritos — hot fillings create steam inside the tortilla which makes things soggy by day three. Spread the love out on a sheet pan and let it breathe while you set up your wrapping station. Also, write the date on the foil with a marker so future-you doesn't have to play freezer roulette.
