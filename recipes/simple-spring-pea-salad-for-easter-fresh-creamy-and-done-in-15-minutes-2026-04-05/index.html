Simple Spring Pea Salad for Easter (Fresh, Creamy, and Done in 15 Minutes)

Published April 5, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple spring pea salad recipe is exactly what your Easter table needs — bright green, creamy, a little tangy, and ready before anyone even asks what's for lunch. It's the kind of dish that looks like you tried really hard but took almost no effort, which is honestly the whole vibe. Perfect for Easter brunch spreads, potlucks, or just eating out of a big bowl on the couch.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
0 mins
Total
15 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 2 cups frozen sweet peas, thawed (or fresh if you're lucky)
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/2 cup sharp cheddar cheese, cubed or shredded
  • 1/3 cup red onion, finely diced
  • 4 slices bacon, cooked and crumbled (optional but come on)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish (optional but pretty)

Instructions

  1. 1Thaw your frozen peas by running them under cold water in a colander for a minute or two — they don't need to be cooked, just woken up. Pat them dry with a paper towel so your dressing doesn't get watery.
  2. 2In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the sour cream, mayonnaise, lemon juice, sugar, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until smooth and creamy. Taste it — it should be tangy, a little sweet, and bright. Adjust the lemon or salt if it needs it.
  3. 3Add the peas, red onion, and cheddar cheese into the bowl and fold everything together gently so the peas stay whole and don't get mashed.
  4. 4Fold in the bacon crumbles if you're using them, and then add your fresh dill. Give it one more gentle stir.
  5. 5Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving — this is the move that makes all the flavors come together. If you're making it for Easter lunch, you can easily prep this the night before and it'll be even better.
  6. 6Right before serving, give it a taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Garnish with fresh mint leaves or a little extra dill on top for that spring garden look. Serve cold.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen peas are genuinely perfect for this — don't let anyone make you feel bad about it. They're sweet, consistent, and way more affordable than fresh shelled peas. The secret is drying them really well after thawing so your dressing stays thick and creamy instead of turning into a soup. Also, if you want to stretch this into a heartier Easter lunch dish, fold in some diced ham — it turns this into a full meal and feels very festive without any extra effort.
