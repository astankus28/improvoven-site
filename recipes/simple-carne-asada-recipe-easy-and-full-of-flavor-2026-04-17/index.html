Simple Carne Asada Recipe (Easy and Full of Flavor)

Published April 17, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple carne asada recipe is easy enough for a weeknight but honestly tastes like something you'd get at a good taqueria in Little Havana. We're talking citrus-marinated skirt steak, charred edges, juicy inside — the whole thing comes together fast with stuff you probably already have. Once you make this, you're gonna wonder why you ever ordered out.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
8 mins
Total
18 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs skirt steak or flank steak
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons fresh orange juice (about half an orange)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 1 lime)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped (optional but do it)
  • Warm flour or corn tortillas for serving
  • Sliced white onion and fresh salsa for topping

Instructions

  1. 1In a bowl or zip-lock bag, mix together the olive oil, orange juice, lime juice, minced garlic, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and black pepper. This is your marinade — smell it and feel good about your life choices.
  2. 2Add the skirt steak to the marinade and coat it well on all sides. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes at room temperature, or up to 8 hours in the fridge if you're planning ahead. Even 20 minutes makes a real difference here.
  3. 3When you're ready to cook, heat a cast iron skillet, grill pan, or outdoor grill over HIGH heat. You want it seriously hot — that's what gives you those beautiful charred edges.
  4. 4Pull the steak out of the marinade and shake off the excess. Lay it down on the hot surface and don't touch it for 3 to 4 minutes. Let it do its thing and build that crust.
  5. 5Flip once and cook another 3 to 4 minutes for medium doneness. Skirt steak is best when it's not overcooked — medium to medium-rare keeps it juicy and tender.
  6. 6Remove the steak from the heat and let it rest on a cutting board for 5 minutes. This step is non-negotiable — resting lets the juices redistribute so every bite is perfect.
  7. 7Slice the steak AGAINST the grain into thin strips. This is the move that makes it tender instead of chewy — always cut against those muscle fibers.
  8. 8Pile it into warm tortillas, top with diced white onion, fresh cilantro, and your salsa of choice. Squeeze a little extra lime over everything and go to town.
💡 Improv Tip

Skirt steak is the classic cut for carne asada and it's usually cheaper than flank — ask your butcher or check the Latin meat section at your grocery store where it's often labeled 'churrasco' or just cheaper in general. If you can only find flank steak, that works great too, just give it an extra minute per side. The real secret here is that screaming hot pan and not skipping the rest time — do those two things and you'll never mess up carne asada again.
