Easy Street Tacos Recipe (Authentic Carne Asada Style)

Published April 21, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy street tacos recipe brings authentic taquería flavor straight to your kitchen — no food truck required, just a hot pan and some hustle. We're keeping it real with a simple citrus-marinated beef, doubled-up corn tortillas, and the classic toppings that actually matter: onion, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime. This is the kind of meal that costs almost nothing and tastes like it came from that little stand you always see a line around.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb skirt steak or flap meat (also sold as carne asada cut)
  • 3 tablespoons orange juice (fresh or from the carton, both work)
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice (about 1 lime)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon oil (vegetable or canola)
  • 16 small corn tortillas (doubled up, so 2 per taco)
  • 1/2 white onion, finely diced
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges for serving
  • Salsa verde or hot sauce of your choice

Instructions

  1. 1In a bowl or zip-lock bag, combine the orange juice, lime juice, minced garlic, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the steak and let it marinate for at least 15 minutes at room temperature — or up to 4 hours in the fridge if you're planning ahead. Even 15 minutes makes a real difference here.
  2. 2Heat a cast iron skillet or heavy pan over high heat until it is seriously hot — you want it almost smoking. Add the oil and swirl to coat.
  3. 3Remove the steak from the marinade, shake off the excess, and lay it in the pan. Cook undisturbed for 3 to 4 minutes per side for medium, depending on thickness. You want a deep, dark sear on the outside. That crust is everything.
  4. 4Transfer the steak to a cutting board and let it rest for 5 full minutes — do not skip this, it keeps all the juice inside where it belongs. Then slice it against the grain into small, bite-sized pieces. If the pieces are a little uneven, that is totally fine and honestly more authentic.
  5. 5While the steak rests, warm your tortillas directly over a gas flame for about 30 seconds per side until they get a little char, or heat them in a dry pan. Double up two tortillas per taco so they hold together.
  6. 6Build your tacos: start with the chopped steak, then top with a generous pinch of diced white onion and fresh cilantro. Squeeze lime over everything and add your salsa verde or hot sauce. That is it. Serve immediately.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret street taco move nobody tells you: after you chop the rested steak, throw it back into the hot pan for just 60 seconds to pick up more of those caramelized bits from the bottom of the pan. It takes 30 seconds and it makes the whole taco taste like it came from someone who has been doing this for 30 years.
