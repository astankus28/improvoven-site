Easy Homemade Birria Tacos Recipe (Crispy, Cheesy, and So Worth It)

Published April 14, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy birria tacos recipe homemade style is the one that's going to make your whole block knock on your door. We're talking slow-braised beef in a smoky, chile-spiked consommé, stuffed into crispy golden tacos that you dip back into the broth like it's nobody's business. No fancy equipment, no hard-to-find ingredients — just real flavor that tastes like it took way more effort than it did.

Prep
25 mins
Cook
3 hrs
Total
3 hrs 25 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck roast, cut into large chunks
  • 3 dried guajillo chiles, stems and seeds removed
  • 2 dried ancho chiles, stems and seeds removed
  • 1 chipotle pepper in adobo sauce (from a can)
  • 1 tablespoon adobo sauce (from the same can)
  • 1 medium white onion, roughly chopped (divided)
  • 6 garlic cloves, peeled
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 12 small corn tortillas
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Oaxaca or mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup white onion, finely diced (for topping)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped (for topping)
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • Oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. 1Season your beef chunks generously on all sides with salt and black pepper. Heat a splash of oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat, then sear the beef in batches until browned on all sides, about 3-4 minutes per side. Don't rush this — that browning is building your flavor foundation. Set the beef aside.
  2. 2While the beef rests, toast your dried guajillo and ancho chiles in the same dry pot for about 30 seconds per side until fragrant (you'll smell it — trust yourself). Then submerge them in a bowl of boiling water and let them soak for 15 minutes until soft.
  3. 3Drain the soaked chiles and add them to a blender along with the diced tomatoes, chipotle pepper, adobo sauce, half of the chopped onion, garlic cloves, oregano, cumin, smoked paprika, cinnamon, and 1 cup of the beef broth. Blend until completely smooth.
  4. 4Pour the chile sauce through a fine mesh strainer back into your pot, pressing with a spoon to get every last drop of goodness through. Discard the solids left in the strainer.
  5. 5Add the seared beef back into the pot with the chile sauce. Pour in the remaining 1 cup of beef broth and tuck in the bay leaves. Bring everything to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for 2.5 to 3 hours until the beef is fall-apart tender. Check it occasionally and add a splash of water if needed.
  6. 6When the beef is done, pull it out with tongs and shred it using two forks — it should fall apart with basically no effort. Taste your consommé (the braising liquid) and adjust salt if needed. Remove the bay leaves. Keep both the shredded beef and consommé warm.
  7. 7Now for the fun part — the crispy tacos. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat with just a tiny bit of oil. Quickly dip a corn tortilla into the consommé so it's lightly coated on both sides, then lay it flat in the skillet.
  8. 8Add a small handful of shredded cheese to one half of the tortilla, then pile on some shredded birria beef on top of the cheese. Fold the tortilla over and press gently. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side until the outside is golden and crispy and the cheese is melted. Repeat with remaining tortillas.
  9. 9Serve your birria tacos immediately with small bowls of the warm consommé on the side for dipping. Top the tacos with finely diced white onion, fresh cilantro, and a big squeeze of lime. Dip. Eat. Repeat.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon here is that can of chipotles in adobo — it adds smokiness and depth that makes people think you've been cooking all week. Buy the whole can, use one pepper for this recipe, then freeze the rest in a zip-lock bag laid flat. You'll have them ready to go for the next time the craving hits, and trust me, it will hit again soon.
