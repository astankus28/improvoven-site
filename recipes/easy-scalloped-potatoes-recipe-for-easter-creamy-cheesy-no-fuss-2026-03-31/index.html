Easy Scalloped Potatoes Recipe for Easter (Creamy, Cheesy, No-Fuss)

Published March 31, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy scalloped potatoes recipe is the Easter side dish that steals the whole show — we're talking bubbling, golden, creamy layers that make people forget about whatever protein is on the table. You don't need fancy equipment or a culinary degree, just a handful of pantry staples and about an hour of your life. Trust me, your family is going to ask you to make this every single year.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
55 mins
Total
1 hr 15 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs russet potatoes (about 4 medium), peeled and thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk (or evaporated milk for extra richness)
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese, divided
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small yellow onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin (the secret touch)
  • Fresh parsley or chives for garnish, optional

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F and lightly butter a 9x13 inch baking dish or any similar-sized casserole dish you have. Don't stress about the exact size — if it holds the potatoes, it works.
  2. 2Peel your potatoes and slice them as thin and even as you can, about 1/8 inch thick. A mandoline is great here but a sharp knife and patience gets the job done just as well. Thin slices mean creamy, tender layers — thick ones stay crunchy in the middle and nobody wants that.
  3. 3In a medium saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 60 seconds until fragrant — you'll smell it, you'll know. Whisk in the flour and stir constantly for about 1 minute to cook out the raw flour taste.
  4. 4Slowly pour in the milk, whisking the whole time so no lumps form. Keep stirring over medium heat until the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon, about 4 to 5 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in half the cheddar, the salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and cumin. Taste it — it should be well-seasoned and make you want to drink it with a spoon.
  5. 5Layer half the sliced potatoes in the bottom of your buttered baking dish, slightly overlapping like roof shingles. Scatter half the sliced onion over the top, then pour half the cheese sauce evenly over everything. Repeat with the remaining potatoes, onion, and sauce.
  6. 6Sprinkle the remaining cheddar cheese over the top in an even layer. Cover the dish tightly with aluminum foil and bake for 40 minutes.
  7. 7Remove the foil and bake uncovered for another 15 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly and the potatoes are completely tender when you poke them with a knife. If you want extra browning, flip on the broiler for the last 2 to 3 minutes — just watch it closely so it doesn't burn.
  8. 8Let the dish rest for at least 10 minutes before serving. I know it's hard to wait, but this step lets everything set up so you get clean, beautiful scoops instead of a soupy mess. Garnish with fresh parsley or chives if you have them and serve hot.
💡 Improv Tip

Slice your potatoes and keep them in a bowl of cold water while you make the sauce — this stops them from turning brown and also rinses off excess starch so your layers don't stick together into one dense brick. Just pat them dry before layering. This one little move makes a big difference in the final texture.
