Easy Eggs Benedict Recipe for Mother's Day Dinner

Published May 5, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy eggs benedict recipe is the Mother's Day dinner move that makes you look like you really, really love her — and honestly, you do. We're talking silky poached eggs, a quick blender hollandaise that doesn't require a culinary degree, and toasted English muffins loaded with Canadian bacon. It's the kind of meal that feels fancy but won't have you stressed out in the kitchen all evening.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs (6 for poaching, 2 for hollandaise)
  • 4 English muffins, split and toasted
  • 8 slices Canadian bacon
  • 2 sticks (1 cup) unsalted butter
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar (for poaching water)
  • Fresh chives or parsley for garnish
  • Paprika for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1Start with your blender hollandaise so it's ready to go when everything else comes together. Melt your two sticks of butter in a small saucepan over low heat until fully melted and just starting to bubble — don't let it brown. While that's going, drop your 2 egg yolks, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, cayenne, and a pinch of salt into a blender. Blend on medium for about 20 seconds until the yolks look pale and a little frothy.
  2. 2With the blender running on low, very slowly drizzle in the hot melted butter through the lid opening — like a thin stream, not a pour. Take about 60 seconds to add it all. The sauce will thicken up beautifully as the hot butter emulsifies with the yolks. Taste it, adjust salt if needed, and set the blender jar in a warm spot (near the stove is fine) while you finish everything else. If it gets too thick, add a tiny splash of warm water and pulse once.
  3. 3Heat a large skillet over medium heat and warm your Canadian bacon slices for about 2 minutes per side until they get a little golden at the edges. No oil needed — they've got enough fat to do their thing. Set aside on a plate and cover loosely with foil to keep warm.
  4. 4Now for the poached eggs — this is the part people get nervous about, but we're going to make it easy. Fill a wide saucepan or deep skillet with about 3 inches of water. Add the tablespoon of white vinegar and bring it to a gentle simmer over medium heat. You want small bubbles, not a rolling boil. Crack each egg into its own small cup or ramekin first — this gives you control.
  5. 5Use a spoon to stir the simmering water in a slow circle to create a gentle whirlpool, then slide one egg from its cup into the center. The swirling water helps the whites wrap around the yolk. Cook for 3 to 3.5 minutes for a runny yolk. Remove with a slotted spoon and rest on a paper towel. Work in batches of 2 to 3 eggs so the water temperature stays steady. Keep finished eggs in a bowl of warm water while you cook the rest.
  6. 6Toast your English muffins and lay them cut-side up on plates — two halves per person. Layer a slice of Canadian bacon on each half, then gently place a poached egg on top. Spoon a generous amount of warm hollandaise over each egg. Finish with a pinch of paprika, fresh chives or parsley, and a little crack of black pepper. Serve immediately while everything is warm.
💡 Improv Tip

The biggest hollandaise panic people have is it breaking or going cold. Here's your insurance policy: keep the blender jar sitting in a bowl of warm (not hot) water while you poach the eggs — it stays pourable and silky the whole time. And if somehow it does break and looks grainy, add a teaspoon of warm water and blend it again for 15 seconds. Nine times out of ten, it comes right back together. Mami will never know there was a moment of drama.
