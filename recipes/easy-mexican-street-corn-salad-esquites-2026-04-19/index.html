Easy Mexican Street Corn Salad (Esquites)

Published April 19, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Mexican street corn salad recipe is everything you love about elotes — but in a bowl, no napkins required. Smoky charred corn, creamy cotija, a little heat, a little lime — it comes together in like 30 minutes and hits every single time. Make it for lunch and watch it disappear before you even sit down.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 4 cups corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned — all work)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or olive oil
  • 1/3 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/3 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup cotija cheese, crumbled (plus more for topping)
  • 1 jalapeño, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Tajín for garnish (optional but highly recommended)

Instructions

  1. 1Get your skillet screaming hot over medium-high heat — this is where the magic happens. Add the butter or olive oil, then throw in your corn. If you're using frozen, no need to thaw it first. Spread it out in an even layer and don't touch it for about 3 to 4 minutes. You want real char on this corn, not just warmth. Stir once, let it sit again, and keep going until you've got good golden-brown color on a lot of the kernels. This takes about 8 to 10 minutes total. Season with a pinch of salt and set it aside to cool slightly.
  2. 2While the corn is doing its thing, mix your dressing. In a large bowl, combine the mayonnaise, sour cream, minced garlic, lime juice, chili powder, smoked paprika, and cumin. Whisk it together until smooth. Taste it — it should be creamy, a little tangy, a little smoky. Adjust lime or seasoning to your liking right now before everything goes in.
  3. 3Add the slightly cooled charred corn to the dressing bowl along with the diced jalapeño, red onion, cilantro, and most of the cotija. Fold everything together gently so the corn gets coated in all that creamy goodness without you smashing it to pieces.
  4. 4Taste again and adjust — more lime, more salt, more chili powder, whatever it needs. Spoon it into a serving bowl or individual cups, then top with the remaining cotija, a fresh sprinkle of chili powder or Tajín, and extra cilantro if you want it to look like it belongs on a food blog. Serve immediately or chill it for 15 minutes first — both are great.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen corn is genuinely your friend here — it chars beautifully and costs almost nothing. The one move people skip is actually letting the corn sit without stirring. Resist the urge. That char is not just for looks, it's the whole flavor foundation of this dish. No char, no soul.
