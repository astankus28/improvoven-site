Quick Fig Jam Grilled Cheese That Tastes Totally Gourmet

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

This quick fig jam grilled cheese gourmet dinner is one of those magical fridge-raid moments where simple ingredients turn into something that feels straight-up restaurant-worthy. We're talking golden, buttery bread stuffed with creamy brie, sweet-savory fig jam, and sharp cheddar — paired with a simple arugula salad to make it a real meal. Mira, once you try this you're gonna wonder why you ever ordered delivery.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 8 slices thick sourdough or Cuban bread
  • 4 tablespoons fig jam (store-bought is totally fine)
  • 4 oz brie cheese, thinly sliced (rind on or off, your call)
  • 4 oz sharp cheddar or gruyere, shredded or sliced
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 4 cups arugula
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: 4 slices prosciutto or deli ham for extra heartiness

Instructions

  1. 1Pull your bread, cheeses, and fig jam out of the fridge about 10 minutes before you start — room temp cheese melts way more evenly and you deserve that perfect melt.
  2. 2In a small bowl, mix your softened butter with the Dijon mustard until combined. This little move adds a subtle savory depth to the crust that takes it from good to 'wait, what did you put in this?'
  3. 3Lay out all 8 slices of bread and spread the Dijon butter on one side of each slice — that's your outside, the side that hits the pan.
  4. 4Flip the slices over. On 4 of them, spread about a tablespoon of fig jam right on the bread. Layer on the brie, then the cheddar or gruyere. If you're adding prosciutto, tuck it in between the cheeses now.
  5. 5Top each with the remaining bread slices, butter-side facing out. Press down gently so everything gets cozy in there.
  6. 6Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium-low heat. You want low and slow here — medium-low is the move so the cheese fully melts before the outside burns. Place sandwiches in the pan.
  7. 7Cook 4 to 5 minutes per side, pressing down lightly with a spatula, until each side is deeply golden and the cheese is visibly melted at the edges. Work in batches if needed and keep finished ones warm in a 200°F oven.
  8. 8While the sandwiches cook, whisk together the olive oil, honey, apple cider vinegar, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Toss in the arugula right before serving — the peppery greens cut right through all that richness.
  9. 9Slice each sandwich diagonally (always diagonal, it just hits different), plate alongside a handful of the dressed arugula, and serve immediately while the cheese is still pull-apart gorgeous.
💡 Improv Tip

If your fig jam is on the chunky side, microwave it for 10 seconds and stir — it spreads way easier and you get that jam in every single bite instead of one big lump in the middle. No fig jam at home? Mango chutney or guava paste work beautifully here and honestly feel even more Miami.
