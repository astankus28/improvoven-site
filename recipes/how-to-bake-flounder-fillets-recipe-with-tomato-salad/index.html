Baked Flounder with Fresh Tomato Salad

Improv Oven Classic · Originally published June 2016

Flounder is one of those fish that's usually pan-fried, but it bakes beautifully too — and it's a lot easier to clean up. Season the fillets, pop them in the oven for 20–25 minutes, finish with a squeeze of lemon, and top with a quick fresh tomato salad. Light, healthy, and genuinely impressive for how little work it takes. The tomato salad is optional but it really does make the dish.

Prep
5 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
2

Ingredients

  • For the Flounder
  • 2 flounder fillets
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or melted butter
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the Tomato Salad
  • 2 roma tomatoes, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely diced
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley or basil, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat oven to 375°F. Coat a baking dish or sheet pan with olive oil or melted butter.
  2. 2Season flounder fillets with salt and pepper on both sides. Lay flat in the prepared baking dish.
  3. 3Bake uncovered for 20–25 minutes, until fillets are firm to the touch and the edges begin to brown and crisp slightly.
  4. 4While the fish bakes, combine tomatoes, red onion, parsley, olive oil, and red wine vinegar in a bowl. Toss and season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  5. 5Remove fish from oven and immediately squeeze fresh lemon juice over the fillets.
  6. 6Spoon the tomato salad on top of the flounder. Serve immediately with a side of rice.
💡 Improv Tip

Flounder fillets are thin, so don't walk away from the oven — they can go from perfect to overdone quickly. You can also try roasting the tomato salad ingredients in the oven for a few minutes first for added depth and sweetness. Any mild white fish works here: tilapia, cod, or sole all bake the same way.
