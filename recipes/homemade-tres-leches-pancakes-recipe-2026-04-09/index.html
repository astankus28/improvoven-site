Homemade Tres Leches Pancakes Recipe

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

This homemade tres leches pancakes recipe takes everything you love about the classic Cuban-bakery cake and turns it into a weekend breakfast situation that feels like a full-on celebration. We're soaking fluffy pancakes in that iconic three-milk mixture — evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and heavy cream — then topping it all off with whipped cream like it's somebody's birthday. Trust me, once you make these, regular pancakes are going to feel very lonely.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup whole milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream (for the three-milk soak)
  • 1 cup heavy cream (for the whipped topping)
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (for whipped cream)
  • Ground cinnamon for dusting

Instructions

  1. 1Make your three-milk soak first so it's ready to go: whisk together the evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and 1/4 cup heavy cream in a small bowl or measuring cup. Set it aside — this is the magic potion.
  2. 2In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt until combined.
  3. 3In a separate bowl, whisk the 3/4 cup whole milk, egg, melted butter, and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract together until smooth.
  4. 4Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir gently until just combined. A few lumps are totally fine — overmixing makes tough pancakes and nobody wants that.
  5. 5Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it with a little butter or cooking spray. Pour about 1/4 cup of batter per pancake and cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set, about 2 to 3 minutes. Flip and cook another 1 to 2 minutes until golden. Repeat with remaining batter.
  6. 6While the pancakes cook, make your whipped cream: beat 1 cup heavy cream with the powdered sugar and 1/2 teaspoon vanilla until soft, pillowy peaks form. Don't overwhip it — you want clouds, not butter.
  7. 7Arrange your pancakes in a shallow dish or on a deep plate in a stack or single layer. Slowly pour the three-milk soak over the pancakes, letting them drink it all in for at least 5 minutes. The pancakes will get beautifully soft and custardy — this is supposed to happen, go with it.
  8. 8Top generously with whipped cream, dust with ground cinnamon, and serve immediately. If you're feeling extra, a drizzle of dulce de leche on top will change your whole life.
💡 Improv Tip

Don't rush the soak — that's literally the whole point of tres leches. If you want to go deeper, poke the pancakes a few times with a fork before pouring the milk mixture so they absorb even more. And if you've got a can of evaporated milk and sweetened condensed milk in your pantry already, you're already halfway there. That's the Improv Oven way.
