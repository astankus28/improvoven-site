Budget Miami-Style Croquetas Recipe (Ham & Bechamel, Crispy Every Time)

Published April 8, 2026 · Improv Oven

This budget Miami-style croquetas recipe gives you those golden, crispy-outside, creamy-inside bites you find at every Cuban bakery and ventanita in the city — no fancy ingredients required. We're talking a classic ham croqueta made from a simple bechamel base, stretched with pantry staples and fried to perfection. Once you make these at home, you'll never pay $1.50 a pop again.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
40 mins (plus 1 hour chilling)
Serves
6 (makes about 18-20 croquetas)

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 small white onion, very finely minced
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup whole milk (or any milk you have)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Pinch of nutmeg (optional but trust me)
  • 1 cup finely chopped or ground deli ham (about 6 oz — the cheap stuff works great)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup plain breadcrumbs (or blitz stale bread in a blender)
  • Vegetable oil for frying (enough for about 2 inches in a deep pan)

Instructions

  1. 1Make your bechamel base: Melt the butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the minced onion and cook for 2-3 minutes until soft and translucent — you want it basically invisible in the final croqueta so chop it tiny. Add the flour and stir constantly for about 1 minute to cook out the raw flour taste. This little paste is called a roux and it's the soul of your croqueta.
  2. 2Build the filling: Slowly pour in the milk a little at a time, whisking constantly so you don't get lumps. Keep stirring over medium heat for 3-4 minutes until the mixture thickens up into a very thick, almost paste-like consistency — it should pull away from the sides of the pan. This is thicker than regular bechamel sauce on purpose. Season with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and nutmeg.
  3. 3Add the ham: Take the pan off the heat and fold in your finely chopped ham. Mix it all together evenly. Taste it right now and adjust salt if needed — this is your moment.
  4. 4Chill the mixture: Transfer the filling to a shallow dish or container, press a piece of plastic wrap directly onto the surface (this stops a skin from forming), and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Overnight is even better. The mixture needs to be completely cold and firm so you can shape it — don't skip this step or you'll be very frustrated.
  5. 5Shape your croquetas: Once the filling is cold and firm, use a spoon or your hands to scoop out portions about the size of your two fingers and roll them into classic oval log shapes, roughly 2-3 inches long. If the mixture is sticking to your hands, wet them slightly with cold water. Place the shaped croquetas on a plate or tray.
  6. 6Set up your breading station: Put the beaten eggs in one shallow bowl and the breadcrumbs in another. Roll each croqueta first in the egg, let the excess drip off, then roll in the breadcrumbs until fully coated. For extra crunch, do a double coat — egg again, then breadcrumbs again. Set them aside on a tray.
  7. 7Fry them golden: Heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil in a deep skillet or saucepan over medium-high heat. You want the oil around 350°F — if you don't have a thermometer, drop a tiny breadcrumb in; if it sizzles immediately and floats, you're ready. Fry the croquetas in batches of 4-5, turning them gently with tongs or a slotted spoon, for about 2-3 minutes until they're deep golden brown all over. Don't crowd the pan or the oil temperature drops and they get greasy.
  8. 8Drain and serve: Transfer the fried croquetas to a paper towel-lined plate and let them rest for 1-2 minutes. Serve hot with a little yellow mustard or hot sauce on the side, just like they do it at the Cuban spots on Calle Ocho. Try not to eat them all before they hit the table.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret most people miss is getting that filling THICK enough before you chill it. If your bechamel is still pourable when it comes off the stove, keep cooking and stirring — it needs to be almost like soft mashed potatoes. Too loose and your croquetas fall apart in the oil, and nobody wants that chaos. Also, day-old deli ham from the clearance section works just as well as fresh-cut — this is a budget recipe and it absolutely shows up.
