Easy Shrimp Pasta Recipe for Lenten Fridays

Published April 4, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy shrimp pasta recipe for Lenten Fridays is exactly what you need when it's 6pm and you promised everyone something special — no meat, no stress, no problem. Garlic, olive oil, a handful of pantry staples, and a bag of frozen shrimp is genuinely all it takes. This one is a weeknight lifesaver from now until Easter, trust me.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb spaghetti or linguine
  • 1 lb frozen medium shrimp, thawed, peeled and deveined
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 teaspoon sazon seasoning
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine or chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup reserved pasta water
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Parmesan cheese for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Bring a big pot of heavily salted water to a boil and cook your pasta according to package directions until al dente. Before you drain it, scoop out at least half a cup of that starchy pasta water — don't skip this, it's liquid gold for your sauce. Drain the pasta and set it aside.
  2. 2While the pasta cooks, pat your shrimp completely dry with paper towels. Season them with sazon, cumin, salt, and black pepper. Dry shrimp = better sear, so don't rush this part.
  3. 3Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once it's shimmering, add the shrimp in a single layer and cook about 1 to 2 minutes per side until they're pink and just curled into a C shape. Pull them out of the pan and set aside — they'll finish cooking later.
  4. 4Turn the heat down to medium and add the remaining 2 tablespoons of olive oil to the same pan. Add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes and cook, stirring constantly, for about 60 seconds until fragrant. Do not walk away here — burnt garlic will ruin everything.
  5. 5Pour in the white wine or broth and let it bubble and reduce for 2 minutes, scraping up any golden bits from the bottom of the pan. This is where the flavor lives.
  6. 6Add the drained pasta directly into the skillet and toss it in the garlic oil. Pour in a splash of the reserved pasta water and keep tossing until the sauce clings to every strand. Add more pasta water a little at a time if it looks dry.
  7. 7Nestle the shrimp back in, squeeze the lemon juice over everything, and add the lemon zest. Toss together for about 1 more minute so the shrimp heat through and everything is coated and glossy.
  8. 8Take it off the heat, scatter the fresh parsley over the top, taste for salt, and adjust. Plate it up, hit it with Parmesan if you're not keeping it strictly meatless, and get it on the table while it's hot.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen shrimp is your best friend here — it's usually cheaper and fresher than the so-called 'fresh' shrimp sitting on ice at the seafood counter. Just thaw it overnight in the fridge or do a quick cold water bath for 15 minutes, then dry it really well before it hits the pan. The dry shrimp is what gets you that beautiful little sear instead of sad, steamed shrimp.
