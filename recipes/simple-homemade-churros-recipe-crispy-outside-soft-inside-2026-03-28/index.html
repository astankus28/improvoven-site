Simple Homemade Churros Recipe (Crispy Outside, Soft Inside)

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple churros recipe homemade style is the real deal — no fancy equipment, no pastry school, just pantry basics and a hot pan between you and something magical. Down here in Miami, churros are basically a love language, and once you make them yourself you'll never go back to the mall version. Toss them in cinnamon sugar and dunk them in chocolate and you're basically living your best life.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar (for dough)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or butter
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (for coating)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions

  1. 1Make your cinnamon sugar coating first so it is ready to go the moment the churros come out of the oil. Mix 1/2 cup sugar and 1 teaspoon cinnamon together in a shallow bowl or plate and set it aside.
  2. 2In a medium saucepan over medium heat, combine the water, 2 tablespoons sugar, salt, and vegetable oil or butter. Stir everything together and bring it to a light boil.
  3. 3Once it is boiling, remove the pan from the heat and immediately dump in all the flour at once. Stir vigorously with a wooden spoon until the dough comes together into a smooth ball and pulls away from the sides of the pan. This happens fast so stay with it.
  4. 4Let the dough cool for about 5 minutes — you do not want to scramble your eggs. Then add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each one until fully incorporated. Add the vanilla with the second egg. The dough will look like it is breaking apart at first but keep stirring and it will come back together into a thick, glossy paste.
  5. 5Pour your frying oil into a deep skillet or small saucepan to about 2 inches deep. Heat over medium-high heat until it reaches around 375°F. If you do not have a thermometer, drop a tiny bit of dough in — it should sizzle immediately and float to the top.
  6. 6Transfer your dough into a piping bag fitted with a large star tip. If you do not have one, use a zip-lock bag with one corner snipped off — the ridges will not be as deep but they will still be delicious and crispy.
  7. 7Carefully pipe 4 to 5 inch strips of dough directly into the hot oil, using scissors or a knife to cut the dough as you go. Fry in small batches so you do not crowd the pan. Cook for about 2 to 3 minutes per side, turning once, until they are deep golden brown.
  8. 8Use a slotted spoon or tongs to lift the churros out of the oil and immediately drop them into the cinnamon sugar bowl. Roll them around until they are fully coated. Eat them right away while they are hot — that is when they are absolutely perfect.
  9. 9Optional but highly recommended: melt a handful of chocolate chips with a splash of milk or cream to make a quick dipping sauce. A little dulce de leche on the side also works beautifully if you have it.
💡 Improv Tip

The number one churro mistake is frying them at low heat — if the oil is not hot enough they will absorb too much oil and come out greasy and sad. Get that oil up to temperature before you pipe anything in, and do not rush it. A hot pan is the whole secret here.
