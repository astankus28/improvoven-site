Easy Egg Muffin Recipe with Whatever's in Your Fridge

Published March 27, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy egg muffin recipe is the move when you need a real lunch that comes together fast and doesn't require a grocery run. Think of them like little personal frittatas baked right in a muffin tin — crispy edges, fluffy centers, and totally customizable with whatever cheese, veggies, or leftover protein you've got hanging around. Cure that refrigerator blindness one muffin at a time, mija.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk (any kind works)
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, pepper jack, or whatever you have)
  • 1/2 cup diced bell pepper (any color)
  • 1/4 cup diced onion
  • 1/4 cup diced ham, cooked bacon, or leftover chicken (optional but good)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Cooking spray or a little butter to grease the pan
  • Hot sauce for serving (Cholula lives on our counter, just saying)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F and generously grease a 12-cup muffin tin with cooking spray or butter — get into the edges or these will stick and break your heart.
  2. 2Crack all 6 eggs into a medium bowl, add the milk, garlic powder, cumin, salt, and pepper, then whisk everything together until the yolks and whites are fully combined and the mixture looks a little frothy.
  3. 3Divide the diced bell pepper, onion, and any protein you're using evenly between the muffin cups — fill each one about halfway with the mix-ins so there's room for the egg.
  4. 4Pour the egg mixture slowly over the fillings in each cup, filling them to about three-quarters full — they will puff up in the oven so don't overfill or you'll have a situation.
  5. 5Sprinkle the shredded cheese evenly over the top of each muffin cup so every single one gets that melty golden layer.
  6. 6Bake for 18 to 22 minutes until the eggs are fully set, the tops are lightly golden, and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean — no jiggly centers, we're not making flan.
  7. 7Let them cool in the tin for 5 minutes before running a butter knife around the edges to pop them out cleanly. Serve warm with hot sauce, sliced avocado, or tuck two into a soft roll for a proper lunch sandwich.
💡 Improv Tip

If your muffins keep sticking even after greasing, switch to silicone muffin liners — they are genuinely life-changing and you'll never fight with a tin again. Also, make a double batch on Sunday and stash them in the fridge all week. They reheat in the microwave in 45 seconds and lunch is suddenly a solved problem.
