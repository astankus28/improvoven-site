Easy Homemade Potato Salad Recipe (Creamy, Classic, Never Boring)

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy homemade potato salad recipe is the one you'll make every single time someone says 'bring something' — because it's simple, it's creamy, and it hits every time. We're keeping it classic but adding just enough personality to make people ask you for the recipe. Think backyard cookout meets Miami kitchen, with a little tang and a little love.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs russet or Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 3 large eggs, hard boiled
  • 3/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons yellow mustard
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup dill pickles, finely chopped (or sweet pickles if that's your thing)
  • 2 stalks celery, finely diced
  • 1/2 small red onion, finely diced
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika, plus more for garnish
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley or chives, chopped (optional but pretty)

Instructions

  1. 1Cut your potatoes into even 1-inch cubes — this is the key to everything cooking at the same time. Toss them in a large pot, cover with cold salted water, and bring to a boil. Cook for 12 to 15 minutes until they're fork-tender but still holding their shape. You want soft, not falling apart. Drain them and spread on a sheet pan or cutting board to cool down a bit.
  2. 2While the potatoes cook, hard boil your eggs. Place them in a small saucepan, cover with cold water, bring to a boil, then turn off the heat and let them sit covered for 12 minutes. Transfer to an ice bath or run under cold water. Once cool, peel and roughly chop them.
  3. 3In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, yellow mustard, apple cider vinegar, garlic powder, salt, and a generous amount of black pepper. Taste it — this is your dressing, so make sure you like it. Need more tang? Add a splash more vinegar. Need more richness? A little more mayo.
  4. 4Add the warm (not hot) potatoes to the bowl with the dressing. Toss gently so the potatoes absorb all that flavor while they're still a little warm — this is the secret move. Then fold in the chopped eggs, celery, red onion, and pickles.
  5. 5Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, but honestly 2 to 3 hours is where the magic happens. The potatoes keep drinking up that dressing as it chills. Before serving, give it a taste, adjust salt and pepper, and finish with a dusting of paprika and fresh herbs on top.
💡 Improv Tip

Cold potatoes don't absorb dressing — warm ones do. That's the whole game. Mix your potatoes in while they're still slightly warm and you'll have a potato salad with flavor all the way through, not just on the outside. Also, if your salad looks a little dry after chilling, just stir in a spoonful of mayo and you're back in business.
