Simple Bacalao Recipe for Good Friday the Latin Way

Published April 3, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple bacalao recipe is what Good Friday looks like in Latin households from Miami to San Juan to Santo Domingo — a big pot of salt cod simmered down with tomatoes, peppers, onions, and olives until everything gets saucy and deeply savory. It's the kind of dish abuela could stretch to feed the whole family without breaking a sweat, and honestly? It tastes like it cooked all day even when it didn't. Serve it over white rice or with tostones on the side and you've got a real Friday night dinner.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb dried salt cod (bacalao), soaked overnight or quick-soaked
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, sliced thin
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced thin
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup pimento-stuffed green olives, halved
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Cooked white rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Soak your bacalao first — this is non-negotiable. If you planned ahead, let it soak in cold water in the fridge for 24 hours, changing the water two or three times. If it's the day of and you forgot, cover it with water in a pot, bring it to a boil, drain it, and repeat that two more times. Taste a small piece — it should be pleasantly salty but not knock-you-out salty. Once it's good, flake it into bite-sized pieces with your hands and set it aside.
  2. 2Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or wide pot over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and green pepper and cook for about 5 to 6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they soften up and get a little golden around the edges.
  3. 3Add the garlic and stir it around for about 30 seconds until it smells amazing, then pour in the canned tomatoes with all their juices. Add the oregano, cumin, and black pepper. Stir everything together and let it simmer for about 5 minutes so the sauce starts to come together.
  4. 4Add the flaked bacalao and the green olives into the pan. Stir gently to coat everything in that tomato sauce, then reduce the heat to medium-low. Let it all simmer together for 10 to 12 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce will thicken and the fish will absorb all those flavors — this is where the magic happens.
  5. 5Taste it before you add any salt. Bacalao is already salty so you may not need any at all. Finish with a big handful of fresh cilantro, give it one last stir, and serve it hot over white rice.
💡 Improv Tip

The biggest mistake people make with bacalao is under-soaking it — you want to pull the excess salt out, not all the flavor. If you're doing the quick-boil method, taste after the second boil. Still too salty? Do one more round. A little salt is your friend here, zero salt is sad fish.
