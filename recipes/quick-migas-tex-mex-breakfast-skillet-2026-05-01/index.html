Quick Migas Tex-Mex Breakfast Skillet

Published May 1, 2026 · Improv Oven

This quick migas Tex-Mex breakfast skillet is the kind of meal that makes you feel like you actually have your life together — even on a Tuesday morning with nothing but eggs, tortilla chips, and some sad leftover peppers in the fridge. Migas are basically the Tex-Mex answer to 'what do I do with stale tortillas,' and the answer is always: something incredible. One pan, twenty minutes, and you've got a smoky, cheesy, crispy-eggy situation that works for breakfast, lunch, or a late-night snack — no judgment here.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 cups tortilla chips, roughly crushed (stale ones work great)
  • 1/2 white onion, diced
  • 1 jalapeño, diced (seeded if you want to keep the peace)
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup canned diced tomatoes, drained (or 1 fresh Roma tomato, diced)
  • 1/2 cup shredded Monterey Jack or cheddar cheese
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or butter
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped (optional but come on)
  • Salsa, hot sauce, sour cream, or sliced avocado for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Crack all 6 eggs into a bowl, season with a pinch of salt and pepper, and beat them lightly with a fork. You're not making a smoothie — just break the yolks and mix until combined. Set aside.
  2. 2Heat your largest skillet over medium heat and add the oil or butter. Once it's shimmering, toss in the diced onion, jalapeño, and bell pepper. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything softens and the onion turns a little translucent.
  3. 3Add the drained diced tomatoes to the skillet and stir everything together. Let it cook for another 2 minutes so the tomatoes lose some of their moisture and the whole thing starts smelling like something good is happening.
  4. 4Season the veggie mixture with the cumin, chili powder, and garlic powder. Stir to coat everything evenly and let the spices bloom for about 30 seconds — that's when they go from dusty to delicious.
  5. 5Scatter the crushed tortilla chips over the vegetable mixture and stir gently to combine. Let the chips sit in contact with the pan for about 1 minute so they pick up some of that flavor and start to soften just slightly at the edges while staying a little crunchy in the middle.
  6. 6Pour the beaten eggs evenly over everything in the skillet. Let them sit undisturbed for about 30 seconds until you see the edges starting to set, then use a spatula to gently fold and scramble everything together — you want big, soft curds, not tiny dry pieces. Stop cooking when the eggs are just barely set; they'll keep cooking from residual heat.
  7. 7Turn off the heat and immediately scatter the shredded cheese over the top. Let it melt for about a minute from the residual heat — no need to torch it or put it under a broiler.
  8. 8Sprinkle with fresh cilantro if you're using it and serve straight from the skillet with your choice of salsa, hot sauce, sour cream, or a few slices of avocado on the side. Warm tortillas are never a bad idea either.
💡 Improv Tip

The real secret to great migas is texture — you want the chips to soften just enough to absorb the egg and spices but still have some crunch left in them. If your chips are totally fresh and thick, crush them a little smaller so they cook faster. If they're already stale and thin, add them right after the eggs hit the pan so they don't turn to mush. Either way, don't walk away from the stove once the eggs go in — this skillet moves fast and overcooked migas are a real heartbreak.
