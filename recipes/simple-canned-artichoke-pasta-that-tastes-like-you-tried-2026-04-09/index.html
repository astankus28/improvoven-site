Simple Canned Artichoke Pasta That Tastes Like You Tried

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple canned artichoke pasta recipe is proof that a can sitting in the back of your pantry can become the star of a legitimately impressive weeknight dinner. We're talking garlicky, lemony, a little briny — the kind of thing that tastes like you spent way more time than you did. Cure that refrigerator blindness and let those artichoke hearts finally shine.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 12 oz spaghetti or linguine
  • 2 cans (14 oz each) quartered artichoke hearts, drained
  • 5 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine or chicken broth
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for serving
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, roughly chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup reserved pasta water

Instructions

  1. 1Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta according to package directions until al dente. Before draining, scoop out about 1/2 cup of that starchy pasta water — don't skip this, it's liquid gold for your sauce.
  2. 2While the pasta cooks, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes and cook for about 2 minutes, stirring often, until the garlic is golden and fragrant. Watch it closely — garlic goes from golden to burnt fast and burnt garlic will wreck your whole vibe.
  3. 3Add the drained artichoke hearts to the skillet and spread them out in a single layer. Let them cook undisturbed for 3 to 4 minutes so they get a little golden and caramelized on one side. Then give them a stir and cook another 2 minutes. This step is what takes this from 'canned vegetable' to 'wait, what is this?'
  4. 4Pour in the white wine or broth and let it simmer for 2 minutes, scraping up any golden bits from the bottom of the pan. Add the lemon juice and lemon zest and stir to combine.
  5. 5Add the drained pasta directly to the skillet. Toss everything together, adding the reserved pasta water a little at a time until you get a loose, silky sauce that coats every strand. You may not need all of it.
  6. 6Remove from heat and stir in the Parmesan cheese and fresh parsley. Taste it and adjust salt, pepper, and lemon to your liking. Plate it up, hit it with extra Parmesan, and serve immediately.
💡 Improv Tip

Pat your artichoke hearts dry with a paper towel before they go in the pan — canned vegetables carry a lot of moisture and if you skip this they'll steam instead of sear. That golden crust is where all the flavor lives, so give them room and don't crowd the pan.
