Budget Cuban Sandwich Wrap Recipe (All the Flavor, Half the Fuss)

Published April 12, 2026 · Improv Oven

This budget Cuban sandwich wrap recipe takes everything you love about the classic Cubano — the ham, the pickles, the mustard, that melty Swiss — and rolls it all up in a flour tortilla so you can press it in a regular skillet without a special sandwich press. It's one of those lunches that tastes like it came from a ventanita in Little Havana but costs you maybe two bucks a wrap. Once you make it once, it's going into your weekly rotation for sure.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 4 large flour tortillas (burrito size)
  • 8 oz deli ham, thinly sliced
  • 6 oz deli roast pork or pulled pork (leftover works great)
  • 4 slices Swiss cheese
  • 1/4 cup yellow mustard
  • 1/2 cup dill pickle slices (the more the better)
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. 1Lay a flour tortilla flat on your counter and spread about a tablespoon of yellow mustard across the whole surface — don't be shy with it, the mustard is what makes a Cuban a Cuban.
  2. 2Layer on a couple slices of ham, then a good handful of your roast pork right on top. If you're using leftover pulled pork from the fridge, this is its moment to shine.
  3. 3Add one slice of Swiss cheese, then lay down a generous row of dill pickle slices across the center of the wrap. Season lightly with garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. 4Fold in the sides of the tortilla and roll it up tightly like a burrito, tucking everything in as you go so nothing escapes when you press it.
  5. 5Mix the softened butter with a pinch of garlic powder and spread a thin layer on the outside of each wrap — this is your secret weapon for getting that golden, crispy exterior.
  6. 6Heat a large skillet or cast iron pan over medium heat. Place the wrap seam-side down and press it flat with a heavy pan, a foil-wrapped brick, or just press down firmly with a spatula.
  7. 7Cook for 3 to 4 minutes per side, pressing occasionally, until both sides are golden brown and crispy and the cheese is fully melted inside.
  8. 8Let it rest for one minute before slicing diagonally, then serve immediately while it's still hot and the cheese is still pulling.
💡 Improv Tip

The number one thing that makes or breaks this wrap is pressing it down hard while it cooks — that flat pressure is what gives you the crispy exterior that mimics a real Cubano press. No fancy equipment needed, just grab your heaviest skillet and set it right on top. Also, if all you have is leftover deli turkey instead of roast pork, go ahead and use it. Not traditional, but still delicious, and that is the whole Improv Oven way.
