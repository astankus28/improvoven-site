What to Cook with Black Beans: Easy Cuban-Style Black Bean Rice Bowls

Published April 7, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're staring into your fridge wondering what to cook with black beans, this is your answer — a hearty, flavor-packed rice bowl inspired by the Cuban classics we grew up eating in Miami. It comes together in about 30 minutes with stuff you probably already have, and it tastes like you actually tried.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar or fresh lime juice
  • Optional toppings: sliced avocado, a fried egg, pickled red onions, fresh cilantro, hot sauce

Instructions

  1. 1Start your rice first so everything finishes around the same time. Combine the rice, water or broth, and a pinch of salt in a medium saucepan. Bring it to a boil, then drop the heat to low, cover, and let it cook undisturbed for 18 minutes. Take it off the heat and let it steam with the lid on for 5 more minutes — don't peek.
  2. 2While the rice is doing its thing, heat the olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat. Add the diced onion and green pepper and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they're soft and the onion is starting to turn translucent.
  3. 3Add the minced garlic to the pan and stir it around for about 60 seconds until it's fragrant. Don't walk away here — garlic goes from golden to burnt fast, and burnt garlic will wreck the whole vibe.
  4. 4Sprinkle in the cumin, oregano, and smoked paprika. Stir everything together and let those spices toast in the oil for about 30 seconds. This step is small but it makes a real difference in depth of flavor.
  5. 5Add both cans of rinsed black beans to the pan. Stir to coat them in all that sofrito-style goodness. Pour in about a quarter cup of water if things look dry, then let the beans simmer on medium-low for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they're heated through and the sauce has thickened slightly.
  6. 6Stir in the apple cider vinegar or lime juice, then taste and adjust salt and pepper. That little hit of acid at the end is what wakes the whole dish up — don't skip it.
  7. 7Fluff your rice with a fork and build your bowls: a scoop of rice, a generous ladle of beans over the top, and then pile on whatever toppings you've got. A fried egg on top turns this into something truly special if you have an extra two minutes.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret move here is the sofrito base — onion, pepper, garlic, and spices cooked in oil before the beans go in. That's the foundation of so much Cuban and Caribbean cooking, and it's what separates a bowl of beans from a bowl of seasoned beans. Once you get comfortable with that base, you can riff on it endlessly: add a can of diced tomatoes, throw in some leftover chicken, swap the rice for sweet plantains. This recipe is a starting point, not a rulebook.
