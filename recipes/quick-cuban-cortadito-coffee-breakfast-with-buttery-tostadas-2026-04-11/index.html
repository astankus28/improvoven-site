Quick Cuban Cortadito Coffee Breakfast with Buttery Tostadas

Published April 11, 2026 · Improv Oven

A quick Cuban cortadito coffee breakfast is the kind of morning ritual that'll make you never want to go to a drive-through again — just a few pantry staples and you're living like an abuela in Little Havana. We're talking that thick, sweet espresso shot cut with steamed milk, paired with golden buttery tostadas that are basically the perfect vehicle for dunking. This whole spread comes together in under 15 minutes and costs less than one fancy coffee shop latte.

Prep
5 mins
Cook
10 mins
Total
15 mins
Serves
2

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons finely ground dark roast espresso coffee (Café Bustelo or Pilon work great)
  • 1 cup water (for the moka pot or espresso maker)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (divided — trust the process here)
  • 1/2 cup whole milk (or evaporated milk for extra richness)
  • 4 slices Cuban bread or sandwich bread
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt (for the butter, optional but good)
  • 2 large eggs (optional — fry them up to make it a real breakfast plate)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter (for the eggs, if using)

Instructions

  1. 1Load your moka pot or stovetop espresso maker with the finely ground dark roast coffee and water according to its capacity. Place it over medium heat. If you don't have a moka pot, a strong drip coffee brewed with double the usual grounds gets you close enough — no judgment here.
  2. 2Here's the most important part of the cortadito — the espumita. As soon as the first few drops of espresso come out of the moka pot, pour them directly into a small cup or bowl with 2 tablespoons of sugar. Whip that together vigorously with a spoon for about 1-2 minutes until it turns into a thick, pale, caramel-colored foam. This is what separates a cortadito from regular coffee — don't skip it.
  3. 3Let the rest of the espresso finish brewing. Meanwhile, heat your milk in a small saucepan over medium-low heat until it's steaming but not boiling — about 2-3 minutes. You want it hot enough that it slightly froths when you stir it. If you have a milk frother, even better.
  4. 4Pour the brewed espresso over the espumita paste and stir it in — it'll bloom into this gorgeous dark, foamy coffee. Then pour in your steamed milk to cut it, roughly equal parts espresso to milk. Give it a gentle stir and taste — if you need more sweetness, add a touch more sugar now.
  5. 5For the tostadas, heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat. Butter your bread slices generously on both sides. Press them onto the hot skillet and cook 2-3 minutes per side until deeply golden and crispy. Cuban bread is ideal because it toasts up with this irresistible crunch, but any sturdy sandwich bread works beautifully.
  6. 6If you're doing eggs, wipe out the same skillet, add your olive oil or butter over medium heat, and fry your eggs sunny-side up or to your liking — about 3-4 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Slide them alongside the tostadas on the plate.
  7. 7Serve everything together — cortadito in a small glass or mug, tostadas on the side ready for dunking, and eggs if you made them. This is a proper Miami morning right here.
💡 Improv Tip

The espumita is the soul of the cortadito — if your first few espresso drops aren't quite enough to dissolve the sugar, add just one extra drop of hot water and keep whipping. And if you only have canned evaporated milk in the pantry instead of fresh milk, use it — that's actually the old-school Miami way and it gives you this rich, slightly sweet flavor that'll blow your mind.
