Quick Vegetable Stir Fry Recipe With Garlic Soy Sauce

Published April 8, 2026 · Improv Oven

This quick vegetable stir fry recipe is exactly what your fridge has been waiting for — a high-heat, 15-minute situation that turns whatever sad vegetables are hanging around into something genuinely delicious. Think broccoli, bell peppers, carrots, whatever you've got — tossed in a garlicky soy sauce with just a little kick. Mi gente, this is how you eat well without spending a dime.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
15 mins
Total
25 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or any neutral oil you have
  • 1 medium head broccoli, cut into small florets
  • 2 medium carrots, thinly sliced on the diagonal
  • 1 large red bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 medium zucchini, halved and sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas or frozen peas, thawed
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated (or 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional but worth it)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons water
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes or a splash of hot sauce
  • Cooked white rice or noodles, for serving
  • Sesame seeds or sliced green onions, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Mix your sauce first so you're not scrambling later — whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, and water in a small bowl and set it right next to the stove. This is the move that separates calm cooks from panicked ones.
  2. 2Get your largest skillet or wok over the highest heat your stove has. We're talking seriously hot — let it sit there for a full 2 minutes before you add anything. A hot pan is the whole secret to stir fry that doesn't steam into a soggy pile.
  3. 3Add the vegetable oil and swirl it around. The second it starts to shimmer and almost smoke, add the carrots and broccoli first since they take the longest. Spread them out and let them sit without stirring for about 90 seconds — that's how you get that beautiful little char.
  4. 4Toss in the bell pepper and zucchini and keep things moving now, stirring every 30 seconds or so. Cook for another 2 minutes until everything is starting to get tender but still has some crunch to it.
  5. 5Push all the vegetables to the outer edges of the pan to create a hot empty center. Drop in the garlic and ginger right into that center spot and let them sizzle for about 30 seconds until fragrant — you'll know because your whole kitchen is going to smell incredible.
  6. 6Add the snap peas or thawed frozen peas and toss everything together. Give your sauce a quick stir since the cornstarch settles, then pour it all over the vegetables.
  7. 7Toss and stir constantly for about 1 to 2 minutes until the sauce thickens and coats every single vegetable in that glossy, garlicky goodness. If it gets too thick, splash in a tablespoon of water.
  8. 8Pull it off the heat and hit it with the red pepper flakes. Serve immediately over rice or noodles and top with sesame seeds or green onions if you have them around. Eat it hot — stir fry waits for nobody.
💡 Improv Tip

The biggest mistake people make with stir fry is crowding the pan — if you overload it, the vegetables steam instead of sear and you lose all that flavor. If you're doubling the recipe, cook it in two separate batches. I know it feels like extra work but trust me, your taste buds will absolutely know the difference.
