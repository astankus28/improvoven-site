Easy Broccoli Cheddar Soup Recipe for Meal Prep (Ready in 30 Minutes)

Published April 30, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy broccoli cheddar soup recipe is basically made for meal prep — you cook one big pot on Sunday and you're eating like royalty all week long. It's thick, cheesy, and way better than anything that comes in a can. Real talk: once you make this at home you're never going back.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 4 cups fresh or frozen broccoli florets, roughly chopped
  • 1 large carrot, shredded or finely diced
  • 2 cups sharp cheddar cheese, freshly shredded
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional but recommended)
  • Crusty bread or crackers for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Melt the butter in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for about 4 to 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it softens and starts to look a little translucent. Add the garlic and cook for one more minute until it smells amazing — don't walk away here, garlic burns fast.
  2. 2Sprinkle the flour over the onion and garlic mixture and stir it constantly for about 1 to 2 minutes. You're building a roux — this is what gives the soup that thick, velvety body. It should look like a pale paste clinging to everything in the pot.
  3. 3Slowly pour in the milk while whisking continuously, then add the broth. Keep whisking as you pour so you don't get lumps. Raise the heat to medium-high and let the mixture come to a gentle simmer, stirring frequently, about 3 to 4 minutes until it starts to thicken up.
  4. 4Add the chopped broccoli and shredded carrot to the pot. Stir in the smoked paprika, cumin, red pepper flakes if using, and a good pinch of salt and black pepper. Reduce heat to medium-low and let everything simmer together for 8 to 10 minutes until the broccoli is completely tender.
  5. 5Use an immersion blender to partially blend the soup directly in the pot — you want some chunks left for texture, not a completely smooth puree. If you don't have an immersion blender, carefully transfer about half the soup to a regular blender, blend it, then stir it back in. Either way works perfectly.
  6. 6Turn the heat down to low. Add the shredded cheddar cheese in two or three handfuls, stirring between each addition until fully melted and smooth. Taste and adjust salt and pepper. Do not let the soup boil after adding the cheese or it can get grainy — keep it low and slow at this stage.
  7. 7Serve hot with crusty bread for dipping. For meal prep, let the soup cool completely before dividing into airtight containers. It keeps in the fridge for up to 4 days and reheats beautifully on the stovetop over low heat with a small splash of broth or milk to loosen it back up.
💡 Improv Tip

Listen — shred your own cheddar from a block, not the pre-shredded bag stuff. The bagged cheese has a coating on it that makes the soup grainy and sad instead of silky and smooth. A block of sharp cheddar from the deli section is only a dollar or two more and it makes a massive difference. This is the one place where I'll always tell you to spend the extra minute.
