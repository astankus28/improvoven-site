Simple Easter Sugar Cookies Recipe, Decorated and Ready to Share

Published April 4, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Easter sugar cookies recipe decorated with colorful royal icing is the kind of thing you make on a quiet Saturday morning and have ready for the whole family by lunchtime. No fancy equipment, no pastry degree — just pantry staples, a little patience, and some good music playing in the kitchen. Think of it like edible art that also happens to taste like butter and vanilla dreams.

Prep
25 mins
Cook
12 mins
Total
37 mins
Serves
24 cookies

Ingredients

  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated white sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract (optional but trust me)
  • 2 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 3 tablespoons milk or water
  • 1 tablespoon light corn syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (for the icing)
  • Food coloring in pastel shades — pink, yellow, lavender, light blue
  • Sprinkles, sanding sugar, or candy pearls for decorating

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F and line two baking sheets with parchment paper. If you don't have parchment, a light spray of cooking oil on the pan works fine — we improvise around here.
  2. 2In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Set this aside — it's your dry team.
  3. 3In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar together until it looks pale, fluffy, and almost creamy — about 3 minutes with a hand mixer or a good 5 minutes if you're going old school with a wooden spoon and some determination.
  4. 4Add the egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract to the butter mixture and beat until everything is smooth and well combined.
  5. 5Gradually add your dry ingredients into the wet mixture, stirring until a soft dough forms. It should come together cleanly and not stick too much to your hands. If it feels sticky, pop it in the fridge for 15 minutes.
  6. 6On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough out to about 1/4 inch thickness. Cut out your Easter shapes — eggs, bunnies, chicks, flowers, whatever cookie cutters you've got. No cookie cutters? Use the rim of a glass for circles and call them Easter eggs. Done.
  7. 7Place cookies on your prepared baking sheets about 1 inch apart and bake for 10 to 12 minutes, until the edges are just barely golden. Don't overbake — you want these soft in the center.
  8. 8Let cookies cool on the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack and cool completely before decorating. This part is non-negotiable — warm cookies will melt your icing and break your heart.
  9. 9While cookies cool, make your simple royal icing: whisk together the powdered sugar, milk, corn syrup, and vanilla extract until smooth and glossy. The consistency should be like thick honey — it flows off a spoon slowly but holds its shape.
  10. 10Divide the icing into small bowls and tint each one with a few drops of pastel food coloring. Stir well. For Easter vibes, go soft — a little pink, a whisper of lavender, sunny yellow, sky blue.
  11. 11Spoon or pipe the icing onto your cooled cookies. Use the back of a spoon to spread it gently to the edges, or pipe an outline first and flood the center — your call. While the icing is still wet, add sprinkles, sanding sugar, or candy pearls.
  12. 12Let the decorated cookies sit at room temperature for at least 1 hour so the icing sets firm. Once dry, they stack beautifully and keep for up to 5 days in an airtight container.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to cookies that actually hold their shape when you cut and bake them is chilling your dough. Even 20 minutes in the fridge before rolling makes a huge difference — the butter firms up and your little bunnies won't spread into blobs. If you're doing this with kids and time is short, skip the fancy piping and just dip the tops of the cookies straight into the icing bowl and let the kids go wild with sprinkles. Messy, beautiful, and honestly more fun.
