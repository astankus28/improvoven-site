Easy Vegetarian Easter Side Dishes: Garlic Herb Roasted Vegetables with Lemon Butter Gnocchi

Published March 31, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're pulling together easy vegetarian Easter side dishes and want something that actually feels special enough for the holiday table, this is your move — roasted seasonal vegetables tossed with pillowy pan-fried gnocchi in a bright lemon herb butter. It comes together in under 30 minutes with stuff you probably already have kicking around, and it hits that sweet spot between comfort food and something that looks like you really tried.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shelf-stable or refrigerated potato gnocchi
  • 2 cups broccoli florets, cut small
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (or nutritional yeast to keep it fully vegan)
  • 1 teaspoon dried Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or basil for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 425°F. Toss the broccoli florets and diced zucchini with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, the Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Spread them out in a single layer on a sheet pan — don't crowd them or they'll steam instead of roast. Roast for 15 to 18 minutes until the edges are golden and slightly caramelized. Add the cherry tomatoes in the last 5 minutes so they burst and get jammy without turning to mush.
  2. 2While the vegetables roast, heat the remaining tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the gnocchi straight from the package — no boiling needed — and let them sit undisturbed for 2 to 3 minutes until a golden crust forms on the bottom. Shake the pan and let them get golden on another side, about 2 more minutes. They should look like little pillows with a tan. Transfer them to a bowl and set aside.
  3. 3In the same skillet, reduce heat to medium and melt the butter. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 60 seconds, stirring constantly — just until it smells incredible and turns barely golden. Don't walk away here, garlic goes from perfect to burnt in a blink.
  4. 4Add the roasted vegetables and pan-fried gnocchi into the skillet with the garlic butter. Pour in the lemon juice, add the lemon zest, and toss everything together so every piece gets coated in that glossy sauce. Taste it and adjust salt and pepper as needed.
  5. 5Plate it up, hit it with the Parmesan or nutritional yeast, a pinch more red pepper flakes if you like heat, and a handful of fresh parsley or torn basil. Serve immediately while the gnocchi are still crispy — that texture is everything.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to this dish is not boiling the gnocchi — pan-frying them straight from the package gives you that crispy outside and soft inside that makes people think you did something way more complicated than you actually did. If your gnocchi are sticking together in the bag, just separate them with your hands before they hit the pan and you're golden.
