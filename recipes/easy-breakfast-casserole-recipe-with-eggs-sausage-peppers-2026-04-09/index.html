Easy Breakfast Casserole Recipe with Eggs, Sausage & Peppers

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy breakfast casserole recipe is the one you make when you've got a fridge full of random stuff and people to feed — no stress, no fancy techniques, just real food that comes together in one pan. It's got that Miami kitchen energy: a little sofrito spirit, some smoky sausage, and eggs that bake up fluffy and golden. Make it Sunday, eat it all week — breakfast is handled.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
40 mins
Total
55 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk (whole or whatever you have)
  • 1/2 lb smoked sausage or kielbasa, sliced into half-moons
  • 1 medium green bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese, divided
  • 2 cups day-old bread, torn into chunks (any bread works — Cuban bread is amazing here)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for topping (optional but worth it)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F and lightly grease a 9x13 baking dish or any oven-safe dish you have — this recipe is forgiving like that.
  2. 2Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the sausage slices and cook about 3 minutes until they get a little color on them. Remove and set aside — don't wash that pan, that flavor stays.
  3. 3In the same skillet, add the diced onion and bell pepper. Cook 4-5 minutes until softened, then add the garlic, cumin, and smoked paprika. Stir for one more minute until fragrant. Your kitchen should smell incredible right now.
  4. 4Spread the torn bread chunks evenly across the bottom of your greased baking dish. Layer the cooked sausage and the pepper-onion mixture right on top.
  5. 5In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until fully combined. Season generously with salt and black pepper. Pour the egg mixture evenly over everything in the baking dish, making sure the bread soaks it all up — press it down gently if you need to.
  6. 6Sprinkle 3/4 of the shredded cheddar over the top. Let the whole thing sit for 5 minutes so the bread really absorbs the egg mixture — this is the secret to a casserole that's custardy instead of dry.
  7. 7Bake uncovered for 35-40 minutes, until the eggs are set in the center and the top is golden and slightly puffed. You'll know it's done when a knife inserted in the middle comes out clean.
  8. 8Pull it out, scatter the remaining cheese on top and let it melt from the residual heat. Top with fresh cilantro or parsley if you've got it. Let it rest 5 minutes before slicing — this helps everything hold together when you serve it.
💡 Improv Tip

Stale or day-old bread is your best friend in a casserole — fresh bread gets mushy because it can't absorb the egg custard properly. Got leftover rice or roasted potatoes instead of bread? Swap them in, no problem. This casserole is basically a blank canvas, so open that fridge and work with what you've got — leftover ham, a handful of spinach, some pepper jack cheese — it all belongs here.
