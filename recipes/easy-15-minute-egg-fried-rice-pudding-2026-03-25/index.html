Easy 15-Minute Egg Fried Rice Pudding

Published March 25, 2026 · Improv Oven

Okay hear me out — this easy 15-minute egg fried rice pudding takes everything you love about classic arroz con leche and gives it a toasty, caramelized twist by starting with day-old rice in a hot pan. It's creamy, fragrant, and comes together so fast you'll be spooning it into bowls before you even realize what happened. This is the dessert your abuela would make if she had a wok and zero patience.

Prep
5 mins
Cook
10 mins
Total
15 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked white rice, day-old and cold
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 3 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon, plus more for topping
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: toasted coconut flakes or raisins for topping

Instructions

  1. 1Get your pan hot first — heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat and melt the butter until it starts to foam. Cold day-old rice is your best friend here because it fries up instead of steaming into mush.
  2. 2Add the cold rice to the pan and press it down into an even layer. Let it sit undisturbed for about 2 minutes so it gets a little golden and toasty on the bottom — that caramelized layer is where all the magic lives.
  3. 3Push the rice to one side of the pan, crack both eggs into the empty space, and scramble them quickly with a spatula. Once they're about 80 percent cooked but still a little glossy, fold them right into the rice and stir everything together.
  4. 4Pour in the coconut milk and sweetened condensed milk, then add the vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and pinch of salt. Stir everything together and reduce the heat to medium.
  5. 5Let the mixture bubble gently for about 4 to 5 minutes, stirring frequently, until the liquid thickens and coats the rice like a loose, creamy pudding. It will thicken a little more as it cools, so pull it off the heat when it still looks slightly saucy.
  6. 6Spoon into bowls, dust generously with extra cinnamon, and top with toasted coconut flakes or raisins if you have them. Serve warm or let it cool to room temperature — honestly both versions are incredible.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon here is truly cold, day-old rice straight from the fridge — freshly cooked rice has too much moisture and will turn gummy the second it hits the hot pan. If you only have fresh rice, spread it on a baking sheet and pop it in the freezer for 15 minutes before you start. And don't skip that toasting step at the beginning — those little golden bits of rice give the pudding a nutty depth that makes people ask what your secret is.
