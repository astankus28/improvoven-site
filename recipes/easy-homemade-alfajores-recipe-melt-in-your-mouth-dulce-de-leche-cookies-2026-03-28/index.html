Easy Homemade Alfajores Recipe (Melt-in-Your-Mouth Dulce de Leche Cookies)

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy homemade alfajores recipe is the one your abuela would've kept secret — buttery, crumbly shortbread cookies sandwiched around thick dulce de leche and rolled in coconut. They look like you went to a fancy bakery but cost almost nothing and come together with stuff you probably already have in your pantry. Trust me, once you make these, they're going to disappear fast.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
12 mins
Total
32 mins
Serves
20 cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon rum or orange juice (optional but worth it)
  • 1 can (13.4 oz) store-bought dulce de leche
  • 1/2 cup shredded sweetened coconut, for rolling

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a medium bowl, whisk together the cornstarch, flour, powdered sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside — this dry mix is what gives alfajores that signature crumbly, melt-away texture.
  2. 2In a large bowl, beat the softened butter with a hand mixer or wooden spoon until it's creamy and light, about 2 minutes. Add the egg yolks one at a time, mixing well after each one. Stir in the vanilla and rum or orange juice if you're using it — that little splash adds a beautiful depth of flavor.
  3. 3Add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture and mix gently until a soft dough forms. Don't overwork it. If it feels sticky, pop it in the fridge for 10 minutes — the butter just needs to firm back up.
  4. 4On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough out to about 1/4 inch thick. Use a round cookie cutter or the rim of a glass to cut out circles about 1.5 to 2 inches in diameter. Re-roll the scraps and keep cutting until all the dough is used up.
  5. 5Place the rounds on your prepared baking sheet and bake for 10 to 12 minutes. You want them just barely golden on the bottom — they should still look pale on top. These cookies are meant to be soft and delicate, not crunchy, so don't overbake them. Let them cool completely on the pan before touching them.
  6. 6Once the cookies are fully cooled, flip half of them upside down. Spoon about a teaspoon of dulce de leche onto each flipped cookie, then press a second cookie on top to make a sandwich. Press gently — they're fragile and that's what makes them so special.
  7. 7Pour the shredded coconut into a shallow bowl. Roll each sandwich cookie along its edge through the coconut so it sticks to the dulce de leche peeking out the sides. Set them on a plate and repeat until all your alfajores are assembled. They're ready to eat right away, but they get even better after an hour when the filling settles into the cookies.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to that iconic crumbly texture is the high ratio of cornstarch to flour — so don't be tempted to swap it out for more flour thinking it's the same thing. Also, store-bought dulce de leche from the Latin aisle at your grocery store (look for Nestle La Lechera or any Argentine brand) is totally the move here. No shame in that shortcut — even the best home bakers use it.
