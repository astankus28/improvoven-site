Easy Cuban Tres Golpes Dessert (Three Hits of Sweet in One Bowl)

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Cuban tres golpes dessert is the kind of thing that shows up at every abuela's table and disappears in about five minutes flat — three simple ingredients, three layers of joy. We're talking guava paste, cream cheese, and saltine crackers coming together in the most humble, ridiculously satisfying way. It's not fancy, it costs almost nothing, and once you taste it you will fully understand why Cubans have been riding with this combo forever.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
0 mins
Total
10 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 1 block (8 oz) cream cheese, softened at room temperature
  • 1 block (10-12 oz) guava paste, sliced into thin rounds or squares
  • 1 sleeve saltine crackers (about 40 crackers)
  • Optional: fresh mint leaves for garnish
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon honey for a little extra drizzle

Instructions

  1. 1Pull your cream cheese out of the fridge at least 20 minutes before you start — cold cream cheese is stiff and nobody wants to fight their dessert. You want it soft enough to spread easily with a butter knife.
  2. 2Slice your guava paste into pieces roughly the same size as your crackers. It doesn't have to be perfect — rustic is part of the charm here. If your guava paste is very firm, a sharp knife dipped in warm water makes cleaner cuts.
  3. 3Now build your tres golpes: lay out a saltine cracker, spread a generous layer of softened cream cheese on top, then place a slice of guava paste right on top of that. Three ingredients, three layers — that's the three hits, the tres golpes.
  4. 4Arrange them on a plate or wooden board. If you want to go a little extra, drizzle the tiniest bit of honey over the top and drop a small mint leaf on each one. That's it. You're done.
  5. 5Serve immediately at room temperature so the cream cheese stays soft and the guava paste has that jammy, almost melty quality. These also work beautifully as a passed appetizer at parties — don't let the dessert label stop you.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon here is temperature — if your cream cheese is cold, the whole thing feels stiff and a little sad. If your guava paste has been sitting in the fridge, give it five minutes on the counter too. Room temperature ingredients let the flavors actually open up and do their thing. Also, store-brand guava paste works just as well as the fancy stuff — look for it in the Latin or international aisle, usually right next to the sofrito.
