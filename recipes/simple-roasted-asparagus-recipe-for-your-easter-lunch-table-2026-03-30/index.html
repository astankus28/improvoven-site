Simple Roasted Asparagus Recipe for Your Easter Lunch Table

Published March 30, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple roasted asparagus recipe is the Easter side dish that low-key steals the whole show — crispy tips, tender stalks, and it takes about 20 minutes in the oven. We're talking the kind of dish that makes people think you spent way more time than you did, which is exactly how we roll at Improv Oven. Dress it up with a squeeze of lemon and some shaved parmesan and watch it disappear before the main course even lands on the table.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 large bunch fresh asparagus (about 1 lb), woody ends snapped off
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional but recommended)
  • 1 lemon, half for roasting and half for serving
  • 1/4 cup shaved or grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon capers, drained (optional, adds a nice briny punch)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 425°F. High heat is the secret here — we want those asparagus tips to get a little crispy and caramelized, not steamed and sad.
  2. 2Snap off the woody ends of the asparagus. The easiest way is to hold one spear at both ends and bend it — it'll break right where the tough part starts. Use that one as your guide and line-cut the rest.
  3. 3Spread the asparagus in a single layer on a large sheet pan. This is important — if they're piled on top of each other they'll steam instead of roast. If you need two pans, use two pans.
  4. 4Drizzle the olive oil over the asparagus, scatter the sliced garlic over the top, and season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes. Toss everything together right on the pan with your hands or tongs until every spear is coated.
  5. 5Cut the lemon in half. Squeeze one half over the asparagus right now before roasting — this brightens everything up from the inside. Cut the other half into wedges for serving.
  6. 6Roast in the oven for 15 to 20 minutes, depending on how thick your spears are. Thin asparagus will be done around 13 to 15 minutes. You want the tips just slightly crispy and the stalks tender when you pierce them with a fork.
  7. 7Pull the pan out and immediately scatter the parmesan over the hot asparagus so it melts slightly into the spears. Add the capers if you're using them.
  8. 8Transfer to a serving platter, hit it with a squeeze from the reserved lemon wedges, and serve warm or at room temperature. Both work beautifully for an Easter lunch spread.
💡 Improv Tip

Buy your asparagus the day before Easter so you're not scrambling — it keeps great in the fridge standing upright in a jar with an inch of water, like flowers. And if your bunch is thick, just add 3 to 4 extra minutes in the oven and don't stress it. Asparagus is way more forgiving than people think.
