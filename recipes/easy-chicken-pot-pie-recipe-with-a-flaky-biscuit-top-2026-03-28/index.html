Easy Chicken Pot Pie Recipe with a Flaky Biscuit Top

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy chicken pot pie recipe skips the fuss of homemade pastry dough and uses refrigerated biscuits instead — and honestly? It's better this way. Creamy, loaded with veggies, and bubbling hot from the oven, this is the kind of comfort food that makes your whole apartment smell incredible. Pure weeknight magic, no culinary degree required.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
35 mins
Total
50 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded or chopped (rotisserie works great)
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of chicken soup
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn, green beans)
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon adobo seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter
  • 1 can (16 oz) refrigerated biscuits (8 count)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F. Grab a 9x13 inch baking dish or a large oven-safe skillet and set it aside.
  2. 2Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for about 3 to 4 minutes until it softens and starts to look a little translucent. Add the garlic and cook one more minute — just until fragrant.
  3. 3Add the cream of chicken soup and chicken broth to the skillet and stir everything together. It should come together into a thick, creamy sauce. If it looks too thick, splash in a little more broth.
  4. 4Stir in the frozen mixed vegetables, shredded chicken, cumin, garlic powder, black pepper, and adobo. Let everything simmer together on medium-low for about 5 minutes so the flavors can get friendly with each other.
  5. 5Pour the filling into your baking dish and spread it out evenly. Open up your biscuits and place them right on top of the filling in a single layer — they don't need to cover every inch, they'll puff up and spread as they bake.
  6. 6Slide the dish into the oven and bake uncovered for 22 to 25 minutes, until the biscuits are deep golden brown on top and the filling is bubbling up around the edges. Let it sit for 5 minutes before serving so it can thicken up a bit.
  7. 7Scoop into bowls making sure everyone gets a biscuit on top. Serve hot and watch it disappear.
💡 Improv Tip

Leftover rotisserie chicken is your best friend here — grab one from the store for under $7 and you've got enough chicken for this recipe plus lunch tomorrow. The adobo and cumin are the Improv Oven twist that take this from basic American comfort food to something with a little Miami soul. Don't skip them.
