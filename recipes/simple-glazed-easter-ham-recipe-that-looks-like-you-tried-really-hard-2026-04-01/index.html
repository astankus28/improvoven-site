Simple Glazed Easter Ham Recipe (That Looks Like You Tried Really Hard)

Published April 1, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Easter ham recipe glazed with a sweet and tangy brown sugar mixture is the kind of dish that makes your whole family think you spent all day in the kitchen — and you did not. We're talking pantry staples, minimal effort, and that gorgeous caramelized crust that has everyone reaching for seconds. Very Miami move: we're adding a little pineapple juice to the glaze because citrus makes everything better, trust.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
1 hr 30 mins
Total
1 hr 40 mins
Serves
8-10

Ingredients

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in ham (7-8 lbs, whatever is on sale)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 3 tablespoons pineapple juice (from a can — use the rest for drinks)
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Whole cloves for studding (optional but pretty)
  • Pineapple rings for garnish (optional, from that same can)

Instructions

  1. 1Pull your ham out of the fridge about 30 minutes before cooking so it's not ice cold going into the oven — this helps it cook evenly. Preheat your oven to 325°F while you wait.
  2. 2Place the ham flat-side down in a roasting pan or a deep baking dish. If your ham has a thick fat cap on top, score it with a sharp knife in a diamond pattern — cut about 1/2 inch deep. This is what gives you that beautiful look on the table and lets the glaze sink in.
  3. 3If you're using whole cloves, press one into the center of each diamond on the scored fat. It's old school and it smells incredible while it bakes.
  4. 4In a small bowl, whisk together the brown sugar, pineapple juice, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, ground cloves, and garlic powder until it forms a thick, glossy glaze. Taste it — it should be sweet, a little tangy, and slightly sharp from the mustard. Good? Let's go.
  5. 5Brush about one third of the glaze all over the ham generously. Cover the pan tightly with aluminum foil and slide it into the oven. Bake for about 15 minutes per pound — so a 7-pound ham will take roughly 1 hour and 45 minutes total.
  6. 6About 30 minutes before the ham is done, remove the foil and brush on another generous layer of glaze. Put it back in uncovered so that caramelization can start happening. That's the good stuff.
  7. 7In the last 10-15 minutes, brush on the remaining glaze and crank the oven up to 400°F. Keep an eye on it — you want it bubbly, sticky, and deeply golden, not burnt. Pull it when it looks like something you want to photograph.
  8. 8Let the ham rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing. This is not optional — resting keeps all the juices inside the meat where they belong. Use this time to warm up your sides and pour yourself something cold.
💡 Improv Tip

Bone-in hams are almost always cheaper per pound than boneless AND they taste better because the bone adds flavor while it heats through. After Easter, throw that leftover ham bone in a pot with some black beans, sofrito, and water and you've got Cuban black bean soup for the whole week. Nothing gets wasted around here.
