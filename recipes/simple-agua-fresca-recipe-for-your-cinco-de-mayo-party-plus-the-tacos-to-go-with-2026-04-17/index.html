Simple Agua Fresca Recipe for Your Cinco de Mayo Party (Plus the Tacos to Go With It)

Published April 17, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're putting together a simple agua fresca recipe for a Cinco de Mayo party, you already know the drinks are half the vibe — but let's make sure the food keeps up. This is a full Cinco de Mayo dinner setup: a big pitcher of watermelon agua fresca alongside smoky chicken tinga tacos that come together fast, taste like you spent all day, and cost way less than ordering out. Miami summer energy meets Mexican kitchen magic, and your crew is going to be very happy.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
40 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • FOR THE WATERMELON AGUA FRESCA:
  • 6 cups seedless watermelon, cubed (about half a small watermelon)
  • 4 cups cold water
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 2 limes)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar or honey, adjust to taste
  • Pinch of salt
  • Ice and fresh mint or cucumber slices for serving
  • FOR THE CHICKEN TINGA TACOS:
  • 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes with juice
  • 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, plus 1 tablespoon of the sauce
  • 1 medium white onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 12 small corn tortillas
  • FOR TOPPING:
  • 1/2 cup sour cream or Mexican crema
  • 1 cup crumbled cotija cheese or shredded queso blanco
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced

Instructions

  1. 1MAKE THE AGUA FRESCA FIRST so it has time to chill: Add the watermelon cubes to a blender and blend until completely smooth, about 30 seconds. You should have bright, gorgeous red juice.
  2. 2Pour the watermelon juice through a fine mesh strainer into a large pitcher, pressing with a spoon to get all the liquid out. Discard the pulp — or eat it, no judgment.
  3. 3Add the cold water, lime juice, sugar or honey, and that pinch of salt to the pitcher. Stir well, taste, and adjust sweetness or lime to your liking. Refrigerate until ready to serve. This is your drink sorted.
  4. 4NOW START THE TINGA: Season your chicken thighs all over with salt, cumin, and oregano. Heat the oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  5. 5Sear the chicken thighs for about 3-4 minutes per side until golden. They don't need to be fully cooked through yet — just get that color. Remove them to a plate.
  6. 6In the same pan, drop the heat to medium and add your sliced onions. Cook for 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they soften and start to turn golden. Add the garlic and cook another minute until fragrant.
  7. 7Add the can of diced tomatoes, chipotles in adobo, and the tablespoon of adobo sauce to the pan. Stir everything together. Using the back of a spoon, break the chipotles into smaller pieces so their smoky heat spreads through the whole sauce.
  8. 8Nestle the seared chicken thighs back into the sauce. Cover, reduce heat to medium-low, and let everything simmer together for 15 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked through.
  9. 9Remove the chicken to a cutting board and shred it using two forks — it should fall apart easily. Return the shredded chicken to the pan and toss it in all that smoky tomato sauce. Let it simmer uncovered for 3-4 more minutes so the chicken soaks everything up and the sauce tightens slightly. Taste for salt.
  10. 10WARM YOUR TORTILLAS: Working in batches, heat corn tortillas directly over a gas flame for about 20 seconds per side for that authentic char, or in a dry skillet over medium-high heat. Wrap them in a clean kitchen towel to keep warm.
  11. 11BUILD YOUR TACOS: Layer the chicken tinga onto the warm tortillas, then top with a drizzle of crema, a pinch of cotija, fresh cilantro, and a slice of avocado. Squeeze lime over everything.
  12. 12Serve the tacos alongside big glasses of your watermelon agua fresca poured over ice, garnished with mint or cucumber. Stand back and accept the compliments.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to great tinga is letting that shredded chicken hang out in the sauce — don't rush it. Those last few minutes of simmering uncovered are where the magic happens, because the chicken absorbs all that chipotle smokiness instead of just sitting next to it. And for your agua fresca, make it the night before if you can. An overnight rest in the fridge makes the flavors marry beautifully, and one less thing to stress about on party day is always a win.
