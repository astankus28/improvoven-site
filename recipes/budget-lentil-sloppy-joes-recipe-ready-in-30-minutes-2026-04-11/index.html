Budget Lentil Sloppy Joes Recipe (Ready in 30 Minutes)

Published April 11, 2026 · Improv Oven

This budget lentil sloppy joes recipe is the kind of thing that makes you wonder why you ever spent money on ground beef in the first place — we're talking messy, saucy, deeply satisfying sandwiches for practically nothing. Red lentils cook down into this thick, hearty filling that soaks up all that smoky-sweet sauce like a dream. It's the cure for a near-empty fridge and a hungry family, and nobody's going to miss the meat.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried red lentils, rinsed
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar or honey
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce (use soy sauce to keep it vegan)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 hamburger buns, toasted

Instructions

  1. 1Add the rinsed red lentils and water or broth to a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a simmer. Cook uncovered for about 12 to 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the lentils are soft and most of the liquid is absorbed. You want them tender but not totally mushy — they'll cook a little more in the sauce.
  2. 2While the lentils cook, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and bell pepper and cook for 4 to 5 minutes until softened and just starting to get a little color. Add the garlic and cook another 60 seconds until fragrant.
  3. 3Add the cooked lentils into the skillet with the vegetables and stir everything together. Pour in the tomato sauce, ketchup, brown sugar, Worcestershire sauce, smoked paprika, cumin, and chili powder. Stir well to combine.
  4. 4Let the mixture simmer on medium-low for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens up and everything looks saucy and gorgeous. Taste and adjust salt, pepper, or a little more sugar if you want it slightly sweeter.
  5. 5Pile that filling high onto toasted buns and serve immediately. Have extra napkins ready — this is supposed to be messy, that's the whole point.
💡 Improv Tip

Red lentils are the move here because they break down fast and get that soft, almost meaty texture that holds the sauce perfectly — don't swap in green or brown lentils or you'll be waiting forever and the texture won't be the same. Also, toast your buns! Warm bread is fine, but a toasted bun with a little butter is the difference between a good sloppy joe and a great one.
