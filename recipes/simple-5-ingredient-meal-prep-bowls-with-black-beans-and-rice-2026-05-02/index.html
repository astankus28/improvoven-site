Simple 5 Ingredient Meal Prep Bowls with Black Beans and Rice

Published May 2, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you've been sleeping on simple 5 ingredient meal prep recipes, these black bean and rice bowls are about to change your whole week. We're talking pantry staples you probably already have, stacked into something that actually tastes like you put in effort. Miami-style comfort food, zero stress, ready to grab from the fridge all week long.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long grain white rice
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes with green chiles
  • 1 packet (1 oz) sazon seasoning or 2 teaspoons cumin plus 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

  1. 1Cook your rice first so everything finishes around the same time. Combine 2 cups of rice with 4 cups of water in a medium saucepan. Bring it to a boil over high heat, then drop it to the lowest setting, cover tight, and let it go for 18 minutes. Do not lift that lid — trust the process.
  2. 2While the rice is doing its thing, heat your olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once it shimmers, pour in the drained black beans and stir them around for about a minute just to get them coated.
  3. 3Add the entire can of diced tomatoes with chiles — liquid and all — right into the beans. Stir in your sazon packet or your cumin and garlic powder mix. Let everything simmer together for about 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce tightens up and smells incredible.
  4. 4Taste your beans and adjust salt if needed. Some sazon packets run salty, so check before you add anything.
  5. 5Fluff your rice with a fork and divide it evenly among 4 meal prep containers. Spoon a generous portion of the black bean mixture right over the top. Let everything cool to room temperature before sealing — about 15 minutes — so you don't get soggy rice from condensation.
  6. 6Store in the fridge for up to 5 days. To reheat, add a tiny splash of water before microwaving so the rice steams back to life instead of drying out.
💡 Improv Tip

This is your base — and it loves toppings. If you've got an avocado on its last leg, a squeeze of lime, some leftover shredded chicken, or even a fried egg, throw it on top when you're ready to eat. The bowls are intentionally simple so your fridge scraps can shine all week without you having to think too hard about it. That's the whole Improv Oven move.
