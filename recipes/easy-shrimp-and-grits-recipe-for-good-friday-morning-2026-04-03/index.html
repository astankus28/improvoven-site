Easy Shrimp and Grits Recipe for Good Friday Morning

Published April 3, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy shrimp and grits recipe for Good Friday is exactly what your table needs when you want something special but you're not trying to be in the kitchen all morning. It's creamy, a little smoky, loaded with plump shrimp, and it comes together in about 30 minutes — which means you still make it to service on time. Think of it as the Miami cousin of the classic Southern bowl, with a little sofrito spirit in every bite.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup quick-cooking grits
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 pound medium shrimp, peeled and deveined (fresh or frozen-thawed)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced small
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, diced small
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of half a lime
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped
  • 4 large eggs (optional, for frying on top)
  • Hot sauce for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a medium saucepan with 1/2 teaspoon salt. Slowly whisk in the grits, reduce the heat to low, and cook according to package directions — usually about 5 to 7 minutes — stirring frequently so nothing sticks to the bottom.
  2. 2Once the grits are thick and creamy, stir in the butter and shredded cheddar until fully melted. Taste and adjust salt. Cover the pot and set it aside on the lowest heat while you cook everything else.
  3. 3Pat your shrimp dry with a paper towel and season them with smoked paprika, cumin, red pepper flakes, salt, and black pepper. Dry shrimp = better sear. Don't skip this step.
  4. 4Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onion and green bell pepper and cook for about 3 to 4 minutes until softened and just starting to get a little color on the edges.
  5. 5Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant — you'll smell exactly when it's ready. Add the seasoned shrimp in a single layer and cook for about 90 seconds per side until pink and just cooked through. Squeeze in the lime juice and toss everything together. Remove from heat immediately so the shrimp don't overcook.
  6. 6If you want to add a fried egg on top — and honestly, you should — wipe out the pan, add a little butter, and fry your eggs over medium heat to your preferred doneness while the shrimp rest.
  7. 7To serve, spoon a generous mound of cheesy grits into each bowl. Top with the shrimp mixture and all those good pan juices. Add a fried egg on top if using. Finish with fresh cilantro or parsley and a few dashes of hot sauce. Serve immediately.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen shrimp is completely fine here and honestly what most of us have on hand — just make sure they're fully thawed and dried before they hit the pan. Wet shrimp will steam instead of sear and you'll lose that beautiful golden crust. Run them under cold water to thaw fast, then spread them on a paper towel for a few minutes before seasoning. Budget move that makes a real difference.
