Simple Argentine Grilled Vegetables (Verduras a la Parrilla)

Published March 27, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Argentine grilled vegetables recipe is straight-up one of the easiest ways to make a pile of produce taste like something special — no fancy technique, just fire, olive oil, and a little chimichurri magic. Argentines have been grilling veggies alongside their asado for generations, and honestly? The vegetables might be the best thing on the table. We're keeping it budget-friendly and flexible, so grab whatever's looking good at the store and let's get that parrilla going.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 medium zucchini, sliced lengthwise into 1/4-inch planks
  • 1 large red bell pepper, quartered and seeded
  • 1 large yellow bell pepper, quartered and seeded
  • 1 medium red onion, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 2 medium tomatoes, halved
  • 8 oz mushrooms, stems trimmed
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil (for chimichurri)

Instructions

  1. 1Start by making your quick chimichurri so the flavors have time to come together. In a small bowl, combine the chopped parsley, minced garlic, red wine vinegar, red pepper flakes, and 3 tablespoons of olive oil. Stir it all together, taste it, add a pinch of salt, and set it aside. This little sauce is what takes everything from good to unforgettable.
  2. 2Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. You want it hot enough that a drop of water sizzles and dances on contact — that's how you get those beautiful char marks.
  3. 3Lay all your prepped vegetables out on a sheet pan or large plate. Drizzle the 3 tablespoons of olive oil over everything and toss to coat. Season with the kosher salt, black pepper, and garlic powder, then toss again so every piece is covered.
  4. 4Place the vegetables on the hot grill. Work in batches if needed — don't crowd them or they'll steam instead of char. Grill the zucchini and peppers for about 4-5 minutes per side, the onion rounds for 5-6 minutes per side, the mushrooms for 3-4 minutes per side, and the tomatoes cut-side down for 4-5 minutes until they're soft and slightly caramelized.
  5. 5As each vegetable finishes, move it to a large serving platter. Once everything is grilled and beautifully charred, drizzle that gorgeous chimichurri generously over the top. Serve warm or at room temperature — both ways are incredible.
💡 Improv Tip

The real cure for refrigerator blindness here is knowing this recipe works with almost anything — eggplant, corn on the cob, asparagus, sweet potato slices, whatever you've got that needs to be used up. The chimichurri is the constant, so as long as that's on the plate, you're eating Argentine-style no matter what veggies you throw on the grill.
