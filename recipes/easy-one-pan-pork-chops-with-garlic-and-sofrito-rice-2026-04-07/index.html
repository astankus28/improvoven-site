Easy One Pan Pork Chops with Garlic and Sofrito Rice

Published April 7, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy one pan pork chops recipe is the kind of weeknight move that makes people think you actually had your life together — seared golden chops, fragrant garlic, and a quick pan sauce all done in one skillet. We're keeping it Miami-style with a little sofrito energy and ingredients you probably already have sitting in your fridge and pantry. Thirty minutes, one pan, zero excuses.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in pork chops (about 3/4 inch thick)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth or water with a pinch of bouillon
  • 1 tablespoon sofrito (jarred is totally fine)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish (optional but worth it)

Instructions

  1. 1Pat your pork chops dry with a paper towel — this is non-negotiable if you want that beautiful golden sear. Mix together the garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper in a small bowl, then rub that all over both sides of each chop like you mean it.
  2. 2Heat your oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. You want it hot — when a drop of water flicks in and dances, you're ready. Lay the pork chops in without crowding the pan and let them sear undisturbed for about 4 to 5 minutes per side until they're deeply golden. Don't touch them, don't move them — let the crust build. Transfer the chops to a plate and set aside.
  3. 3Turn the heat down to medium and add your minced garlic to the same pan. Stir it around for about 30 seconds until fragrant — your kitchen should smell incredible right now. Add the sofrito and stir it into the garlic for another 30 seconds, letting it cook down slightly.
  4. 4Pour in the chicken broth and scrape up all those browned bits from the bottom of the pan — that's pure flavor and you want every bit of it. Let the liquid simmer for about 2 minutes to reduce slightly, then swirl in the butter until it melts into a glossy little sauce.
  5. 5Nestle the pork chops back into the pan, spoon that sauce over the top, and let everything come together over low heat for 2 to 3 more minutes. The chops should be cooked through with no pink in the center — a meat thermometer should read 145°F if you want to be precise. Scatter some fresh cilantro on top and bring the whole pan straight to the table.
💡 Improv Tip

Thin pork chops are sneaky — they cook fast and dry out even faster. If yours are closer to half an inch thick, cut your sear time to 3 minutes per side and pull them off the heat the moment they hit 145°F. And listen, if you don't have sofrito in your fridge, just dice up a little onion, green pepper, and tomato paste — you'll get close enough to make this sing.
