Simple Mexican Shrimp Cocktail (Cóctel de Camarón)

Published April 14, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Mexican shrimp cocktail recipe is the real deal — not the sad little cup you get at a bad buffet, but the bold, tangy, ice-cold cóctel de camarón they serve in giant goblets at seafood spots all over Mexico and Miami. It's a full dinner situation: loaded with shrimp, avocado, and cucumber in a tomato-Clamato bath that hits every single note. Make a big batch, grab some tostadas, and eat it on a warm night like the beautiful person you are.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
5 mins
Total
25 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb medium shrimp, peeled and deveined (fresh or thawed frozen)
  • 1 cup Clamato juice
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice (about 3 limes), plus extra wedges for serving
  • 2 tablespoons hot sauce (Valentina or Tabasco work great)
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 medium cucumber, peeled, seeded, and diced small
  • 1/2 white onion, finely diced
  • 2 Roma tomatoes, seeded and diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced (leave seeds in if you want heat)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 ripe avocados, cubed
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • Tostadas or saltine crackers, for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Cook the shrimp: Bring a small pot of salted water to a boil. Drop in the shrimp and cook just until pink and curled, about 2 to 3 minutes — do not walk away, overcooked shrimp will ruin your whole vibe. Drain immediately and transfer to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking. Once cool, drain well, pat dry, and if the shrimp are large, chop them into bite-sized pieces. Set aside.
  2. 2Make the cocktail sauce: In a large bowl, whisk together the Clamato, ketchup, lime juice, hot sauce, and Worcestershire sauce until fully combined. Taste it — this is your base and it should be tangy, a little spicy, and bold. Adjust with more lime, hot sauce, or a pinch of salt until it tastes exactly right to you.
  3. 3Add the vegetables: Stir in the diced cucumber, white onion, Roma tomatoes, jalapeño, and cilantro. Give everything a good mix so the vegetables get coated in that sauce.
  4. 4Add the shrimp: Fold the cooked shrimp into the mixture. At this point you can let it chill in the refrigerator for 15 to 30 minutes if you have time — the flavors come together beautifully when it's cold — or serve it right away if you cannot wait, which is completely understandable.
  5. 5Add avocado last: Right before serving, gently fold in the cubed avocado so it stays in nice chunks and does not get mushy. Season the whole thing one more time with salt and a squeeze of lime.
  6. 6Serve it up: Spoon into large glasses, bowls, or giant goblets if you have them. Lay a few lime wedges on the side, hit it with a little extra hot sauce on top, and serve with tostadas or crackers for scooping. This is dinner — do not let anyone tell you otherwise.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen shrimp is your best friend here and genuinely what most taquería cooks are using — just thaw it overnight in the fridge or run it under cold water for 10 minutes. The secret move is letting the whole cocktail sit cold for at least 20 minutes before you eat it. That rest time lets the shrimp absorb the tangy sauce and makes everything taste like it came from a proper mariscos spot instead of your kitchen. Also, do not skip the Clamato — that is not negotiable, that is the soul of this dish.
