Homemade Cuban Flan Dessert Recipe (Classic Flan de Leche)

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

This homemade Cuban flan dessert recipe is the one your abuela would approve of — silky, wobbly, and drowning in that gorgeous amber caramel that makes everyone at the table go quiet for a second. It uses basic pantry staples you probably already have, which means no excuses and no special trip to the store. This is Miami comfort food at its finest, and once you nail it, you'll be the person everyone begs to bring dessert.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
55 mins
Total
1 hr 10 mins (plus 4 hrs chilling)
Serves
8

Ingredients

  • 1 cup granulated sugar (for caramel)
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F and set a kettle of water on to boil — you'll need it for the water bath. Grab a 9-inch round cake pan or a bundt pan if you want to feel fancy.
  2. 2Make the caramel first: pour the sugar and water into a small saucepan over medium heat. Do NOT stir it — just swirl the pan gently if you need to. Let it cook for 8 to 10 minutes until it turns a deep amber color, like dark honey. The second it hits that color, pull it off the heat and immediately pour it into your cake pan, tilting the pan quickly to coat the bottom evenly. It hardens fast, so move with confidence.
  3. 3Let the caramel set for a few minutes while you make the custard. In a blender or a large bowl with a whisk, combine the sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, eggs, vanilla extract, and pinch of salt. Blend or whisk until completely smooth — about 1 minute in a blender or 2 to 3 minutes by hand. You want zero streaks of egg.
  4. 4Gently pour the custard mixture over the hardened caramel in the pan. Don't worry if it looks like the caramel is going to float up — it won't. Trust the process.
  5. 5Set your filled pan inside a larger roasting pan or baking dish. Carefully pour the boiling water into the outer pan until it reaches about halfway up the sides of the flan pan. This water bath (baño de María) is what gives Cuban flan that impossibly smooth, creamy texture — don't skip it.
  6. 6Carefully slide the whole setup into the oven and bake for 50 to 55 minutes. The flan is done when the edges are set but the center still has a gentle jiggle — like Jell-O, not liquid. It will firm up as it cools.
  7. 7Remove the flan pan from the water bath and let it cool on the counter for about 30 minutes. Then cover it with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, but overnight is even better. Cold flan is life.
  8. 8To unmold, run a thin knife or spatula around the edges of the pan. Place a large plate or platter upside down over the pan, then flip them together in one confident motion. Lift the pan slowly and let that caramel cascade down over the flan like the beautiful thing it is. Slice and serve cold.
💡 Improv Tip

The number one flan killer is overcooking — if your custard comes out grainy or spongy instead of silky smooth, it went too long in the oven. Pull it out while the center still jiggles. Also, if your caramel seizes up or crystallizes before it turns amber, don't panic — add a tiny splash of water, lower the heat, and let it melt back down slowly. Caramel is dramatic but forgiving.
