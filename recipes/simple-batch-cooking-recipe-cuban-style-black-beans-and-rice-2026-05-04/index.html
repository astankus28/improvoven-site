Simple Batch Cooking Recipe: Cuban-Style Black Beans and Rice

Published May 4, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you've been looking for simple batch cooking recipes that actually make your week easier, this is the one to bookmark. We're talking a big pot of smoky, sofrito-kissed black beans over fluffy white rice — real Miami comfort food that feeds you for days without breaking the bank. Make it Sunday, eat like royalty Monday through Thursday.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
45 mins
Total
55 mins
Serves
8

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 small white onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for serving (optional)
  • Lime wedges for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the diced onion and green bell pepper and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything softens and turns translucent. This is your sofrito base and it is doing a lot of heavy lifting here — don't rush it.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic, cumin, and oregano to the pot. Stir everything together and let it cook for another 60 to 90 seconds until the garlic is fragrant. Your kitchen should smell incredible right about now.
  3. 3Add the drained black beans to the pot along with the bay leaf, white vinegar, sugar, and a generous pinch of salt and black pepper. Pour in 1 cup of water or broth, stir to combine, and bring everything to a gentle simmer. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the beans cook for 25 to 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. The liquid will thicken into a rich, glossy sauce. Taste and adjust salt as needed, then remove the bay leaf.
  4. 4While the beans are simmering, cook your rice. Combine the 2 cups of rice with 4 cups of water or broth in a separate medium saucepan. Add a pinch of salt and bring to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to the lowest setting, cover tightly, and cook for 18 minutes. Remove from heat and let it sit covered for 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork.
  5. 5To store for batch cooking, let both the beans and rice cool completely before portioning into individual airtight containers. The beans will keep in the fridge for up to 5 days and actually taste better on day two once the flavors have had time to get cozy together. Rice keeps well for up to 4 days. Reheat with a splash of water to bring everything back to life.
  6. 6Serve a big scoop of rice topped with the black beans, a squeeze of fresh lime, and some cilantro if you have it. From here you can go in a million directions — add a fried egg on top for breakfast, stuff it into a burrito, serve it alongside grilled chicken, or just eat it straight from the container standing over the sink with no shame whatsoever.
💡 Improv Tip

The vinegar and sugar in the beans is the secret weapon — it balances everything and gives the beans that deep, complex flavor you get at a real Cuban restaurant. Don't skip it. And if your beans look too thick when you reheat them during the week, just add a tablespoon or two of water and stir over medium heat. They'll loosen right up.
