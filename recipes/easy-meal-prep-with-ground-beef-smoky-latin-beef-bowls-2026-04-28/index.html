Easy Meal Prep with Ground Beef: Smoky Latin Beef Bowls

Published April 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're looking for an easy meal prep with ground beef that actually tastes like something you'd order at a restaurant, this is your new Sunday ritual. We're seasoning ground beef with cumin, smoked paprika, and a little sofrito magic, then pairing it with rice and black beans for bowls that hold up beautifully all week long. Think of it like a Cuban picadillo's laid-back cousin — fast, filling, and ridiculously good.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef (80/20 works great here)
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, but do it)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups chicken or beef broth (for cooking rice)
  • Fresh cilantro for topping (optional)
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Start your rice first so everything lands at the same time. In a medium saucepan, bring 2 cups of broth to a boil, add your rice with a pinch of salt, stir once, then drop the heat to low, cover, and let it do its thing for 18 minutes. Don't lift that lid.
  2. 2While the rice is going, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until it softens and goes a little translucent — you want some color on it.
  3. 3Add the minced garlic and stir for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Don't walk away here, garlic burns fast and it will betray you.
  4. 4Add the ground beef to the skillet and break it up with a wooden spoon or spatula. Season generously with cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, garlic powder, cayenne if you're using it, salt, and pepper. Cook for 6-8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the beef is fully browned and no pink remains.
  5. 5Pour in the diced tomatoes with their juices and stir everything together. Let it simmer for 3-4 minutes so the flavors marry and the sauce tightens up just slightly. Taste it here and adjust your salt.
  6. 6Warm your black beans in a small pot or microwave with a tiny pinch of cumin and salt — just 2 minutes, nothing fancy.
  7. 7To assemble your meal prep bowls, divide the rice evenly between 4 containers, then spoon the beef mixture over the top, and add a scoop of black beans on the side. Finish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime if you're eating right now. For meal prep, store the lime on the side and add it fresh when you reheat.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to meal prep that doesn't taste sad by Wednesday? Don't overcook your rice on Sunday. Pull it just when it's done and spread it on a sheet pan for 5 minutes to cool before packing — it stays fluffy instead of turning into a brick. And always reheat with a tiny splash of water in the container before microwaving.
