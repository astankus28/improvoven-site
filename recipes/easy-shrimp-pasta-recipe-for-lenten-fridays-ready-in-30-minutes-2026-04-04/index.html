Easy Shrimp Pasta Recipe for Lenten Fridays (Ready in 30 Minutes)

Published April 4, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy shrimp pasta recipe for Lenten Fridays is the one you save when it's 6pm, you skipped meat, and you still want to eat like you actually tried. We're talking garlicky, buttery, lemony shrimp tossed with pasta and a handful of pantry staples — no fancy fish market required, just that bag of frozen shrimp you've been stepping over in the freezer. It's budget-friendly, comes together fast, and honestly might become your Friday night tradition all year long.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb frozen shrimp, thawed, peeled and deveined (medium or large, whatever's on sale)
  • 12 oz spaghetti or linguine
  • 4 tablespoons butter (or olive oil if you're keeping it lighter)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine or chicken broth or reserved pasta water — pick your pantry player
  • Juice of 1 lemon (about 3 tablespoons)
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried in a pinch)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated parmesan, optional but encouraged
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. 1Bring a big pot of heavily salted water to a boil — it should taste like the ocean, trust me. Cook your pasta according to package directions until just al dente. Before you drain it, scoop out a full cup of that starchy pasta water and set it aside. That liquid gold is your backup sauce thickener.
  2. 2While the pasta cooks, pat your thawed shrimp dry with paper towels. This is the step most people skip and then wonder why their shrimp steams instead of sears — dry shrimp equals golden shrimp. Season them with salt, pepper, and the red pepper flakes.
  3. 3Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add the olive oil. Once it shimmers, add the shrimp in a single layer. Cook for about 1 to 2 minutes per side until they're pink and just curled into a loose C shape — a tight C means overcooked. Pull them out and set aside.
  4. 4Turn the heat down to medium and add the butter to the same pan. Once it melts, add the garlic and let it cook for about 60 seconds, stirring constantly so it gets fragrant and golden but not burnt. Burnt garlic is bitter and will haunt your whole dish.
  5. 5Pour in your white wine, broth, or a splash of pasta water and let it bubble up and reduce for about 2 minutes, scraping up any of those browned bits from the bottom of the pan — that's flavor, don't waste it.
  6. 6Add the lemon juice and lemon zest and stir to combine. If the sauce looks thin, add a splash of pasta water and let it simmer another minute to come together.
  7. 7Add the drained pasta directly into the skillet and toss everything together over medium heat. Add another splash of pasta water if needed to loosen it up and coat every strand with that garlic butter sauce.
  8. 8Nestle the shrimp back in, toss gently, and taste for seasoning. Add more salt, a squeeze more lemon, or another pinch of red pepper if it needs it. Finish with fresh parsley and parmesan if you're using it. Serve immediately while it's hot and glossy.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen shrimp is genuinely your friend here — it's often fresher than what's sitting on ice at the counter because it's frozen right after the catch. Just thaw it in a colander under cold running water for about 5 minutes and you're good to go. And if you want to give this a little Miami soul, add a pinch of cumin and a splash of sofrito to the garlic butter step. It'll take this pasta somewhere your guests won't expect and will absolutely ask about.
