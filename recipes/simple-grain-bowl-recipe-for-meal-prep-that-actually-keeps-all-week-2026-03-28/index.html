Simple Grain Bowl Recipe for Meal Prep (That Actually Keeps All Week)

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple grain bowl recipe is the meal prep move that's going to save your whole week — built on pantry staples you probably already have sitting around. We're talking fluffy rice or farro, roasted veggies, a punchy lime-tahini drizzle, and whatever protein you've got on hand. It's the kind of lunch that feels like you really had your life together when you packed it.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain white rice (or farro, brown rice, quinoa — whatever grain you have)
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups frozen corn, thawed
  • 1 large zucchini, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice (about 1 big lime)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced or grated
  • 2-3 tablespoons warm water (to thin the dressing)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional toppings: sliced avocado, fresh cilantro, hot sauce, pickled red onions, crumbled queso fresco

Instructions

  1. 1Cook your grain first so it has time to cool before packing. Rinse your rice and cook it according to the package — 2 cups dry rice to about 3.5 cups water, bring to a boil, cover, and simmer on low for 18 minutes. Fluff with a fork and let it cool uncovered so it doesn't get soggy in your containers.
  2. 2While the rice cooks, preheat your oven to 425°F. Toss the diced zucchini and bell pepper on a sheet pan with 2 tablespoons olive oil, cumin, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread them out in a single layer — don't crowd them or they'll steam instead of roast. Roast for 18 to 20 minutes until they're golden and a little caramelized on the edges.
  3. 3In a small skillet over medium heat, warm the remaining tablespoon of olive oil and add your black beans. Season with a pinch of cumin, salt, and pepper. Let them sit for 2 to 3 minutes without stirring so they get a little crispy on the outside. Give them a stir and let them go another minute. This is not a required step but it is a life-changing one.
  4. 4Make your lime-tahini dressing: whisk together the tahini, lime juice, olive oil, minced garlic, and cumin in a small bowl or jar. Add warm water one tablespoon at a time until it's pourable — the consistency of a creamy salad dressing. Taste it. Add more lime if you want more brightness, more salt if it needs it. This dressing keeps in the fridge for up to a week.
  5. 5Now build your bowls. Divide the cooled rice among four meal prep containers. Top each with a scoop of roasted veggies, a scoop of black beans, and a handful of corn. Store the dressing in a separate small container so everything stays fresh. When you're ready to eat, drizzle the dressing over the top and add any fresh toppings you like.
💡 Improv Tip

The biggest meal prep mistake people make is building their bowls while everything is still hot — that steam is what makes your grains mushy by day three. Let everything cool on the counter for 15 to 20 minutes before you pack the containers. Also, this recipe is a framework, not a rule — swap the black beans for chickpeas, the zucchini for broccoli, or the rice for whatever grain is in your pantry. That's the whole point.
