Easy Mexican Wedding Cookies Recipe (Melt-In-Your-Mouth Pecan Snowballs)

Published April 20, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Mexican wedding cookies recipe is one of those magic pantry bakes that turns a handful of humble ingredients into something that feels downright fancy. You probably already have everything you need sitting in your kitchen right now — butter, flour, powdered sugar, and some nuts. These little snowball cookies are buttery, crumbly, and absolutely addictive, and they come together so fast you'll be rolling them in sugar before you know it.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
18 mins
Total
33 mins
Serves
24 cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar, plus about 1 1/2 cups more for rolling
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup finely chopped pecans (or walnuts if that's what you've got)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F and line two baking sheets with parchment paper. No parchment? Just leave the pans ungreased — these cookies have so much butter in them they won't stick.
  2. 2In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and 1/2 cup powdered sugar together with a hand mixer or a wooden spoon and some elbow grease until it's light and fluffy, about 2-3 minutes. Don't skip this step — creaming the butter properly is what gives these their delicate, melt-in-your-mouth texture.
  3. 3Mix in the vanilla extract, then add the flour and salt. Stir until a soft dough comes together. It'll look crumbly at first — just keep mixing and trust the process. Fold in your finely chopped pecans until evenly distributed throughout the dough.
  4. 4Roll the dough into 1-inch balls — roughly the size of a large grape — and place them about 1 inch apart on your prepared baking sheets. They don't spread much, so no need for a ton of space between them.
  5. 5Bake for 15 to 18 minutes, until the bottoms are just barely golden and the tops look set but still pale. You do NOT want to overbake these — pull them when they look like they might need just one more minute. That's your sweet spot.
  6. 6Let the cookies cool on the pan for just 5 minutes — you want them warm but firm enough to handle. While they're still warm, gently roll each cookie in powdered sugar until fully coated. Set them on a rack to cool completely, then roll them in powdered sugar one more time. That second coat is what gives you that gorgeous snowy finish.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon here is chopping your pecans super fine — almost like a coarse pecan flour. If you have a food processor, pulse them a few times until they're nearly sandy. This is what keeps the cookies from crumbling apart when you roll them and gives that classic smooth snowball shape. No food processor? Just chop them patiently with a knife until they're as fine as you can get them. Worth every minute.
