Easy Baked Cod Recipe for Good Friday (Ready in 30 Minutes)

Published April 3, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy baked cod recipe for Good Friday is the one you're going to make every year — simple enough for a weeknight but special enough for the occasion. We're talking flaky, golden cod with a garlicky herb crust that comes together with stuff you probably already have in your pantry. No fancy skills, no expensive ingredients — just a beautiful fish dish that'll have everyone at the table asking for the recipe.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs cod fillets (fresh or thawed frozen)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional but recommended)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • Lemon slices for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 400°F and line a baking dish or sheet pan with foil or parchment paper. Give it a light drizzle of olive oil so nothing sticks.
  2. 2Pat your cod fillets completely dry with paper towels — this is the move that gets you golden edges instead of steamed fish. Don't skip it.
  3. 3In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, minced garlic, smoked paprika, oregano, cumin, onion powder, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes. You just made a quick marinade that's doing a lot of heavy lifting here.
  4. 4Lay the cod fillets in your prepared baking dish and spoon or brush that garlic-herb mixture all over the top of each fillet. Make sure every piece is well coated.
  5. 5Squeeze the lemon juice over all the fillets, then lay a couple of lemon slices right on top if you have them — they look beautiful and add even more flavor as everything bakes.
  6. 6Slide the dish into the oven and bake for 15 to 20 minutes, depending on the thickness of your fillets. The fish is done when it flakes easily with a fork and turns opaque all the way through. Thinner fillets may only need 12 to 15 minutes, so start checking early.
  7. 7Pull it out, hit it with fresh parsley, and serve immediately with rice, roasted potatoes, or a simple salad. This one disappears fast — fair warning.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen cod is totally your friend here — it's usually cheaper than fresh and works just as beautifully. Just make sure you thaw it in the fridge overnight or run it under cold water in its sealed bag for 30 minutes before cooking. The biggest mistake people make with cod is overbaking it, so keep an eye on it after the 12-minute mark. Flaky and just-opaque is the goal — that's when it's perfect.
