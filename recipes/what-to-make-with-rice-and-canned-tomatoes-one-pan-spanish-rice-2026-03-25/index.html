What to Make with Rice and Canned Tomatoes: One-Pan Spanish Rice

Published March 25, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're staring into your pantry wondering what to make with rice and canned tomatoes, this one-pan Spanish rice is your answer — and it's going to taste like you actually tried. It comes together in about 30 minutes, costs almost nothing, and has that deep, savory tomato flavor that makes people ask you for the recipe.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, with liquid
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 3/4 cups chicken broth or water
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon sazon or 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional but worth it)

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the oil in a wide, heavy-bottomed skillet or saucepan over medium-high heat. Once shimmering, add the diced onion and green bell pepper. Cook for about 3 to 4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and starting to get a little golden around the edges.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant. Don't walk away here — garlic burns fast and bitter garlic will haunt your whole dish.
  3. 3Add the dry rice directly into the pan with the vegetables and stir to coat every grain in that flavored oil. Toast the rice for about 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until it smells slightly nutty and looks a little opaque. This step is the secret to rice that has real flavor and doesn't turn mushy.
  4. 4Pour in the entire can of diced tomatoes — liquid and all — along with the chicken broth or water. Add the cumin, sazon or smoked paprika, and oregano. Stir everything together and taste the liquid. It should be well-seasoned since the rice is going to absorb all of it. Adjust salt and pepper now.
  5. 5Bring the liquid to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pan tightly with a lid, and let it cook undisturbed for 18 minutes. No peeking — you're trapping the steam and that's what cooks the rice through.
  6. 6After 18 minutes, turn off the heat and let the pan sit covered for another 5 minutes. This resting step lets the rice finish steaming and makes it fluffy instead of gummy. Then uncover, fluff with a fork, top with fresh cilantro if you have it, and serve hot.
💡 Improv Tip

The toasting step is non-negotiable if you want restaurant-quality results — when you toast that raw rice in the oil before adding any liquid, you're building a flavor layer that plain boiled rice never gets. And if you want to turn this into a full meal, throw in a can of drained black beans or some sliced smoked sausage right before you add the tomatoes. Refrigerator blindness, cured.
