Easy Lenten Fish Recipe for Good Friday Breakfast: Bacalao Hash with Eggs

Published March 29, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you need an easy Lenten fish recipe for Good Friday that actually feels like a real breakfast and not just a sacrifice, this bacalao hash with crispy potatoes and runny eggs is your answer. Salted cod is a staple in so many Miami kitchens — Cuban, Haitian, Dominican — and it stretches beautifully into a hearty morning meal for just a few dollars. This one-skillet situation is done in 30 minutes and will have everyone at the table asking why we don't eat this every weekend.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb dried salted cod (bacalao), soaked overnight or quick-soaked in hot water for 1 hour, drained and flaked
  • 3 medium Yukon Gold or russet potatoes, diced small (about 1/2 inch)
  • 1/2 medium white or yellow onion, diced
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 Roma tomato, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional but recommended)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped
  • Hot sauce for serving

Instructions

  1. 1If you haven't soaked your bacalao overnight, place the flaked fish in a bowl and cover with very hot water. Let it sit for at least 45 minutes to 1 hour, changing the water once halfway through. Taste a small piece — it should be pleasantly salty but not overwhelming. Drain well and use your fingers to break it into small flakes, removing any bones or skin you find.
  2. 2Heat your largest skillet (cast iron is perfect here) over medium-high heat and add the oil. Once the oil is shimmering, add the diced potatoes in a single layer. Don't touch them for about 4 to 5 minutes — you want them to get a golden crust on the bottom. Then stir and let them cook another 4 to 5 minutes until they're cooked through and crispy on most sides. Season with just a tiny pinch of salt since the bacalao brings plenty.
  3. 3Push the potatoes to the edges of the pan and add the onion and green pepper to the center. Cook for 3 minutes until softened, then stir everything together. Add the garlic and tomato and cook for another 2 minutes until the tomato breaks down a little.
  4. 4Add the flaked bacalao to the skillet along with the smoked paprika, black pepper, and red pepper flakes if using. Stir everything together and let it cook for 2 to 3 minutes so the fish gets a little color and all the flavors come together. Taste here and adjust salt only if needed.
  5. 5Create 4 small wells in the hash using a spoon. Crack one egg into each well. Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover the skillet with a lid or a sheet of foil, and cook for 3 to 5 minutes depending on how you like your yolks. Three minutes gives you runny yolks, five minutes gives you fully set. Watch them closely because they go fast.
  6. 6Remove from heat, scatter the fresh cilantro or parsley all over the top, and bring the whole skillet straight to the table. Serve with hot sauce on the side and some crusty bread or warm tortillas if you have them.
💡 Improv Tip

The biggest mistake people make with bacalao is not soaking it long enough — overnight in the fridge is always your best bet. But if it's Good Friday morning and you forgot, do two rounds of hot water soaking back to back, about 30 minutes each, and taste between rounds. You want the fish flavorful, not mouth-puckering salty. And here's a little bonus move: if you have any leftover sofrito in the fridge, stir in a tablespoon when you add the garlic. That's it. Game over.
