Easy Arroz con Leche Recipe (Just Like Abuela Made It)

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy arroz con leche recipe is the kind of comfort dessert that makes you feel like someone loves you — creamy, cinnamon-kissed, and done in about 30 minutes with stuff you probably already have in your pantry. It's a classic across Latin America and the Caribbean, and Miami kitchens have been making it forever for good reason. Whether you serve it warm on a rainy night or chilled straight from the fridge, this one never misses.

Prep
5 mins
Cook
30 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Ground cinnamon for topping
  • Lemon or lime zest (optional but highly recommended)

Instructions

  1. 1Add the rice, water, cinnamon stick, and salt to a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to a simmer. Cook uncovered, stirring occasionally, until most of the water has been absorbed — about 10 minutes. The rice will not be fully cooked yet and that is totally fine, it is going to finish in the milk.
  2. 2Pour in the whole milk and evaporated milk. Stir everything together and bring the heat back up to medium. You want a gentle, bubbly simmer — not a rolling boil. Stir frequently so nothing sticks to the bottom of the pan, because the second you walk away is the second it burns. Ask me how I know.
  3. 3Cook for about 15 to 18 minutes, stirring often, until the mixture thickens to a creamy, porridge-like consistency. It will look a little loose but remember it thickens more as it cools. Remove the cinnamon stick.
  4. 4Turn the heat to low and stir in the sweetened condensed milk and vanilla extract. Taste it right here — this is your moment to adjust sweetness. Want it sweeter? Add a little more condensed milk. Want a citrus pop? Stir in a pinch of lemon or lime zest now.
  5. 5Serve warm in bowls or cups with a generous dusting of ground cinnamon on top. If you prefer it cold, pour it into a container and refrigerate for at least 2 hours. It will thicken up beautifully in the fridge and honestly tastes even better the next day.
💡 Improv Tip

The biggest arroz con leche mistake people make is leaving it too thick on the stove — pull it off the heat when it still looks a little loose and saucy, because it will keep thickening as it sits and cools. If you reheat leftovers and they are too thick, just splash in a little regular milk and stir it back to life.
