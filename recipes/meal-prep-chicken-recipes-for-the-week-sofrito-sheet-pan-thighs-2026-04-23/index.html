Meal Prep Chicken Recipes for the Week: Sofrito Sheet Pan Thighs

Published April 23, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you've been searching for meal prep chicken recipes for the week that don't taste like sad gym food, this is your answer. We're talking juicy bone-in thighs roasted in a garlicky sofrito marinade that perfumes your whole kitchen and holds up beautifully in the fridge for days. Make a big tray on Sunday and you've got lunches, dinners, and rice bowls locked in before Monday even starts.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
40 mins
Total
50 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 6 bone-in skin-on chicken thighs (about 3 lbs)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup sofrito (jarred is totally fine)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon sazón seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 large onion, sliced into rings
  • 1 bell pepper (any color), sliced

Instructions

  1. 1Pat the chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels — this is the move for crispy skin, don't skip it.
  2. 2In a large bowl, mix together the sofrito, minced garlic, olive oil, sazón, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, black pepper, and lime juice until you have a thick, fragrant marinade.
  3. 3Add the chicken thighs to the bowl and toss to coat every inch. If you have time, let them marinate in the fridge for at least 30 minutes — overnight is even better, but honestly they're great going straight to the oven too.
  4. 4Preheat your oven to 425°F. Scatter the sliced onion and bell pepper across a large rimmed sheet pan in an even layer — this is your built-in side dish right here.
  5. 5Nestle the marinated chicken thighs skin-side up on top of the vegetables. Every bit of leftover marinade in that bowl? Scrape it right over everything.
  6. 6Roast for 38 to 42 minutes until the skin is deep golden and a little charred at the edges and an instant-read thermometer reads 165°F at the thickest part. If the skin isn't as crispy as you want at the end, flip the broiler on high for 2 to 3 minutes and watch it close.
  7. 7Let the chicken rest on the pan for 5 minutes before dividing into meal prep containers. Spoon those jammy caramelized vegetables over each portion — they're half the magic.
💡 Improv Tip

These thighs are your blank canvas for the whole week — serve them over white rice with black beans on Monday, shred the leftovers into a quesadilla Tuesday, chop them over a salad Wednesday. Bone-in thighs are the move here over breasts because they stay moist and flavorful even on day four when you're pulling them cold out of the fridge at lunch.
