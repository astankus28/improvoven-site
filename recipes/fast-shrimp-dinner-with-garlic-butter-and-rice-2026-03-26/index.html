Fast Shrimp Dinner with Garlic Butter and Rice

Published March 26, 2026 · Improv Oven

When you need a fast shrimp recipe for dinner that actually tastes like you put in real effort, this is your go-to. We're talking garlicky, buttery shrimp over fluffy white rice — all done in about 20 minutes with stuff you probably already have. It's the kind of meal that makes people think you've been cooking for hours when really you were watching TV until 6pm.

Prep
5 mins
Cook
15 mins
Total
20 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb frozen shrimp, peeled and deveined (thawed)
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes (optional but do it)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice, cooked
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for topping
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. 1If your shrimp are frozen, run them under cold water in a colander for about 5 minutes until thawed. Pat them completely dry with paper towels — this is not optional, wet shrimp will steam instead of sear and you'll lose all that gorgeous color.
  2. 2Get your rice going first if you haven't already. Regular white rice takes about 15 minutes, so start it right when you walk in the kitchen and let it do its thing in the background.
  3. 3Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil and let it get hot — about 30 seconds. You want that pan talking to you when things hit it.
  4. 4Add the butter and let it melt into the oil. Once it starts to foam, add your minced garlic and stir it around for about 60 seconds until it smells incredible. Don't walk away — garlic goes from golden to burnt in seconds.
  5. 5Season your shrimp with cumin, smoked paprika, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes right in the pan. Toss everything together so every shrimp gets coated in that spiced garlic butter.
  6. 6Spread the shrimp into a single layer and cook for 2 minutes without touching them. Flip each one and cook another 1 to 2 minutes on the other side. They're done when they're pink, opaque, and curl into a loose C shape. If they curl into a tight O, you've gone a little too far but it's still delicious — no stress.
  7. 7Squeeze the lime juice over everything right at the end and give it one final toss. That acid is going to wake up every single flavor in the pan.
  8. 8Spoon your rice into bowls, pile the shrimp and all that garlic butter sauce right on top, and finish with fresh cilantro and an extra lime wedge on the side. Dinner is done.
💡 Improv Tip

The move that levels this up from good to great is browning the butter just slightly before you add the garlic — let it go a few extra seconds until it smells a little nutty. That toasty butter flavor with the garlic and lime is honestly dangerous. Also, frozen shrimp from a bag is totally what we're using here — fresh shrimp at the seafood counter is beautiful but a bag of frozen gets the job done on a Tuesday night without any drama.
