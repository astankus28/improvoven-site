Quick Weeknight Pasta with Garlic, Olive Oil, and Whatever You've Got

Published March 25, 2026 · Improv Oven

This quick weeknight pasta recipe is the one I make when it's 7pm, nobody's decided what's for dinner, and I need something on the table fast. It starts with the holy trinity of pantry cooking — garlic, olive oil, and pasta — and from there you build it out with whatever's hanging around. It's simple, it's satisfying, and honestly? It tastes like you planned it all along.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb spaghetti or any long pasta you have
  • 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (more if you like heat)
  • 1/2 cup pasta water, reserved before draining
  • 1 can (15 oz) white beans or chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 big handfuls fresh spinach or whatever leafy green is in your fridge
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Parmesan or cotija cheese for topping
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for finishing

Instructions

  1. 1Bring a big pot of water to a boil. Salt it like you mean it — it should taste like the ocean. Drop in your pasta and cook it according to the package, but pull it out one minute before it says done. You want it just shy of al dente because it's going to finish cooking in the pan.
  2. 2Before you drain the pasta, scoop out about half a cup of that starchy pasta water and set it aside. This stuff is liquid gold — it's what makes your sauce silky instead of greasy.
  3. 3While the pasta cooks, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-low heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes. Let them slowly sizzle and get golden and fragrant, about 3 to 4 minutes. Watch it closely — garlic goes from golden to burnt in seconds and burnt garlic is bitter and sad.
  4. 4Add the drained beans to the skillet and stir them around to coat in all that garlicky oil. Let them warm through and pick up a little color, about 2 minutes.
  5. 5Add the spinach or greens right into the pan and toss until wilted. Season with salt and black pepper.
  6. 6Add the drained pasta directly into the skillet. Pour in a splash of that reserved pasta water and toss everything together over medium heat. Keep adding pasta water a little at a time until you've got a light, glossy sauce that clings to every strand. Squeeze in the lemon juice and toss again.
  7. 7Taste it. Adjust salt, pepper, and lemon. Plate it up, hit it with a shower of parmesan or cotija, and scatter some fresh parsley or cilantro on top. Get it on the table while it's hot.
💡 Improv Tip

The pasta water is not optional, mija. That starchy water is what transforms a greasy pile of noodles into something that actually tastes like a restaurant made it. Set a coffee mug right next to the pot before you drain so you never forget to grab it — once it's down the drain, it's gone forever and your sauce will miss it.
