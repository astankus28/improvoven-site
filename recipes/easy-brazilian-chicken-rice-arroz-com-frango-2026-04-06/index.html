Easy Brazilian Chicken Rice (Arroz com Frango)

Published April 6, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Brazilian chicken rice recipe is the kind of one-pot comfort food that'll make your whole kitchen smell incredible — think turmeric-golden rice, tender shredded chicken, and sofrito-style aromatics all cooked together until everything's best friends. It's inspired by arroz com frango, a classic you'll find on tables from São Paulo to your abuela's kitchen in Miami. Budget-friendly, weeknight-approved, and way easier than it looks.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
30 mins
Total
40 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1.5 cups long-grain white rice
  • 3 cups chicken broth (or water with 1 bouillon cube)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 roma tomatoes, diced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas (optional but recommended)
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1Season your chicken thighs on both sides with salt, pepper, paprika, and cumin. Don't be shy — this is where the flavor starts.
  2. 2Heat olive oil in a large deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the chicken thighs for about 4 minutes per side until golden brown. They don't need to be fully cooked through yet. Remove them from the pan and set aside.
  3. 3In the same pan with all those beautiful drippings, drop in your onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Cook for 3-4 minutes until softened and fragrant, scraping up any brown bits from the bottom — that's pure flavor right there.
  4. 4Add the diced tomatoes and turmeric, stir everything together, and cook another 2 minutes until the tomatoes start breaking down and the pan turns that gorgeous golden color.
  5. 5Add the dry rice directly into the pan and stir it around for about 1 minute so every grain gets coated in all that seasoned oil. This step is the secret to non-mushy rice — toast it first.
  6. 6Pour in the chicken broth and stir to combine. Nestle the seared chicken thighs right on top of the rice. Bring everything to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover tightly, and let it cook for 20 minutes without lifting the lid.
  7. 7After 20 minutes, check that the liquid is absorbed and the rice is tender. Remove the chicken thighs and use two forks to shred them right on a cutting board — they should fall apart easily.
  8. 8Fold the shredded chicken and frozen peas back into the rice, replace the lid, and let it sit off the heat for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork, taste for salt, garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro, and serve straight from the pan.
💡 Improv Tip

The biggest arroz com frango mistake people make is lifting the lid while the rice cooks — just trust the process and walk away. If you want to stretch this even further, add one diced carrot with the onions and throw in a handful of sliced olives at the end. Very Brazilian, very delicious, costs almost nothing extra.
