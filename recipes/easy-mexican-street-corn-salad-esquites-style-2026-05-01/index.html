Easy Mexican Street Corn Salad (Esquites Style)

Published May 1, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Mexican street corn salad recipe is basically elote in a bowl — all that smoky, creamy, tangy goodness without needing to hover over a grill. It comes together in about 30 minutes with ingredients you probably already have, and honestly? It slaps at cookouts, weeknight dinners, or straight out of the bowl standing at the counter.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 4 cups corn kernels (fresh off the cob, frozen, or canned — all work)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or olive oil
  • 1/3 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/3 cup sour cream (or plain Greek yogurt if that's what you've got)
  • 1/2 cup cotija cheese, crumbled (feta works great as a sub)
  • 1 lime, juiced (plus more to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, adjust to your heat level)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 3 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Extra chili powder and cotija for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1Get a large skillet — cast iron if you have it — over medium-high heat. Add your butter or olive oil and let it get hot. You want it almost shimmering.
  2. 2Add the corn in a single layer as best you can and just leave it alone for 2-3 minutes. Resist the urge to stir. You want real char on that corn — those dark spots are where the flavor lives. Then stir, let it sit again, and repeat until most of the corn has some color. Total char time is about 8-10 minutes. If you're using canned corn, pat it dry first or it'll steam instead of char.
  3. 3Pull the skillet off the heat and let the corn cool for about 5 minutes. You don't want it melting your dressing.
  4. 4While the corn cools, whisk together the mayo, sour cream, lime juice, chili powder, smoked paprika, cayenne, and garlic powder in a large bowl. Taste it — it should be tangy, a little smoky, and have some kick. Adjust lime or seasoning as needed.
  5. 5Add the charred corn to the bowl with the dressing and toss it all together. Then fold in most of the cotija, the cilantro, and the green onions. Save a little of each for the top.
  6. 6Taste and adjust — more lime, more salt, more chili powder, whatever it needs. Then pile it into a serving bowl and garnish with the reserved cotija, a fresh sprinkle of chili powder, and the rest of the cilantro and green onions.
  7. 7Serve warm, at room temperature, or cold straight from the fridge the next day — it honestly gets better as it sits.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen corn is your best friend here and I will die on that hill. It's already cut, it's cheap year-round, and it chars beautifully in a hot skillet. Just don't skip the drying step — spread it on a paper towel for a minute before it hits the pan and you'll get that gorgeous char instead of a sad steam situation.
