Easy Homemade Waffle Recipe (Crispy Outside, Fluffy Inside)

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy homemade waffle recipe is the one you'll come back to every weekend — no fancy ingredients, no stress, just golden waffles that come out crispy on the outside and cloud-soft in the middle. I learned early on that the secret isn't some special mix from the store, it's just knowing what's already in your pantry. Mira, once you make these yourself, you're never going back to the box.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups whole milk (or whatever milk you have)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil or melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Non-stick cooking spray or butter for the waffle iron

Instructions

  1. 1Plug in your waffle iron and let it preheat while you mix the batter — this is key, you want it fully hot before the batter touches it or you'll get sad, pale waffles.
  2. 2In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt until combined. Make a little well in the center of your dry ingredients.
  3. 3In a separate bowl or large measuring cup, whisk together the eggs, milk, oil (or melted butter), and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. 4Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir gently with a spatula or wooden spoon until just combined. Stop when you no longer see dry flour — lumps are totally fine and actually good here. Overmixing is how you end up with tough, chewy waffles, and we're not doing that.
  5. 5Let the batter rest for 3 to 5 minutes while the iron finishes heating. This short rest lets the baking powder activate and gives you a fluffier waffle.
  6. 6Lightly grease your waffle iron with cooking spray or a little butter, then pour in enough batter to fill the iron about three-quarters full (check your iron's instructions since they all differ a little). Close the lid and cook for 4 to 5 minutes, or until the steam slows way down and the waffle is deep golden brown.
  7. 7Carefully remove the waffle and serve immediately, or place it directly on the rack in an oven set to 200°F to keep it warm and crispy while you finish the batch. Never stack hot waffles — they'll steam each other and go soft.
  8. 8Serve with maple syrup, fresh fruit, whipped cream, or go full Miami and drizzle with a little sweetened condensed milk. You earned it.
💡 Improv Tip

The single best thing you can do for crispy waffles is trust the iron — don't lift the lid early to check. Every time you peek before the steam dies down, you're ripping the waffle apart before it's set. Wait until the steam coming out the sides slows to almost nothing, then check. That patience right there is what separates a good waffle from a great one.
