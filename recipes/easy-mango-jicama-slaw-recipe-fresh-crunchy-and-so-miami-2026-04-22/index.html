Easy Mango Jicama Slaw Recipe (Fresh, Crunchy, and So Miami)

Published April 22, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy mango jicama slaw recipe is the kind of thing you throw together in ten minutes and people think you spent all day on it — that's the magic. Sweet mango, crispy jicama, a little heat, a little lime... it's basically summer in a bowl. Serve it as a side, pile it on tacos, or eat it straight from the bowl standing at the counter. No judgment here.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
0 mins
Total
15 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 small jicama (about 1 lb), peeled and cut into thin matchsticks
  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and cut into thin matchsticks or small chunks
  • 1/2 small red cabbage, thinly shredded (about 2 cups)
  • 1/2 small red onion, very thinly sliced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and finely minced (leave seeds in if you like heat)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves, roughly chopped
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 2 limes)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or neutral oil
  • 1 teaspoon honey or agave
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Optional: 1/4 teaspoon chili powder or Tajín for extra kick

Instructions

  1. 1Peel your jicama and cut it into thin matchstick strips about 2 inches long. Jicama skin is thick so use a sturdy peeler or a knife — once you get past the skin, the inside is easy to work with. Don't skip this step or rush it, the thin cut is what makes the texture so satisfying.
  2. 2Cut your mangoes into similar-sized matchsticks. If your mangoes are very ripe and soft, slightly larger chunks work fine — they'll still taste incredible. The goal is pieces that eat well in one bite.
  3. 3Shred your red cabbage thin. A sharp knife works great here. You want ribbons, not chunks. The cabbage adds crunch, color, and makes the whole slaw feel more substantial.
  4. 4Slice the red onion as thin as you can — almost translucent. If raw onion is too sharp for your taste, soak the slices in cold water for 5 minutes then drain. Takes the bite down without losing the flavor.
  5. 5Make your dressing right in the bottom of your serving bowl: whisk together the lime juice, oil, honey, salt, pepper, and chili powder or Tajín if using. Taste it — it should be bright, a little sweet, a little tangy. Adjust as needed.
  6. 6Add everything into the bowl — jicama, mango, cabbage, red onion, jalapeño, and cilantro. Toss well to coat everything in the dressing. Let it sit for 5 minutes before serving so the flavors can get to know each other.
  7. 7Taste one more time and adjust salt or lime. Serve immediately for maximum crunch, or refrigerate up to a few hours. It's great next to grilled fish, piled on tacos, or alongside anything that just needs a little brightness on the plate.
💡 Improv Tip

Jicama doesn't brown or get soggy like regular cabbage slaws do, which means this holds up beautifully for a few hours in the fridge — actually making it perfect for meal prep or bringing somewhere. If you can't find jicama, green apple cut into matchsticks gives you a similar crunch with a little more sweetness. Not the same, but absolutely delicious in its own way.
