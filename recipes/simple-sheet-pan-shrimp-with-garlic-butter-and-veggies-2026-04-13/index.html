Simple Sheet Pan Shrimp with Garlic Butter and Veggies

Published April 13, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple sheet pan shrimp recipe is the kind of weeknight move that makes you look like you actually have it together — one pan, 30 minutes, done. We're tossing shrimp with whatever vegetables you've got hanging around, a little garlic butter, and some pantry spices that bring real flavor without the fuss. Very Miami, very easy, very delicious.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
12 mins
Total
22 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined (fresh or frozen-thawed)
  • 2 cups broccoli florets (or whatever veggie you have)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  • 4 tablespoons butter, melted (or olive oil)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (optional but recommended)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish
  • Cooked white rice or crusty bread for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 425°F and line a large sheet pan with foil or parchment. You want the oven hot — that's what gives the shrimp that nice little char on the edges instead of steaming them sad.
  2. 2Pat your shrimp completely dry with paper towels. This step sounds extra but it matters — dry shrimp roast, wet shrimp steam. Big difference in texture.
  3. 3In a large bowl, whisk together the melted butter, minced garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, onion powder, chili flakes, a good pinch of salt, and black pepper.
  4. 4Add your vegetables to the bowl first and toss them well in that garlic butter situation. Spread them out on the sheet pan in a single layer — give them room to breathe so they actually roast.
  5. 5Roast the vegetables alone for 8 minutes. Shrimp cook much faster than veggies, so we give the veggies a head start or everything ends up mushy.
  6. 6Pull the pan out, push the veggies to the sides, and add the shrimp to the center of the pan in a single layer. Drizzle any remaining butter mixture over the shrimp.
  7. 7Return the pan to the oven and roast for 6 to 8 more minutes, until the shrimp are pink and just curled. The moment they curl into a tight C, they're done — don't let them go past that or they'll get rubbery.
  8. 8Pull the pan out, squeeze that lime juice all over everything right away, and hit it with fresh cilantro or parsley. Serve immediately over white rice or with crusty bread to soak up all that garlic butter on the pan.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen shrimp is your best friend here — it's almost always cheaper than fresh at the seafood counter and the quality is honestly just as good. Just make sure you thaw them completely in cold water and dry them really well before they hit the pan. Also, don't crowd that sheet pan. If you pile everything on top of each other, you're making steamed shrimp soup, not roasted shrimp. Use two pans if you have to.
