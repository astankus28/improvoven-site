Easy Homemade Horchata Recipe (Creamy, Cold, and So Good)

Published April 15, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy homemade horchata recipe is the one you make once and never buy the store version again — I promise. Creamy, cinnamon-kissed, perfectly sweet, and ridiculously simple to pull off with ingredients you probably already have. It's the drink that shows up at every Latin table for a reason, and now it's showing up in your fridge too.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
0 mins
Total
4 hrs 10 mins (mostly hands-off soaking time)
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice (uncooked)
  • 4 cups water (for soaking)
  • 2 cups cold water (for blending)
  • 2 cups whole milk or any milk you have
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cinnamon stick (optional but worth it)
  • Ice, for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Add the dry white rice and the cinnamon stick (if using) to a bowl or large jar. Pour in the 4 cups of water, give it a little stir, cover it, and let it soak in the fridge for at least 4 hours — overnight is even better. This is where all the magic flavor starts, so don't skip the soak.
  2. 2Once the rice has soaked, drain off and discard the soaking water. Add the soaked rice to your blender along with the 2 cups of fresh cold water. Blend on high for about 2 full minutes until the mixture looks milky and smooth. Don't rush this step — you want to really break down that rice.
  3. 3Set a fine mesh strainer or a cheesecloth over a large pitcher or bowl and slowly pour the blended rice mixture through it. Press or squeeze out as much liquid as you can, then discard the rice solids left behind. This straining step is what gives you that silky smooth texture.
  4. 4To the strained rice water in your pitcher, add the milk, sugar, ground cinnamon, and vanilla extract. Stir everything together really well until the sugar is fully dissolved. Taste it — this is your moment to adjust sweetness or cinnamon to make it yours.
  5. 5Pour your horchata over a big glass full of ice and serve immediately, or keep it in the fridge in a sealed pitcher for up to 3 days. Give it a good stir or shake before each pour because it will naturally settle — that's totally normal and means you made the real thing.
💡 Improv Tip

If your blender isn't super powerful, let the rice soak a full 8 hours — the softer it gets, the more flavor and creaminess you'll pull out during blending. Also, don't sleep on using evaporated milk in place of regular milk if you want that rich, slightly sweet depth that reminds you of the horchata from a good taqueria.
