Simple Stuffed Cabbage Lazy Skillet

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple stuffed cabbage lazy skillet gives you every single flavor you love about the classic — tender cabbage, savory meat, tomato sauce, rice — all cooked in one pan without any of the rolling, stuffing, or drama. It's the kind of recipe your abuela would call 'taking shortcuts' but your taste buds would call 'genius.' Perfect for a weeknight when the fridge is looking rough but dinner still needs to happen.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef or ground turkey
  • 1 small head of green cabbage, roughly chopped (about 6 cups)
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice, uncooked
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 cup beef or chicken broth
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large deep skillet or wide pot over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for about 3 minutes until it starts to soften and go a little translucent around the edges.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds, just until fragrant — don't let it burn. Then add the ground beef or turkey, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Season with the cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook until the meat is browned and no longer pink, about 5-6 minutes. Drain any excess fat if needed.
  3. 3Add the chopped cabbage right into the skillet and stir everything together. Let it cook for about 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cabbage starts to wilt down and make room in the pan.
  4. 4Pour in the uncooked rice, diced tomatoes (with their juices), tomato sauce, and broth. Stir everything together until combined and bring it up to a boil.
  5. 5Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet tightly with a lid, and let it cook for 18-20 minutes — or until the rice has absorbed all the liquid and is fully cooked through. Don't lift that lid early or you'll let the steam escape and the rice won't cook evenly.
  6. 6Remove the lid, give everything a good stir, and taste for seasoning. Add salt and pepper if needed. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro and serve straight from the skillet. Boom — dinner is done.
💡 Improv Tip

The cabbage is your best friend here — don't be scared if it looks like too much when you first add it to the pan. It wilts down dramatically within a few minutes and practically disappears into the dish, just like in traditional stuffed cabbage. If your skillet lid doesn't fit tight, lay a sheet of foil over the pan first and then press the lid on top. That seal keeps the steam locked in and gets your rice perfectly fluffy every time.
