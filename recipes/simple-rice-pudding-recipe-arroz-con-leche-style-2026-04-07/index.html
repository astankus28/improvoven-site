Simple Rice Pudding Recipe (Arroz con Leche Style)

Published April 7, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple rice pudding recipe is the kind of dessert that makes people think you spent all day in the kitchen — but we both know better. It's creamy, warmly spiced, and comes together with basic pantry stuff you probably already have sitting around. Think of it as arroz con leche's easygoing cousin who shows up, does the job, and leaves everyone happy.

Prep
5 mins
Cook
30 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 3 cups whole milk (or whatever milk you have)
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (plus more for topping)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Zest of half a lime (optional but trust me on this)

Instructions

  1. 1Rinse your rice under cold water until it runs mostly clear — this keeps the pudding from getting gluey and gives you that silky, creamy texture you're going for.
  2. 2Add the rinsed rice, water, cinnamon stick, and a pinch of salt to a medium saucepan over medium heat. Bring it to a gentle boil, then reduce to low and let it simmer uncovered until most of the water is absorbed, about 10 minutes. Stir occasionally so nothing sticks.
  3. 3Once the water is mostly gone, pour in the whole milk and sweetened condensed milk. Stir everything together and bring it back up to a low simmer over medium-low heat. This is where the magic happens — don't rush it and don't crank the heat.
  4. 4Cook uncovered, stirring frequently, for about 18 to 22 minutes. You want it to thicken up nicely but still look a little loose — it will continue to thicken as it cools. If it looks too thick, splash in a little more milk.
  5. 5Stir in the sugar, vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, and lime zest if you're using it. Taste and adjust — more sugar, more cinnamon, you're in charge here.
  6. 6Fish out the cinnamon stick and remove from heat. Spoon into bowls or cups and dust generously with ground cinnamon on top. Serve warm for a cozy vibe or refrigerate for a couple hours if you like it cold and thick — both versions are completely valid life choices.
💡 Improv Tip

Whole milk is the move here for maximum creaminess, but if you only have 2% or even oat milk, it still works — just cook it a few minutes longer since lower-fat milks take more time to thicken up. And that lime zest? It sounds weird but it cuts through the sweetness just enough to make people ask what your secret ingredient is. You don't have to tell them.
