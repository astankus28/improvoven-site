Simple Brownies Recipe From Scratch (No Box Needed)

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple brownies recipe from scratch uses nothing but pantry staples you probably already have sitting around — no fancy chocolate, no mixer, no drama. We're talking fudgy, crackly-top brownies that taste like you actually know what you're doing in the kitchen. One bowl, one pan, and about 30 minutes is all it takes.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
16 brownies

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • Optional: 1/2 cup chocolate chips or chopped walnuts

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease an 8x8 inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper — either works, just don't skip this step or you'll be sad later.
  2. 2Melt the butter in a medium saucepan over low heat or in a large microwave-safe bowl in 30-second bursts. Let it cool for just a minute so you don't scramble your eggs when they go in.
  3. 3Add the sugar to the melted butter and stir it together until combined. It'll look a little grainy — that's totally normal, don't stress it.
  4. 4Crack in both eggs and add the vanilla. Stir well until the mixture looks smooth and glossy, almost like it's coming together into something real. That's because it is.
  5. 5Add the cocoa powder, flour, salt, and baking powder directly into the same bowl. Stir until just combined — you want to stop as soon as you don't see dry streaks anymore. Overmixing is how you end up with cakey brownies instead of fudgy ones, and we are not about that life.
  6. 6Fold in chocolate chips or walnuts if you're using them. Pour the batter into your prepared pan and spread it into an even layer.
  7. 7Bake for 20 to 25 minutes. Start checking at 20 — a toothpick inserted in the center should come out with just a few moist crumbs, not wet batter. Pull them when they're barely done. They firm up as they cool.
  8. 8Let the brownies cool in the pan for at least 15 minutes before cutting. I know it's hard. But cutting them too soon means they fall apart on you. Patience is an ingredient too.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to that beautiful crackly top? Stir the sugar and butter really well before adding the eggs — that friction is what creates it. And if you only have salted butter, skip the added salt in the recipe. Brownies are forgiving like that.
