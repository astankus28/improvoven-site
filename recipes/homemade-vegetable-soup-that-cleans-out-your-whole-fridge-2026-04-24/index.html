Homemade Vegetable Soup That Cleans Out Your Whole Fridge

Published April 24, 2026 · Improv Oven

This homemade vegetable soup recipe is basically the cure for that moment you open the fridge and see a sad half-onion, two carrots, and whatever else has been hanging around all week. We're throwing it all in one pot with some pantry staples and turning it into something genuinely comforting and delicious. Miami winter or not, a bowl of this hits different at lunch.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 medium carrots, sliced into coins
  • 3 stalks celery, sliced
  • 2 medium potatoes, cut into bite-sized cubes
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) cannellini beans or kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup green beans, fresh or frozen, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 6 cups vegetable broth (or water with 2 bouillon cubes)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of half a lime
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for about 3-4 minutes until it softens and starts to look a little translucent. Add the garlic and stir for another minute — your kitchen should already smell amazing.
  2. 2Toss in the carrots and celery and let them cook with the onion for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. This little sauté step builds flavor that you just cannot get if you dump everything in cold.
  3. 3Add the cubed potatoes, canned diced tomatoes (juice and all), cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, and the bay leaf. Stir everything together so the spices coat the vegetables.
  4. 4Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the pot to a boil over medium-high heat. Once it's boiling, reduce the heat to medium-low, add the drained beans and green beans, and let everything simmer uncovered for 15-18 minutes until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  5. 5Fish out the bay leaf and discard it. Squeeze the lime juice right into the pot and stir — this is the move that wakes the whole soup up and makes it taste like you actually know what you're doing. Taste and adjust salt and pepper as needed.
  6. 6Ladle into bowls and top with fresh cilantro or parsley. Serve with crusty bread, a warm tortilla, or just eat it straight from the bowl while standing at the counter — no judgment here.
💡 Improv Tip

The lime juice at the end is non-negotiable — acid is what separates a flat, forgettable soup from one people ask you for the recipe on. Same idea works with a splash of apple cider vinegar if you're out of limes. Also, this soup is even better the next day once everything gets friendly in the fridge, so make a big batch and thank yourself at lunch tomorrow.
