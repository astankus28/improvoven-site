Easy Homemade Coleslaw Recipe (The One You'll Make Every Week)

Published April 8, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy homemade coleslaw recipe is the kind of thing that turns a simple weeknight dinner into something people actually talk about. We're doing a creamy, tangy slaw loaded with Caribbean-inspired flavor — think a little lime, a little heat, and that bright freshness that makes everything on the plate pop. Pile it on top of crispy fried fish, pulled pork, or jerk chicken and you've got a full dinner that costs almost nothing to make.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
0 mins
Total
15 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 small head green cabbage, thinly shredded (about 6 cups)
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and grated
  • 1/2 small red onion, very thinly sliced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced (optional but recommended)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 1 lime)
  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 4 tilapia fillets or 1 lb boneless chicken thighs, for serving
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon jerk seasoning or all-purpose seasoning, for the protein

Instructions

  1. 1Start with the slaw so the flavors have time to come together while you cook the protein. Shred your cabbage as thin as you can — a sharp knife works fine, no fancy tools needed. Grate the carrot, slice the red onion paper-thin, and toss everything into a big bowl along with the jalapeño and cilantro.
  2. 2In a separate small bowl, whisk together the mayo, apple cider vinegar, lime juice, honey, garlic powder, cumin, salt, pepper, and cayenne if you're using it. Taste the dressing before it hits the slaw — it should be creamy, tangy, and just a little sweet with some heat on the back end. Adjust the salt or lime to your liking.
  3. 3Pour the dressing over the cabbage mixture and toss everything really well until every strand of cabbage is coated. Cover the bowl and let it sit in the fridge for at least 10 minutes while you cook the protein. The cabbage will soften slightly and the flavors will deepen — don't skip this rest time.
  4. 4Season your tilapia fillets or chicken thighs generously with jerk seasoning or all-purpose seasoning on both sides. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. For tilapia, cook 3 to 4 minutes per side until golden and flaky. For chicken thighs, cook 6 to 7 minutes per side until cooked through and nicely charred on the edges.
  5. 5Pull the slaw out of the fridge and give it one more toss. Plate the fish or chicken and pile a generous heap of slaw right on top or alongside it. Finish with an extra squeeze of lime and a few cilantro leaves if you have them. Dinner is done and it looks like you actually tried.
💡 Improv Tip

If your cabbage releases a lot of water after sitting, don't panic — just drain a little of the liquid off before serving and give it a fresh toss. And listen, this slaw only gets better the next day, so make a double batch and use the leftovers in tacos, on sandwiches, or straight out of the container standing in front of the fridge at midnight. No judgment here.
