Budget Sopa de Fideo: Mexican Noodle Soup Ready in 30 Minutes

Published April 11, 2026 · Improv Oven

This budget sopa de fideo — the classic Mexican noodle soup — is one of those dishes that punches way above its price tag, built almost entirely from pantry staples you already have sitting around. It's toasty, tomato-rich, and deeply comforting in a way that'll make you wonder why you ever paid for takeout. Think of it as your midday reset button, ready in 30 minutes and costing less than two dollars a bowl.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 7 oz fideo noodles (or vermicelli broken into 1-inch pieces)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 can (15 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 medium white onion, roughly chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 4 cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Fresh cilantro for topping (optional but highly encouraged)
  • Lime wedges for serving
  • Sour cream or Mexican crema for drizzling (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Add the crushed tomatoes, onion, and garlic to a blender and blend until completely smooth. Set this aside — this is your flavor base and it's doing a lot of heavy lifting today.
  2. 2Heat the vegetable oil in a medium pot or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is shimmering, add the dry fideo noodles in a single layer and toast them, stirring constantly, for 3 to 4 minutes until they turn a deep golden brown. Keep your eyes on them — they go from perfect to burnt fast. That toasty color is where all the nutty flavor comes from, so don't skip this step.
  3. 3Pour the blended tomato mixture directly into the pot with the toasted noodles — stand back a little because it'll sizzle and steam. Stir everything together and let it cook for 2 minutes, letting the tomato paste into the noodles.
  4. 4Add the chicken broth, water, cumin, chili powder, salt, black pepper, and bay leaf. Stir to combine and bring everything to a boil over high heat.
  5. 5Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium-low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 10 to 12 minutes until the noodles are tender and have absorbed most of the broth. The soup should be thick and saucy — not watery. If it looks too thick, splash in a little more broth or water.
  6. 6Fish out the bay leaf, taste for salt, and adjust as needed. Ladle into bowls and top with fresh cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and a drizzle of crema if you're feeling fancy. Serve immediately.
💡 Improv Tip

The toasting step is non-negotiable — it's what separates a great sopa de fideo from a sad bowl of tomato noodles. If your noodles aren't turning a real golden brown, turn the heat up. And if you've got leftover rotisserie chicken, shred a little on top before serving — instant upgrade for basically free.
