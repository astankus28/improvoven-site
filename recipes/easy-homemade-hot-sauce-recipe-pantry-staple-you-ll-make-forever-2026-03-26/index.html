Easy Homemade Hot Sauce Recipe (Pantry Staple You'll Make Forever)

Published March 26, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy homemade hot sauce recipe is the one that ends your dependence on those little store bottles forever — we're talking real flavor, not just heat. A few fresh peppers, some garlic, vinegar, and about 30 minutes is all it takes. Once you taste this, mija, you're never going back.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
16 (makes about 1 cup)

Ingredients

  • 10 fresh hot peppers (fresno, jalapeño, habanero, or whatever you've got — mix them up)
  • 5 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1/2 small white onion, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Juice of half a lime

Instructions

  1. 1Roughly chop your peppers — no need to be precise here, they're all going in the blender. If you want less heat, pull out some of the seeds. If you want to cry happy tears, leave them all in. Your call.
  2. 2Heat the olive oil in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté for about 5 minutes until softened and just starting to get a little golden. This step is the difference between a flat hot sauce and one with actual soul.
  3. 3Add the chopped peppers to the pan and stir everything together. Cook for another 5 minutes so the peppers soften and get friendly with the onion and garlic.
  4. 4Pour in the vinegar and water, add the salt and sugar, and bring everything to a gentle simmer. Let it cook for about 8 to 10 minutes until the peppers are completely soft and the liquid has reduced just slightly.
  5. 5Let the mixture cool for about 5 minutes — do not skip this or your blender will fight you — then transfer everything to a blender or use an immersion blender right in the pot. Blend until completely smooth.
  6. 6Add the lime juice and taste. Adjust salt, vinegar, or sugar until it tastes exactly like yours. If it's too thick, add water one tablespoon at a time and blend again.
  7. 7Strain through a fine mesh strainer if you want a silky smooth sauce, or leave it chunky if you like texture. Pour into a clean glass jar or bottle and store in the fridge. It gets better after a day, I promise.
💡 Improv Tip

The pepper combo is where you make this recipe your own — don't stress about following it exactly. No fresnos? Use serranos. Have a lone habanero sitting in the drawer looking at you? Throw it in for smokiness and drama. The ratio of vinegar to peppers is what actually makes it a hot sauce, so keep that consistent and improvise everything else freely.
