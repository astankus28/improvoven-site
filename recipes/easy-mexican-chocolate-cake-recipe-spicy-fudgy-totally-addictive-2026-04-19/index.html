Easy Mexican Chocolate Cake Recipe (Spicy, Fudgy & Totally Addictive)

Published April 19, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Mexican chocolate cake recipe is what happens when your pantry fights back with something absolutely magical — deep cocoa, warm cinnamon, and just enough cayenne to make you raise an eyebrow in the best way. It's a one-bowl situation that comes together faster than you'd think, and honestly tastes like something you'd pay good money for at a little café in Coyoacán. Once you go Mexican chocolate, regular chocolate cake is going to feel a little boring.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
35 mins
Total
50 mins
Serves
8

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (or more if you're brave)
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup brewed coffee or warm water
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar or apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips (optional but highly recommended)
  • Powdered sugar or extra chocolate chips for topping

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease an 8x8 or 9x9 inch square baking pan, or a standard 9-inch round cake pan — whatever you've got works here.
  2. 2In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, cinnamon, cayenne, baking soda, and salt until everything is fully combined. Take a little sniff — that spiced cocoa smell is already telling you this is going to be something special.
  3. 3Pour the brewed coffee (or warm water), vegetable oil, vinegar, and vanilla extract directly into the bowl with your dry ingredients. Stir everything together with a wooden spoon or spatula until just combined — a few small lumps are totally fine, don't overwork it.
  4. 4Fold in the chocolate chips if you're using them, which, why wouldn't you? Pour the batter into your prepared pan and spread it out evenly.
  5. 5Bake for 30 to 35 minutes, until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean or with just a couple of moist crumbs. The top should look set and slightly crackled — that's your cue.
  6. 6Let the cake cool in the pan for at least 15 minutes before slicing. Dust with powdered sugar, scatter some extra chocolate chips on top, or honestly just cut right into it and eat it warm. No judgment here.
  7. 7Slice, serve, and watch people ask you what that secret flavor is. You can tell them or let it be mysterious — totally your call.
💡 Improv Tip

The coffee is your secret weapon here — it deepens the chocolate flavor without making anything taste like coffee, so don't skip it even if you're not a coffee person. No brewed coffee on hand? Dissolve a teaspoon of instant coffee or espresso powder in a cup of hot water and you're good to go. Also, if you want to lean even harder into the Mexican chocolate vibe, swap the plain chocolate chips for a chopped Abuelita or Ibarra tablet — those already have the cinnamon and sugar built right in and the flavor payoff is incredible.
