Easy Homemade Dulce de Leche Recipe (Just One Ingredient!)

Published April 7, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy homemade dulce de leche recipe is honestly one of the most magical things you can make in your kitchen — one can of sweetened condensed milk transforms into this rich, golden, caramel-like spread that tastes like it came straight from your abuela's stove. We're talking deep, buttery, sweet perfection that you can drizzle on toast, swirl into ice cream, spoon over flan, or honestly just eat with a spoon at midnight. No judgment here, this is a Miami kitchen.

Prep
2 mins
Cook
2 hrs 30 mins
Total
2 hrs 32 mins
Serves
8

Ingredients

  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk (label removed)
  • Water, enough to fully submerge the can in a pot

Instructions

  1. 1Grab your unopened can of sweetened condensed milk and peel off the paper label — you don't want that floating around in your pot.
  2. 2Place the can on its side in a deep pot or Dutch oven. This helps the milk circulate and cook more evenly inside.
  3. 3Cover the can completely with cold water — you want at least 1 to 2 inches of water above the top of the can at all times. This is the most important rule: never let the water drop below the can while it's cooking.
  4. 4Bring the water to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to a steady simmer. Set your timer and let it cook for 2 hours for a light, pourable dulce de leche, or 2.5 to 3 hours for a thicker, deeper, more spreadable result. Check the water level every 30 minutes and add hot water as needed to keep the can submerged.
  5. 5Once done, use tongs to carefully remove the can from the water and place it on a towel on your counter. Here comes the hardest part — let it cool completely to room temperature before opening. We're talking at least 1 hour. Do not rush this. Opening a hot pressurized can is a real bad time.
  6. 6Once fully cooled, open the can and stir the dulce de leche with a spoon. It should be smooth, creamy, and that gorgeous caramel-brown color. Transfer to a jar and store in the fridge for up to 3 weeks — if it lasts that long.
💡 Improv Tip

The longer you cook it, the darker and thicker it gets — 2 hours gives you something light and saucy, perfect for drizzling over cheesecake or mixing into coffee. Go 3 hours and you've got a thick, spreadable dulce de leche that holds its shape on a cookie. Know what you need before you start the timer, and always, always keep that can underwater the whole time.
