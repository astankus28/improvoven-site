Homemade Argentine Potato Salad (Ensalada Rusa)

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This homemade Argentine potato salad recipe — known as Ensalada Rusa — is one of those dishes that shows up at every asado, every family lunch, every 'we need something to bring' situation across Argentina and honestly most of Latin America. It's creamy, simple, made with stuff you probably already have, and it hits way harder than it has any right to for how easy it is.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs russet or Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas (or canned, drained)
  • 3/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon yellow or Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more for boiling water
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped (optional but very traditional)
  • 1/4 cup green olives, sliced (optional, adds that Argentine flair)

Instructions

  1. 1Peel and cube your potatoes into roughly 1-inch pieces and do the same with your carrots — keeping them a similar size means they cook evenly and nobody bites into a crunchy carrot in a creamy salad.
  2. 2Add the potatoes and carrots to a pot of well-salted cold water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat and cook for about 15 to 18 minutes, until both are fork-tender but not falling apart. You want them to hold their shape in the salad.
  3. 3In the last 2 minutes of cooking, toss the frozen peas right into the pot with the potatoes and carrots — this saves you a dish and gently cooks the peas without turning them to mush.
  4. 4Drain everything into a colander and let it cool for at least 15 minutes. Do not skip this — dressing hot potatoes gives you a greasy, broken mess instead of that creamy, fluffy texture you're after.
  5. 5While everything cools, whisk together the mayonnaise, mustard, white vinegar, salt, and black pepper in a large bowl until smooth and combined.
  6. 6Once the potatoes, carrots, and peas are cooled to room temperature, add them to the bowl with the dressing. If you're using hard-boiled eggs and olives, add those now too.
  7. 7Fold everything together gently with a spatula — you want to coat everything in that creamy dressing without smashing the potatoes. Taste and adjust salt if needed.
  8. 8Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving. Argentine Ensalada Rusa is always served cold, and a little chill time lets all those flavors come together beautifully. Serve it as a side, stuff it into a sandwich on crusty bread, or honestly just eat it with a spoon standing at the fridge — no judgment here.
💡 Improv Tip

The real secret to Ensalada Rusa that tastes like it came from an Argentine kitchen and not a sad deli container is the mustard and vinegar in the dressing. Most people just do straight mayo and wonder why it tastes flat — that little bit of acid and tang is what makes it sing. Also, if you can make it the night before, do it. Overnight Ensalada Rusa is genuinely better than fresh.
