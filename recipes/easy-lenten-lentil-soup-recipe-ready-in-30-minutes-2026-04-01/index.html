Easy Lenten Lentil Soup Recipe (Ready in 30 Minutes)

Published April 1, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Lenten lentil soup recipe is the one you're going to make every Friday from Ash Wednesday straight through Holy Week — thick, smoky, and satisfying enough that nobody's sitting at the table wishing for meat. It's got that sofrito base that makes everything taste like somebody's abuela was involved, and it comes together with stuff you probably already have sitting in your pantry right now.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (about 2 cups) dry green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 roma tomatoes, diced (or one 14 oz can diced tomatoes, drained)
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and sliced into coins
  • 2 medium Yukon Gold or russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 6 cups vegetable broth (or water plus 2 bouillon cubes)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of half a lemon or lime
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for serving (optional but highly encouraged)

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Once it shimmers, add the diced onion and bell pepper. Cook for about 4 to 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is soft and translucent. This is your sofrito base — don't rush it, this is where the flavor starts.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, and turmeric. Stir everything together and let the spices bloom in the oil for about 60 seconds. You'll smell it — that's the good stuff waking up.
  3. 3Toss in the diced tomatoes and stir to combine. Cook for another 2 minutes until the tomatoes start to break down and everything looks like a thick, fragrant paste.
  4. 4Add the rinsed lentils, sliced carrots, cubed potatoes, bay leaf, and vegetable broth. Stir well and bring the pot to a boil over high heat.
  5. 5Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium-low and let the soup simmer uncovered for 20 to 25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the lentils are completely tender and the potatoes are cooked through. If the soup gets too thick, add a splash of water or extra broth to loosen it up.
  6. 6Remove the bay leaf. Taste and adjust salt and black pepper. Squeeze in the lemon or lime juice — this is non-negotiable, that little hit of acid wakes the whole pot up.
  7. 7Ladle into bowls and top with fresh cilantro or parsley if you have it. Serve with crusty bread or white rice on the side and call it a meal.
💡 Improv Tip

If your lentils are sitting in the back of the pantry and you're not sure how old they are, don't panic — just add 5 to 10 extra minutes of simmering time and check them for tenderness. Old lentils are stubborn but they'll get there. And listen, if you want to make this soup even heartier, throw in a handful of chopped spinach or kale in the last 5 minutes of cooking. Leafy greens, no extra cost, no complaints at the table.
