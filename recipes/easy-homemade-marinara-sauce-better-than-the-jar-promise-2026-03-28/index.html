Easy Homemade Marinara Sauce (Better Than the Jar, Promise)

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy homemade marinara sauce recipe is the one you're going to come back to every single week — it takes 30 minutes, costs almost nothing, and tastes like it's been simmering all day. We're talking pantry staples: canned tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, done. Serve it over pasta for a weeknight dinner that hits every time.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional but highly recommended)
  • 1 28-ounce can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil (or 5-6 fresh basil leaves if you have them)
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 pound spaghetti or pasta of choice, cooked to package directions
  • Parmesan cheese and fresh basil for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Get your pasta water going first — big pot, heavily salted water, bring it to a boil. Cook your pasta according to the package and before you drain it, scoop out about a cup of that starchy pasta water and set it aside. That stuff is liquid gold for the sauce.
  2. 2While the water heats up, warm the olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium-low heat. Add the sliced garlic and let it get fragrant and just barely golden — about 3 to 4 minutes. Don't rush this step. Burnt garlic will haunt your sauce. If you're using red pepper flakes, toss them in now and let them bloom in the oil for about 30 seconds.
  3. 3Pour in the crushed tomatoes carefully — it will splatter a little, so stand back or use a lid as a shield. Stir everything together and add the sugar, oregano, basil, salt, and black pepper. The sugar isn't making this sweet, it's just balancing the acidity of the tomatoes.
  4. 4Raise the heat to medium and let the sauce come to a gentle simmer, then drop it back to medium-low. Cook uncovered for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the color deepens to a rich red. Taste it around the 10-minute mark and adjust salt as needed.
  5. 5Add your cooked, drained pasta directly into the skillet with the sauce and toss everything together. If the sauce feels too thick, splash in a little of that reserved pasta water a few tablespoons at a time until it coats the noodles perfectly. Serve hot with parmesan and fresh basil if you've got it.
💡 Improv Tip

The single best thing you can do for this sauce is buy good canned tomatoes — San Marzano style if your store carries them, but any whole or crushed tomatoes that aren't watery will work. Check the ingredient list: tomatoes, salt, maybe basil. That's it. The shorter the list, the better the sauce.
