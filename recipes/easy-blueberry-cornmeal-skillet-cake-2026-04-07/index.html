Easy Blueberry Cornmeal Skillet Cake

Published April 7, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy blueberry cornmeal skillet cake is the kind of dessert that makes people think you spent way more time in the kitchen than you did — golden, slightly crispy edges, jammy berries bursting through every bite. It comes together in one pan with ingredients you probably already have sitting around, and honestly? It hits different warm out of the oven with a little honey drizzled on top. Very much a 'cure your sweet tooth without a trip to the store' situation.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup whole milk (or any milk you have)
  • 1/3 cup sour cream or plain yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted — plus 1 tablespoon for the skillet
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar for topping (optional but do it)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F. Put your 10-inch cast iron skillet (or any oven-safe skillet) on the stovetop over medium-low heat and let it warm up while you mix everything together — this is the move that gives you those gorgeous crispy edges.
  2. 2In a large bowl, whisk together the cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon until combined. Make a well in the center.
  3. 3In a separate small bowl or measuring cup, whisk together the eggs, milk, sour cream, melted butter, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. 4Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir gently with a spatula until just combined — a few lumps are totally fine, don't overwork it. Fold in about 3/4 of the blueberries and save the rest for the top.
  5. 5Add the remaining tablespoon of butter to your warm skillet and swirl it around to coat the bottom and sides — it should sizzle a little, that's perfect. Pour in the batter and spread it into an even layer.
  6. 6Scatter the remaining blueberries on top and press them in just slightly. If you want, drizzle a little honey or sprinkle a pinch of sugar over the surface for a beautiful caramelized top.
  7. 7Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for 22 to 25 minutes, until the top is golden, the edges pull away from the pan, and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. 8Let it cool in the skillet for at least 10 minutes before slicing — or just grab a spoon and go straight from the pan like the rest of us. Serve warm with whipped cream, a scoop of vanilla ice cream, or just a drizzle of honey.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen blueberries work beautifully here — don't thaw them first. Just toss them in straight from the freezer and they'll release the most gorgeous purple swirls into the batter as it bakes. If your berries are on the tart side, bump the sugar up by a tablespoon and you're golden.
