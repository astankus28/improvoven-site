Cheap Protein Meals on a Budget: Spicy Black Bean and Egg Skillet

Published March 25, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're hunting for cheap protein meals on a budget, this smoky black bean and egg skillet is about to become your new best friend. It comes together in one pan in about 30 minutes using stuff you probably already have sitting in your pantry right now. Big flavors, zero stress — this is the kind of meal Miami kitchens were built for.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, with juices
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeño, thinly sliced (seeds in if you like heat)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro for topping (optional but highly recommended)
  • Warm tortillas or rice for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once it shimmers, add the diced onion and jalapeño and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is soft and translucent.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, chili powder, and oregano. Stir everything together and let those spices toast for about 60 seconds — your kitchen is about to smell incredible.
  3. 3Pour in the drained black beans and the full can of diced tomatoes with all their juices. Stir to combine, then let the mixture simmer uncovered for 8 to 10 minutes until it thickens up and the liquid reduces by about half.
  4. 4Taste the bean mixture and season generously with salt, black pepper, and a squeeze of lime juice. This step matters — beans need salt to shine, so don't be shy.
  5. 5Use a spoon to make 6 small wells in the bean mixture, evenly spaced around the skillet. Crack one egg into each well. Cover the skillet with a lid and cook on medium-low heat for 5 to 7 minutes, until the egg whites are fully set but the yolks are still runny. If you prefer fully cooked yolks, give it another 2 minutes.
  6. 6Remove from heat, scatter fresh cilantro over the top, and bring the whole skillet straight to the table. Serve with warm tortillas for scooping or spoon it over a bowl of rice.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to stretching this even further — add one diced bell pepper when you cook the onion, or stir in a handful of frozen corn with the beans. Both cost almost nothing and bulk the dish up beautifully. Leftovers reheat great the next morning with a fried egg on top, just saying.
