Simple White Bean Kale Soup for Meal Prep

Published April 27, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple white bean kale soup is exactly the kind of meal prep magic we live for — one pot, thirty minutes, and you've got lunch or dinner handled for the whole week. It's hearty, it's healthy, and it's the kind of thing your abuela would approve of even if she'd probably sneak in a ham hock. We're keeping it pantry-friendly and budget-tight, Miami style.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) white beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 bunch curly kale, stems removed and leaves roughly chopped
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced into coins
  • 4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Optional: crusty bread or cooked white rice for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Once it shimmers, add the diced onion, carrots, and celery. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion is soft and translucent and everything smells amazing.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, and red pepper flakes to the pot. Stir everything together and let it cook for 60 seconds — you want those spices to bloom in the oil and get fragrant. Don't skip this step, it builds real flavor.
  3. 3Pour in the diced tomatoes with their juices and stir to scrape up any bits stuck to the bottom of the pot. Let it cook for 2 minutes so the tomatoes can deepen a little.
  4. 4Add the drained white beans, vegetable broth, and water. Stir to combine, then bring the soup up to a gentle boil over medium-high heat.
  5. 5Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium-low and let the soup simmer for about 8 minutes. Use the back of a wooden spoon or a ladle to lightly mash some of the beans against the side of the pot — this thickens the broth naturally without any flour or cornstarch.
  6. 6Add the chopped kale directly into the pot and stir it in. It'll look like a lot at first but it wilts down fast. Cook for another 4 to 5 minutes until the kale is tender but still has a little color and life to it.
  7. 7Squeeze in the lemon juice, taste the soup, and adjust salt and black pepper as needed. Serve hot with crusty bread or over a scoop of white rice if you want to stretch it further. For meal prep, let it cool completely before dividing into airtight containers — it keeps in the fridge for up to 5 days and gets even better on day two.
💡 Improv Tip

If your kale is sitting in the fridge looking a little sad and wilted, this soup is literally its destiny — don't throw it out. Also, when you're reheating meal prep portions during the week, add a small splash of broth or water to loosen it back up because the beans absorb liquid as it sits. And if you've got a parmesan rind hanging around in your freezer, drop it in while the soup simmers for a deep, savory richness that'll make people think you cooked all day.
