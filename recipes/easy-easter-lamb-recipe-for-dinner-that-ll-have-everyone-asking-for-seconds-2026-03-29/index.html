Easy Easter Lamb Recipe for Dinner That'll Have Everyone Asking for Seconds

Published March 29, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Easter lamb recipe for dinner is the move when you want something that feels special without spending all day in the kitchen — because Easter should be about the people at the table, not stressing over the stove. We're doing bone-in lamb chops with a garlic-herb marinade that pulls from your pantry and hits with serious flavor. Miami-style, we're keeping it bold, keeping it simple, and keeping it absolutely delicious.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 8 bone-in lamb loin chops (about 1 inch thick)
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh or dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1Pat your lamb chops dry with paper towels — this step is non-negotiable if you want a good sear. Dry meat browns, wet meat steams, and we are not here for steamed lamb.
  2. 2In a small bowl, mix together the minced garlic, olive oil, oregano, rosemary, cumin, smoked paprika, red pepper flakes, lemon juice, salt, and black pepper until you have a thick, fragrant marinade.
  3. 3Rub that marinade all over the lamb chops, getting it into every crevice. If you have time, let them sit at room temperature for 20 to 30 minutes — even that short rest makes a real difference. If you planned ahead, marinate them in the fridge overnight and you will absolutely taste the upgrade.
  4. 4Heat a large cast iron skillet or heavy pan over medium-high heat until it's genuinely hot — like, hold your hand near it and respect it. Add a tiny drizzle of oil and let it shimmer.
  5. 5Place the lamb chops in the pan without crowding them. Cook for 3 to 4 minutes per side for medium-rare, or 4 to 5 minutes per side if you want them closer to medium. You're looking for a deep golden-brown crust on each side.
  6. 6Pull the chops off the heat and let them rest on a cutting board for 5 minutes. Resting is where the magic happens — all those juices redistribute and you get a chop that's tender all the way through instead of dry.
  7. 7Plate them up, hit them with fresh parsley, squeeze a little extra lemon if you're feeling it, and bring them to the table with pride. You did that.
💡 Improv Tip

Lamb chops are actually one of the more forgiving proteins once you understand the heat — the real budget hack here is buying them from a Latin market or butcher shop instead of the big grocery chain, where you'll often pay almost half the price for the same quality cut. And don't skip the cumin; that's the little Miami whisper in this recipe that makes people say 'wait, what IS that' in the best possible way.
