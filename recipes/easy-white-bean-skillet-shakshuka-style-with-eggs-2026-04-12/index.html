Easy White Bean Skillet Shakshuka-Style with Eggs

Published April 12, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy white bean skillet shakshuka-style recipe is what happens when you open the pantry, find a can of white beans, and refuse to settle for boring. Instead of the classic tomato-and-egg situation, we're building a creamy, garlicky, herb-forward base with cannellini beans that makes the whole thing feel luxurious — and nobody has to know it cost you about three dollars. Perfect for a weekend morning when you want something real on the table without a grocery run.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) white cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup chicken or vegetable broth (or water with a pinch of bouillon)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional but recommended)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish
  • Crusty bread or warm tortillas for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once shimmering, add the diced onion and green bell pepper. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and the onion starts to turn translucent.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, and red pepper flakes. Stir everything together and let it cook for about 60 seconds — you want those spices to bloom in the oil and get fragrant. Your kitchen should smell amazing right now.
  3. 3Pour in the diced tomatoes with all their juices and the broth. Stir to combine, then add the white beans. Use the back of your spoon to gently mash about a quarter of the beans against the side of the pan — this thickens the sauce and gives it that creamy, saucy body that makes shakshuka so satisfying.
  4. 4Let the whole mixture simmer on medium-low for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly and the flavors come together. Taste and adjust salt and black pepper as needed.
  5. 5Using a spoon, create 6 small wells in the bean mixture, spacing them evenly around the skillet. Crack one egg into each well. Season the eggs lightly with salt and pepper.
  6. 6Cover the skillet with a lid and cook on medium-low heat for 6 to 8 minutes, depending on how you like your yolks. Check at 6 minutes — the whites should be set but the yolks should still have some jiggle for that runny, saucy finish. Cook 1 to 2 minutes longer if you prefer firm yolks.
  7. 7Remove from heat, scatter fresh cilantro or parsley over the top, and bring the whole skillet straight to the table. Serve with crusty bread or warm tortillas for scooping up every last bit of that creamy bean sauce.
💡 Improv Tip

If your sauce looks too thick before you add the eggs, splash in a little more broth or even just water — the beans keep absorbing liquid as they sit, so don't be shy about loosening it up. You want it saucy enough to cradle those eggs and still be scoop-able with bread at the end.
