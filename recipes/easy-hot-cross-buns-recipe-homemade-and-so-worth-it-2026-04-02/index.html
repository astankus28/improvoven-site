Easy Hot Cross Buns Recipe (Homemade and So Worth It)

Published April 2, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy hot cross buns recipe homemade style is the kind of thing that makes your kitchen smell like a bakery and your whole afternoon feel like a win. We're talking soft, spiced, slightly sweet rolls with that iconic cross on top — made from pantry staples you probably already have. Perfect for a cozy midday spread with butter, jam, or whatever you've got going in the fridge.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
45 mins plus 1 hour rise time
Serves
12 buns

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast (one standard packet)
  • 1/2 cup warm milk (dairy or plant-based both work)
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground allspice (this is the Caribbean secret right here)
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup raisins or mixed dried fruit
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • For the cross: 1/2 cup flour mixed with 5 tbsp water
  • For the glaze: 2 tbsp honey or apricot jam warmed with 1 tbsp hot water

Instructions

  1. 1Proof your yeast first — combine the warm water, warm milk, and sugar in a large bowl. Sprinkle in the yeast and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it looks foamy and smells a little yeasty. If nothing happens, your yeast might be expired — check the date and start fresh if needed.
  2. 2Add the softened butter, egg, vanilla extract, salt, cinnamon, allspice, and nutmeg to the yeast mixture. Stir everything together until combined.
  3. 3Add the flour one cup at a time, stirring as you go. Once it comes together into a shaggy dough, fold in the raisins or dried fruit. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8 minutes until smooth and elastic. It should bounce back when you poke it.
  4. 4Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm spot for 1 hour or until doubled in size. If your kitchen runs cold, pop it in the oven with just the light on — that little bit of warmth does the trick.
  5. 5Once risen, punch the dough down gently and divide it into 12 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a smooth ball and place them close together in a greased 9x13 baking dish. Cover again and let them rise for another 30 minutes.
  6. 6Preheat your oven to 375°F. While the buns are doing their second rise, mix together the cross paste — combine 1/2 cup flour with about 5 tablespoons of water until you get a thick, pipeable paste. Transfer it to a zip-lock bag and snip a tiny corner off.
  7. 7Pipe the crosses onto the risen buns in one long line across the rows, then repeat in the other direction. It goes faster than you think.
  8. 8Bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown on top. While they're hot out of the oven, brush generously with your honey or warm jam glaze — that shine is everything.
  9. 9Let them cool for at least 10 minutes before pulling apart. Serve warm with butter, cream cheese, or whatever you've got. They are absolutely elite for a midday lunch spread alongside a cup of tea or café con leche.
💡 Improv Tip

The allspice is non-negotiable in my kitchen — it's what gives these buns that warm, slightly mysterious depth that tastes more Caribbean than British, and honestly that's a compliment. If you don't have dried fruit, chopped dates or even chocolate chips work beautifully. And if your buns are browning too fast before they're cooked through, just lay a piece of foil loosely over the top for the last 5 minutes.
