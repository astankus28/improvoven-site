Easy Citrus Chipotle Grilled Chicken Thighs

Published April 25, 2026 · Improv Oven

These easy citrus chipotle grilled chicken thighs are the kind of recipe that makes people think you've been cooking all day — but real talk, it comes together in under 30 minutes with stuff you probably already have. The smoky heat from the chipotle meets bright orange and lime juice and just sings on a hot grill. This one's a weeknight hero that tastes like a weekend cookout.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (about 4-5 pieces)
  • 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, minced (from a can)
  • 1 tablespoon adobo sauce (from the same can)
  • 3 tablespoons fresh orange juice (about half an orange)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 1 lime)
  • 1 teaspoon orange zest
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar or honey

Instructions

  1. 1Pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels — this is non-negotiable if you want that gorgeous sear instead of steamed, sad chicken. Set them aside on a plate.
  2. 2In a bowl, whisk together the minced chipotles, adobo sauce, orange juice, lime juice, orange zest, minced garlic, olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, black pepper, and brown sugar until fully combined. Taste it — it should be smoky, a little spicy, and bright from the citrus.
  3. 3Add the chicken thighs to the bowl and toss to coat every piece generously. If you have time, let them marinate for at least 20 minutes at room temp, or up to 8 hours in the fridge covered. Even 10 minutes makes a difference.
  4. 4Heat your grill or grill pan to medium-high. Brush the grates with a little oil so nothing sticks. Place the chicken thighs skin-side down and grill for about 8-10 minutes without moving them — let that crust develop.
  5. 5Flip the thighs and grill another 8-10 minutes on the other side, until the internal temperature hits 165°F. If the outside is charring too fast, move them to indirect heat and let them finish low and slow for a few minutes.
  6. 6Pull the chicken off the grill and let it rest for 5 minutes before serving. That rest keeps all the juices locked in — don't skip it. Squeeze a little extra lime over the top and serve with rice, black beans, or a simple slaw.
💡 Improv Tip

Only have naval oranges or even bottled OJ? Use it — don't let the absence of a fresh orange stop you from making this. The citrus is mostly there to balance the smoky heat and help tenderize the meat, and bottled juice does the job just fine. Same goes for the chipotles — if you open a can and only need two peppers, freeze the rest in a small zip bag by the tablespoon. Future you will be very grateful.
