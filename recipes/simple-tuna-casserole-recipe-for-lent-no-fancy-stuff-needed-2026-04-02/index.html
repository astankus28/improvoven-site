Simple Tuna Casserole Recipe for Lent (No Fancy Stuff Needed)

Published April 2, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple tuna casserole recipe for Lent is exactly what you need when Friday rolls around and you want something cozy, filling, and way better than sad fish sticks. It comes together with pantry staples you probably already have sitting around — canned tuna, cream of mushroom soup, egg noodles — and it bakes up golden and bubbling like pure comfort food. No stress, no expensive ingredients, just a real meal that feeds the whole family.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (5 oz each) tuna in water, drained
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of mushroom soup
  • 1/2 cup sour cream or mayonnaise
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese, divided
  • 2 cups egg noodles, cooked and drained
  • 1/4 cup diced onion
  • 1/4 cup diced green bell pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup crushed crackers or breadcrumbs (for topping)
  • 1 tablespoon butter, melted
  • Cooking spray

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F and lightly spray an 8x8 or similar baking dish with cooking spray so nothing sticks.
  2. 2Cook your egg noodles according to package directions until just al dente — they'll keep cooking in the oven so don't overdo it. Drain and set aside.
  3. 3In a large mixing bowl, combine the cream of mushroom soup and sour cream (or mayo) and stir until smooth. This is your creamy base and it's doing all the heavy lifting here.
  4. 4Add the drained tuna, frozen peas, diced onion, diced green bell pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper to the bowl. Give it a good stir.
  5. 5Fold in the cooked egg noodles and half of your shredded cheddar cheese. Mix everything together gently until it's all coated and combined.
  6. 6Pour the mixture into your prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  7. 7In a small bowl, mix the crushed crackers or breadcrumbs with the melted butter until they look like damp sand. Sprinkle this over the top of the casserole, then add the remaining cheddar cheese right on top of that.
  8. 8Bake uncovered for 20 to 25 minutes, until the top is golden brown and the edges are bubbling. Let it rest for 5 minutes before serving — it'll firm up a little and be easier to scoop.
💡 Improv Tip

If your casserole looks a little dry before it goes in the oven, splash in a few tablespoons of milk and stir — canned tuna and soup brands all behave a little different, so trust your eyes. And listen, if you've got a can of corn, some diced jalapeño, or even a little sofrito in the fridge, throw it in. That's the Improv Oven way — use what you've got and make it yours.
