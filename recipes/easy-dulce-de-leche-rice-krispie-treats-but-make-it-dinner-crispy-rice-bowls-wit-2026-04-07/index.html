Easy Dulce de Leche Rice Krispie Treats (But Make It Dinner — Crispy Rice Bowls with Caramelized Milk Glaze)

Published April 7, 2026 · Improv Oven

Okay so this started as an easy dulce de leche rice krispie treats recipe situation and turned into the most unexpectedly fire dinner bowl I've made all month. We're talking crispy pan-fried rice cakes glazed with that sticky sweet caramel milk sauce, topped with savory pulled chicken and a punchy vinegar slaw — it's sweet, salty, crunchy, and completely unhinged in the best way. Miami energy all the way, and it comes together with stuff you probably already have.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
40 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked white rice (day-old works best — even better)
  • 1/2 cup dulce de leche (store-bought can or homemade from sweetened condensed milk)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 2 cups shredded rotisserie chicken (or canned chicken drained well)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • For the quick slaw: 2 cups pre-shredded cabbage mix
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar (for the slaw)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for the slaw)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional garnish: fresh cilantro, lime wedges, hot sauce

Instructions

  1. 1Start your slaw first so it has time to soften and get tangy. In a medium bowl, toss the shredded cabbage with 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar, 1 tablespoon olive oil, 1 teaspoon sugar, and a pinch of salt. Mix it well, then set it aside — the longer it sits the better it gets.
  2. 2Make your dulce de leche glaze by whisking together the dulce de leche, soy sauce, 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar, garlic powder, smoked paprika, and red pepper flakes in a small bowl. Taste it — it should hit sweet, salty, smoky, and a little spicy all at once. Adjust seasoning to your liking.
  3. 3Divide your cooked rice into 4 equal portions and press each one firmly into a flat rice cake patty, about half an inch thick. Day-old rice is your best friend here because it holds together without falling apart in the pan.
  4. 4Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet or cast iron pan over medium-high heat. Once the oil is shimmering hot, add the rice cakes and press them down gently with a spatula. Let them cook undisturbed for 4 to 5 minutes until a deep golden crust forms on the bottom — resist the urge to move them.
  5. 5Flip the rice cakes carefully and cook the other side another 3 to 4 minutes until that side is equally crispy and golden. This is your rice krispie treat moment — that crunch is everything.
  6. 6While the rice cakes finish crisping, warm your shredded chicken in a separate small pan or microwave. Season it lightly with salt, pepper, and a tiny pinch of smoked paprika.
  7. 7Turn the heat down to low under your rice cakes and spoon about half of the dulce de leche glaze directly over the tops of each crispy cake. Let it bubble and caramelize for about 60 seconds — it will smell absolutely insane.
  8. 8To plate, set one glazed crispy rice cake on each plate, pile on a generous amount of shredded chicken, spoon a little more glaze over the chicken, then top with a heap of the tangy cabbage slaw. Finish with fresh cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and hot sauce if you're about that life.
💡 Improv Tip

The move that takes this from good to ridiculous is letting your rice cakes sit in the hot dry pan for 30 seconds before you add the oil — it dries out the surface just enough so you get that shatteringly crispy bottom like a Persian tahdig situation. Also do not skip the slaw — the acid cuts through the sweetness of the dulce de leche glaze and keeps the whole thing from being too rich. That contrast is the whole dish.
