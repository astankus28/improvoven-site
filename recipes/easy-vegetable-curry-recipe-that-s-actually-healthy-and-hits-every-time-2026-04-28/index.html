Easy Vegetable Curry Recipe That's Actually Healthy and Hits Every Time

Published April 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy vegetable curry recipe is healthy, ridiculously simple, and built almost entirely from pantry staples — the kind of meal that makes you feel like you really have your life together. We're talking creamy coconut milk, warm spices, and whatever vegetables you've got hanging around in the fridge doing absolutely nothing. It's the cure for refrigerator blindness, Miami style.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or coconut oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated (or 1 teaspoon ground ginger)
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, but recommended)
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk (full fat for creaminess, lite works fine too)
  • 2 cups cauliflower florets (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup frozen peas or spinach
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar or honey (balances the acidity)
  • Juice of half a lime
  • Fresh cilantro for serving
  • Cooked rice or warm flatbread for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Heat your oil in a large deep skillet or pot over medium heat. Once it shimmers, add the diced onion and cook for about 4 minutes, stirring here and there, until it softens and turns a little golden on the edges. Don't rush this part — that onion is building your whole flavor foundation right now.
  2. 2Add the garlic and ginger and stir constantly for about 60 seconds. You'll smell it immediately and it's going to smell incredible. This is the moment your kitchen transforms.
  3. 3Add the curry powder, cumin, turmeric, and cayenne right into the pan with the aromatics. Stir everything together and let those spices toast for about 30 seconds. This step is what separates a good curry from a flat, one-dimensional one — toasting blooms the spices and deepens the flavor.
  4. 4Pour in the diced tomatoes and stir to combine, scraping up any bits stuck to the bottom of the pan. Let it cook for 2 minutes so the tomatoes break down a little and everything comes together.
  5. 5Add the coconut milk, chickpeas, and cauliflower. Stir well, bring it to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low. Let it simmer uncovered for about 10 to 12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cauliflower is tender and the sauce thickens up nicely.
  6. 6Stir in the frozen peas or spinach and cook for another 2 minutes until just wilted and bright. Add your salt, sugar, and lime juice. Taste it — and adjust. More lime for brightness, more salt to pull the flavors forward, a pinch more cayenne if you want heat.
  7. 7Serve over fluffy rice or scoop it up with warm flatbread. Finish with a generous handful of fresh cilantro on top. If you have it, a drizzle of sriracha or a spoonful of plain yogurt on the side takes this over the edge.
💡 Improv Tip

The real secret weapon here is that pinch of sugar and squeeze of lime at the end — don't skip it. The sugar cuts the acidity from the tomatoes and the lime wakes everything up. It's the same logic we use in a lot of Caribbean cooking: balance the sour, balance the sweet, and suddenly the whole dish clicks into place. Also, this curry is honestly better the next day, so if you're meal prepping for the week, you just won your Monday.
