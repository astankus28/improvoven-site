Simple Elote Mexican Street Corn Recipe (Made Into a Full Dinner Bowl)

Published May 4, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple elote Mexican street corn recipe takes everything you love about that iconic street food — the charred corn, the creamy sauce, the cotija, the lime — and turns it into a legit dinner bowl that'll have everyone at the table asking for seconds. We're talking smoky grilled corn over a bed of seasoned rice with black beans, all draped in that addictive elote crema. It's the kind of meal that sounds fancy but costs next to nothing and comes together in about 30 minutes.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 4 ears of fresh corn (or 3 cups frozen corn kernels, thawed)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/3 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/3 cup sour cream
  • 1 lime, zested and juiced
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (for the crema)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 1/2 cup crumbled cotija cheese (or feta if that's what you've got)
  • 2 cups cooked white or brown rice
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, thinly sliced (optional)
  • Extra lime wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Start your rice first so everything lands at the same time. Cook 1 cup dry rice according to package directions, then fluff it with a fork and season with a pinch of salt and cumin. Keep it warm on low heat.
  2. 2While the rice cooks, warm your black beans in a small saucepan over medium-low heat with a splash of water and that half teaspoon of cumin. Stir occasionally and let them get hot and fragrant, about 5 minutes. Season with salt and set aside.
  3. 3Now the star of the show — the corn. If you're using fresh ears, cut the kernels off the cob. If you're using frozen, make sure they're thawed and patted dry — dry corn chars, wet corn steams, and we want char. Heat a large cast iron skillet or heavy pan over HIGH heat until it's seriously hot. Add the olive oil or butter, then spread the corn in a single layer. Do not stir it for 2 to 3 minutes. Let it just sit there and get those beautiful dark spots. Then toss and let it char on the other side for another 2 minutes.
  4. 4Season the charred corn right in the pan with smoked paprika, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat and remove from heat.
  5. 5Make your elote crema by whisking together the mayonnaise, sour cream, lime zest, lime juice, chili powder, and cayenne in a small bowl until smooth. Taste it — it should be tangy, creamy, and have a little kick. Adjust lime or seasoning to your liking.
  6. 6Build your bowls: start with a scoop of cumin rice, add a spoonful of black beans alongside it, then pile on a generous heap of that charred corn. Drizzle the elote crema over everything — and don't be shy about it.
  7. 7Finish each bowl with a heavy hand of crumbled cotija, fresh cilantro, jalapeño slices if you want that heat, and a fresh lime wedge on the side. Serve immediately while the corn is still warm.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to getting that real street corn char at home is patience and a dry pan — if your corn is releasing steam and looking pale, your pan wasn't hot enough or the corn was too wet. Crank the heat, pat the corn dry, and walk away for a full 2 to 3 minutes before you even think about touching it. That's where all the flavor lives.
