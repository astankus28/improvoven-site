Simple Deviled Eggs Recipe for Easter Dinner (With a Little Miami Magic)

Published April 2, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple deviled eggs recipe for Easter turns a classic appetizer into a showstopping Easter dinner centerpiece when you pile them high on a platter alongside some bold Latin-inspired sides. We're talking creamy, tangy, perfectly seasoned eggs that disappear before you can even set the tray down. Budget-friendly, zero stress, and honestly — people will think you went to culinary school.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
15 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 12 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon yellow mustard
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar or fresh lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce (we love Cholula)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika, plus more for garnish
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh chives or green onion
  • Optional: 4 slices crispy bacon, crumbled
  • Optional: 1/2 ripe avocado, mashed into the filling for Miami-style richness
  • 1 head butter lettuce or romaine, for serving as a dinner platter base
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 English cucumber, sliced thin
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Crusty bread or tostones on the side for a real dinner spread

Instructions

  1. 1Place your 12 eggs in a single layer in a large pot and cover with cold water by about an inch. Bring to a full boil over medium-high heat, then immediately cut the heat, cover the pot, and let the eggs sit in the hot water for exactly 12 minutes. This is your ticket to perfectly cooked yolks — no gray ring, no rubbery whites.
  2. 2While the eggs are sitting, fill a large bowl with ice water. When the 12 minutes are up, transfer the eggs straight into the ice bath and let them chill for at least 10 minutes. Cold eggs peel so much easier — do not skip this step, mija.
  3. 3Peel all 12 eggs under a thin stream of cold running water. The water gets under the membrane and the shells practically slide off. Pat them dry, then slice each egg in half lengthwise with a sharp knife.
  4. 4Pop all the yolks out into a medium mixing bowl. Set the white halves on a clean kitchen towel to dry while you make the filling.
  5. 5To the yolks, add the mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar or lime juice, hot sauce, garlic powder, onion powder, and smoked paprika. Season generously with salt and black pepper. Mash everything together with a fork first, then switch to a whisk and beat it until the filling is completely smooth and creamy. Taste it — it should be tangy, a little spicy, and make you want to eat it with a spoon. Adjust seasoning until it does.
  6. 6If you are going the avocado route — and honestly you should — mash the half avocado into the yolk mixture now. It makes the filling extra rich and gives it this beautiful pale green color that looks gorgeous on the Easter table.
  7. 7Transfer your filling into a zip-lock bag, press out the air, and snip a small corner off. Pipe the filling generously into each egg white half. Do not be shy — pile it up.
  8. 8Dust each deviled egg with a little smoked paprika, scatter the chives or green onion over everything, and add the bacon crumbles if you are using them.
  9. 9Now here is how you turn this into a dinner: lay a big bed of butter lettuce leaves on your largest platter or cutting board. Arrange the deviled eggs across the center. Scatter the cherry tomatoes and cucumber slices around the edges. Tuck in the lime wedges. Bring it to the table with crusty bread, tostones, or rice and beans on the side, and a big cold drink. This is Easter dinner, Improv Oven style — abundant, beautiful, and it cost you almost nothing.
💡 Improv Tip

The number one deviled egg mistake is under-seasoning the filling. Egg yolks absorb a lot of salt and acid, so taste your filling at least twice and keep adjusting. A little extra squeeze of lime at the end right before serving wakes up every single flavor — that bright hit of acid is the secret move.
