Simple Homemade Guacamole Recipe for Cinco de Mayo (and Every Other Day)

Published April 18, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple homemade guacamole recipe is the one you'll make for Cinco de Mayo and then refuse to stop making all summer long — it's that good. We're talking creamy avocado, bright lime, a little heat, and that fresh cilantro hit that makes everything feel like a party. Scoop it onto a lunch spread with chips, stuff it into a sandwich, or pile it on a salad — this guac does it all.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
0 mins
Total
10 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe Hass avocados
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 1 large lime)
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder (or 1 small garlic clove, finely minced)
  • 1/4 cup white onion, finely diced
  • 1 small jalapeño, seeded and finely diced (leave seeds in if you like heat)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin (optional but trust us)
  • 1 small Roma tomato, seeded and diced

Instructions

  1. 1Cut your avocados in half, remove the pits, and scoop the flesh into a medium bowl. Add the lime juice and salt right away — this is not the time to get distracted. The lime is doing double duty: flavor AND keeping your guac green.
  2. 2Use a fork to mash the avocado to your preferred texture. Some people like it completely smooth, but real talk? Leaving a few chunks gives it that homemade, hand-crafted vibe that no restaurant can replicate. Mash until it's mostly creamy with a little personality.
  3. 3Add the garlic powder (or fresh garlic), diced white onion, jalapeño, and cumin if using. Fold everything together gently — you're mixing, not punishing it.
  4. 4Add the diced tomato and cilantro last, and fold in carefully so the tomato doesn't turn the whole thing watery. Give it a taste and adjust salt and lime until it makes you close your eyes for a second.
  5. 5Serve immediately with tortilla chips for dipping, or use it as a spread on tortas, lettuce wraps, grain bowls, or wherever your lunch needs a little Miami sunshine. To store leftovers, press plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the guac before refrigerating — it'll stay green for up to a day.
💡 Improv Tip

The only guacamole secret worth knowing: your avocados have to be ripe. Press the skin gently — it should give just a little, like a soft handshake. Too firm and you'll be mashing forever with sad, flavorless results. Too mushy and it tastes like it's been sitting in a gas station. If your avocados aren't ready, put them in a paper bag with a banana overnight and wake up to perfection.
