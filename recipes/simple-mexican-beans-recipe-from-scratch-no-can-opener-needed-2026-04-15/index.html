Simple Mexican Beans Recipe from Scratch (No Can Opener Needed)

Published April 15, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Mexican beans recipe from scratch is one of those dishes that makes you wonder why you ever bought the canned stuff — dried beans, a few aromatics, and some patience is honestly all you need. We're talking creamy, deeply savory frijoles that taste like they've been simmering in someone's abuela's kitchen all day, except you can pull them off on a weeknight. Big flavor, tiny budget — that's the Improv Oven way.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
1 hour 45 mins
Total
1 hour 55 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 1 lb dried pinto beans (or black beans — both work beautifully)
  • 8 cups water, plus more as needed
  • 1/2 white onion, left in one piece
  • 5 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 jalapeño, halved (seeds in for heat, seeds out for mild)
  • 2 tablespoons lard or olive oil
  • 1.5 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano (Mexican oregano if you have it)
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • Optional: a small bunch of fresh cilantro stems tied together

Instructions

  1. 1Sort through your dried beans and pick out any little pebbles or shriveled beans — takes two minutes and it matters. Rinse them well under cold water. No soaking required for this recipe, but if you want to speed things up, a quick soak of even 30 minutes helps.
  2. 2Add the rinsed beans to a large heavy-bottomed pot — a Dutch oven is perfect here. Pour in 8 cups of water. Drop in the half onion, smashed garlic cloves, jalapeño, bay leaves, and cilantro stems if you're using them. These aromatics are your flavor foundation.
  3. 3Bring the pot to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce to a gentle simmer. You want to see lazy bubbles, not a rolling boil — aggressive boiling makes the beans tough and blows out the skins. Skim off any foam that rises to the top in the first 10 minutes.
  4. 4Add the lard or olive oil now. Fat helps the beans cook up creamy and rich — do not skip this step. Keep simmering uncovered, stirring occasionally, for about 1 hour to 1 hour 30 minutes. Check every 20 minutes or so and add hot water if the level drops below the beans.
  5. 5Here is the most important thing about cooking beans from scratch — do NOT add salt until the beans are already tender. Adding salt too early can keep the beans from softening properly. Start checking for doneness around the 1-hour mark by squishing a bean between your fingers. You want it soft all the way through with no chalky center.
  6. 6Once the beans are tender, add the salt, cumin, and dried oregano. Stir and simmer for another 15 minutes so the beans absorb all that seasoning. Taste and adjust salt as needed.
  7. 7Fish out the onion half, bay leaves, jalapeño, and cilantro bundle. At this point you have two choices — leave them whole as frijoles de olla, or grab a wooden spoon and smash about a quarter of the beans against the side of the pot to thicken everything up into a rustic, saucy consistency. Both versions are incredible.
  8. 8Serve hot with warm tortillas, over rice, alongside eggs, tucked into tacos — honestly these beans go with everything. Store leftovers in their liquid in the fridge for up to 5 days, and know that they taste even better the next day.
💡 Improv Tip

If your beans are still tough after 90 minutes, check how old they are — dried beans older than a year can take forever to soften no matter what you do. Buy from a store with good turnover, and when in doubt, check the bulk bins at a Latin grocery store. Fresh dried beans are a completely different animal and worth seeking out.
