Easy Shrimp Pasta Recipe for Lenten Fridays

Published April 1, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy shrimp pasta recipe is exactly what you need when Lenten Friday rolls around and you still want something that feels like a real meal — not a sacrifice. We're talking garlicky, buttery, a little lemony, ready in 30 minutes, and built almost entirely from pantry staples. Miami taught me that meatless doesn't mean flavorless, and this bowl of goodness is proof.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 12 oz spaghetti or linguine
  • 1 lb frozen shrimp, peeled and deveined (thawed)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup pasta water, reserved
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried)
  • Optional: grated Parmesan or crumbled cotija for topping

Instructions

  1. 1Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil. Cook your pasta according to package directions until al dente. Before draining, scoop out about 1/4 cup of that starchy pasta water and set it aside — don't skip this, it's liquid gold for your sauce.
  2. 2While the pasta cooks, pat your thawed shrimp completely dry with paper towels. This is important — wet shrimp steam instead of sear, and we want a little color on these guys.
  3. 3Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once shimmering, add the shrimp in a single layer. Season with paprika, cumin, salt, and black pepper. Cook for about 90 seconds per side until pink and just cooked through. Remove shrimp to a plate and set aside.
  4. 4In the same skillet, reduce heat to medium. Add the butter and let it melt, then add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes. Sauté for about 60 seconds, stirring constantly — you want it fragrant and golden, not burnt. Burnt garlic will ruin your whole vibe.
  5. 5Add the drained pasta directly into the skillet with the garlic butter. Toss to coat, adding a splash of that reserved pasta water to loosen everything up and create a light, silky sauce.
  6. 6Return the shrimp to the pan. Add the lemon juice and lemon zest, then toss everything together over medium heat for about 1 minute so all the flavors get to know each other.
  7. 7Remove from heat, fold in the fresh parsley, and taste for seasoning. Adjust salt, pepper, or lemon as needed. Plate it up and finish with Parmesan or cotija if you like. Serve immediately while it's hot and glossy.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen shrimp is your best friend here — it's budget-friendly, already cleaned, and honestly just as good as fresh for a pasta dish like this. Buy the bag when it's on sale and keep it in the freezer. To thaw fast, just run cold water over them in a colander for about 5 minutes. Never microwave shrimp to thaw unless you enjoy rubbery sadness.
