Simple Weekly Prep Chicken Rice Bowls

Published April 30, 2026 · Improv Oven

These simple weekly prep chicken rice bowls are the move when you want to eat good all week without standing over a stove every single night. Season some thighs, cook a big pot of rice, and suddenly Monday through Friday is handled. Miami flavors, pantry ingredients, zero stress.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth (or water with 2 bouillon cubes)
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro for topping (optional but come on)
  • Hot sauce for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Pat your chicken thighs dry with a paper towel — this is the secret to getting that nice golden sear. Mix together the garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Rub that seasoning blend all over both sides of the chicken.
  2. 2Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once it's shimmering, add the chicken thighs in a single layer. Don't crowd them — cook in batches if you need to. Sear 5 to 6 minutes per side until deeply golden and cooked through (internal temp 165°F). Pull them off the heat and let them rest for 5 minutes before slicing or shredding.
  3. 3While the chicken is cooking, rinse your rice under cold water until it runs clear. Add the rinsed rice and chicken broth to a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil over high heat, then drop it to the lowest simmer, cover with a tight lid, and cook for 15 minutes. Kill the heat and let it steam covered for another 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  4. 4While rice steams, toss your drained black beans and frozen corn into a small saucepan over medium heat with a pinch of cumin and a splash of water. Stir until warmed through, about 3 to 4 minutes. Squeeze in a little lime juice and taste for salt.
  5. 5To build your bowls, start with a scoop of rice, add your sliced or shredded chicken on top, spoon over the beans and corn, and hit it with a squeeze of fresh lime. Cilantro and hot sauce are not optional in this house. For meal prep, divide everything into four containers and refrigerate for up to 4 days — the flavors only get better.
💡 Improv Tip

The real cheat code here is chicken thighs over breasts — they cost less, forgive you when you overcook them a little, and stay juicy even after three days in the fridge. If you want to stretch this even further, one extra cup of rice and another can of beans will feed six people for basically the same price.
