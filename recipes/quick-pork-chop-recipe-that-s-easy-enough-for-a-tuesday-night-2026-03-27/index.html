Quick Pork Chop Recipe That's Easy Enough for a Tuesday Night

Published March 27, 2026 · Improv Oven

This quick pork chop recipe is easy to pull off even on your most exhausting weeknights — we're talking juicy, golden-seared chops with a garlicky pan sauce that tastes like you spent way more time than you did. It's the kind of meal that makes your whole kitchen smell incredible and your family think you really had it together today. Pantry ingredients, one skillet, done.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in pork chops, about 3/4 inch thick
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Juice of half a lime
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Pull your pork chops out of the fridge about 10 minutes before cooking — room temperature meat sears way better than cold meat straight from the fridge. Pat them completely dry with paper towels. This is not optional. Dry chops get that beautiful golden crust. Wet chops steam instead of sear, and nobody wants that.
  2. 2Mix together the garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and black pepper in a small bowl. Rub that seasoning blend all over both sides of each pork chop like you mean it. Get into the edges too.
  3. 3Heat your largest skillet — cast iron if you have it — over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil and let it get hot until it shimmers. You want that pan properly hot before the chops go in.
  4. 4Lay the pork chops in the skillet in a single layer and do not touch them for 4 minutes. Seriously, walk away. Let the crust build. Flip them once and cook another 3 to 4 minutes on the second side. Bone-in chops at this thickness should hit an internal temperature of 145°F. Pull them out and set them on a plate to rest.
  5. 5Turn the heat down to medium. Add the smashed garlic cloves and butter to the same skillet — all those brown bits on the bottom are pure flavor, do not wash that pan. Let the garlic sizzle for about 30 seconds until it smells amazing.
  6. 6Pour in the chicken broth and soy sauce, then scrape up all those caramelized bits from the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon. Let the sauce simmer and reduce for about 2 to 3 minutes until it thickens slightly. Squeeze in the lime juice and give it a stir.
  7. 7Nestle the pork chops back into the skillet and spoon that pan sauce all over them. Let everything hang out together for one more minute so the chops soak up some of that goodness. Plate them up, pour the remaining sauce on top, and hit it with fresh parsley or cilantro if you have it. Serve immediately.
💡 Improv Tip

The number one reason pork chops come out dry is overcooking — modern pork is totally safe at 145°F internal temperature, which still has a tiny blush of pink in the center. If you pull them at 145°F and let them rest for 5 minutes before cutting, they will be juicy every single time. Grab a cheap instant-read thermometer if you don't have one yet — it'll change your whole cooking game.
