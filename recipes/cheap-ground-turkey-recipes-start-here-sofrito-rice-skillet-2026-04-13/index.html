Cheap Ground Turkey Recipes Start Here: Sofrito Rice Skillet

Published April 13, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're hunting for cheap ground turkey recipes that actually taste like something, this sofrito rice skillet is your new weeknight ride-or-die. Everything comes together in one pan in about 30 minutes using stuff you probably already have sitting in your pantry. It's got that Miami kitchen energy — bold, savory, a little smoky — without touching a $20 bill.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice (uncooked)
  • 2 cups chicken broth (or water with a bouillon cube)
  • 1/2 cup sofrito (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes with juices
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for topping (optional but highly encouraged)

Instructions

  1. 1Heat olive oil in a large deep skillet or wide sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 2-3 minutes until softened and translucent.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds, stirring constantly so it doesn't burn. Your kitchen should already smell amazing.
  3. 3Add the ground turkey to the pan. Break it up with a wooden spoon and cook until no pink remains, about 5-6 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, cumin, smoked paprika, and oregano right in the pan.
  4. 4Stir in the sofrito and let it cook with the turkey for about 2 minutes. This is where all that flavor gets locked in — don't skip this step.
  5. 5Pour in the uncooked rice and stir it around so every grain gets coated in that seasoned turkey mixture. Toast it for about 1 minute.
  6. 6Add the diced tomatoes with their juices, the drained black beans, and the chicken broth. Stir everything together and bring it to a boil.
  7. 7Once boiling, reduce heat to low, cover the skillet tightly with a lid, and let it cook undisturbed for 18-20 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and has absorbed the liquid.
  8. 8Remove from heat and let it sit covered for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork, taste for seasoning, and top with fresh cilantro before serving.
💡 Improv Tip

Sofrito is the secret weapon here — most Latin grocery stores sell it in a jar near the seasonings for under $3 and it does more flavor work than a whole spice rack. If your rice comes out a little wet, just leave the lid off for a few minutes on low heat and it'll dry right up. And listen, this reheats beautifully the next day, so make the whole batch even if you're cooking for two.
