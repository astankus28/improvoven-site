Easy Refried Beans Recipe from Scratch for Cinco de Mayo (or Any Day You're Hungry)

Published May 3, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy refried beans recipe from scratch is exactly what your Cinco de Mayo spread has been missing — creamy, smoky, and so much better than anything that comes out of a can. We're talking dried pinto beans, a little lard or oil, some aromatics, and about 30 minutes of active work. Make a big batch because these disappear fast at any table.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
2 hours (plus overnight soak)
Total
2 hours 10 mins active (plus soaking time)
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 1 lb dried pinto beans
  • 10 cups water (for boiling)
  • 3 tablespoons lard, bacon fat, or neutral oil
  • 1 medium white onion, roughly chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Reserved bean cooking liquid (about 1 to 2 cups)

Instructions

  1. 1The night before, pick through your dried pinto beans and remove any tiny pebbles or shriveled beans — yes, this step matters. Rinse them well, then cover with cold water by about 3 inches and let them soak overnight on the counter. They'll plump up and cut your cooking time way down.
  2. 2Drain and rinse the soaked beans. Add them to a large pot and cover with fresh water — about 10 cups. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a steady simmer. Cook uncovered for 1.5 to 2 hours until the beans are completely soft and creamy when you press one between your fingers. No crunchy beans allowed here.
  3. 3Before you drain anything, scoop out and save about 2 cups of that cloudy bean cooking liquid — that stuff is liquid gold for getting your refried beans to the perfect consistency. Now drain the rest.
  4. 4In a large heavy skillet or Dutch oven, heat your lard, bacon fat, or oil over medium heat. Once it shimmers, add the chopped onion and cook for 5 to 7 minutes until soft and a little golden. Add the smashed garlic and cook another 2 minutes until fragrant — your kitchen should smell incredible right now.
  5. 5Add the cooked beans to the skillet in batches, using a potato masher or the back of a sturdy wooden spoon to mash them as you go. You don't want a perfectly smooth paste — some texture is what makes these feel homemade. Stir everything together with the onions and garlic.
  6. 6Add the cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Stir and taste. Now start adding your reserved bean liquid a few tablespoons at a time, mashing and stirring until you hit your ideal consistency — some people like them thick like a spread, others like them looser and scoopable. You're in charge here.
  7. 7Cook on low heat for another 5 minutes, stirring frequently, until everything is combined and the flavors have melded. Taste one more time and adjust salt as needed. Serve hot topped with a little crumbled cotija, a drizzle of crema, or just straight up with warm tortillas.
💡 Improv Tip

If you forgot to soak your beans overnight, no stress — do a quick soak instead. Cover the dry beans with water in a pot, bring to a boil for 2 minutes, then turn off the heat and let them sit for 1 hour. Drain, rinse, and cook as normal. It adds a little time but gets you to the same delicious place.
