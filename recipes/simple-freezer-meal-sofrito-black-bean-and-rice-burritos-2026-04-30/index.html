Simple Freezer Meal: Sofrito Black Bean and Rice Burritos

Published April 30, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're building a stash of simple freezer meal recipes, these sofrito black bean and rice burritos need to be at the top of your list — they come together in 30 minutes and freeze like a dream. We're talking pantry staples, a little Latin flavor, and zero stress on those nights when cooking from scratch just isn't happening. Wrap them up, stack them in the freezer, and thank yourself later.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
8 burritos

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 1/2 cup jarred sofrito
  • 1 can (10 oz) diced tomatoes with green chiles
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup shredded Mexican-blend cheese
  • 8 large flour tortillas (burrito size)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

  1. 1Cook the rice according to package directions. While that's going, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. 2Add the sofrito to the skillet and let it cook for about 2 minutes until it gets fragrant and starts to deepen in color — that's where all the flavor lives.
  3. 3Stir in the black beans, frozen corn, and diced tomatoes with green chiles. Season with cumin, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Let everything simmer together for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. 4Once the rice is cooked, fold it into the bean mixture and stir until evenly combined. Taste it — adjust salt or spices if needed. Let the filling cool for about 5 minutes so it's easier to handle.
  5. 5Lay out your tortillas and spoon about 3/4 cup of filling down the center of each one. Sprinkle with shredded cheese. Fold in the sides, then roll tightly from the bottom up like you mean it.
  6. 6To freeze: wrap each burrito individually in aluminum foil, then place them all in a large zip-lock freezer bag. Label it with the date and freeze for up to 3 months.
  7. 7To reheat from frozen: remove the foil, wrap the burrito in a damp paper towel, and microwave for 2 to 3 minutes, flipping halfway through. Or pop the foil-wrapped burrito in the oven at 375°F for 25 minutes for that crispy edge. Either way, you win.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon here is jarred sofrito — you can find it in almost any Latin grocery store or the international aisle at a regular supermarket, and it does the heavy lifting on flavor so you don't have to chop a single onion or pepper. Goya and Kirby brands both work great. If you can't find it, blend together half an onion, half a green bell pepper, a few garlic cloves, and a handful of cilantro — boom, homemade sofrito in two minutes.
