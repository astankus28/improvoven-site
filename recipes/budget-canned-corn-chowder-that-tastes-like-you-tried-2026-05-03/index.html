Budget Canned Corn Chowder That Tastes Like You Tried

Published May 3, 2026 · Improv Oven

This budget canned corn chowder recipe is the kind of thing that makes people think you spent way more time and money than you actually did — and we love that for you. A few pantry staples, one pot, and about 30 minutes is all it takes to land a creamy, smoky, soul-warming bowl of chowder on the table. It's the kind of lunch that makes you actually look forward to the middle of the day.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) whole kernel corn, drained
  • 1 can (15 oz) creamed corn
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup milk or evaporated milk
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 strips bacon or 2 oz smoked sausage, chopped (optional but recommended)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or scallions for topping

Instructions

  1. 1Heat butter or olive oil in a medium pot over medium heat. If you're using bacon or smoked sausage, add it first and cook for 2-3 minutes until it starts to brown and release its fat — that's your flavor foundation right there.
  2. 2Add the diced onion to the pot and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened and translucent. Toss in the garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant. Don't walk away — garlic burns fast and nobody wants that.
  3. 3Sprinkle in the smoked paprika and cumin, stirring everything together for about 30 seconds so the spices bloom in the fat. This is a small step that makes a big difference in depth of flavor.
  4. 4Add the diced potatoes and pour in the broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a steady simmer. Cook uncovered for about 10 minutes until the potatoes are just fork-tender.
  5. 5Add both cans of whole kernel corn and the can of creamed corn. Stir to combine — the creamed corn is your secret weapon here, it thickens the chowder naturally without any flour or heavy cream.
  6. 6Pour in the milk or evaporated milk and stir gently. Let everything simmer together on low heat for 5 more minutes. Don't let it boil hard after adding the milk or it can separate on you.
  7. 7Taste and season with salt and black pepper. Ladle into bowls and top with fresh cilantro or sliced scallions. Serve with crusty bread or crackers and call it a win.
💡 Improv Tip

If your chowder feels too thin, scoop out about a cup of it and mash or blend it, then stir it back in — instant body, no cornstarch needed. And if you've got a leftover sofrito cube or a splash of hot sauce hanging around in the fridge, either one would slide right into this recipe like they were always invited.
