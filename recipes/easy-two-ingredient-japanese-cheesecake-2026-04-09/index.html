Easy Two Ingredient Japanese Cheesecake

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy two ingredient Japanese cheesecake is the kind of magic trick that makes people think you went to culinary school — but all you need is cream cheese and eggs. It comes out jiggly, pillowy, and just lightly sweet, like a cheesecake and a soufflé had a baby. Honestly? This one lives rent-free in my head.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened (half a standard block)
  • 3 large eggs, separated and at room temperature

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 320°F and place a rack in the middle. Set a small baking dish or oven-safe pan filled with about an inch of hot water on the lower rack — this steam bath is what gives the cheesecake that famous jiggly texture, so don't skip it.
  2. 2In a microwave-safe bowl, warm your softened cream cheese for about 20 seconds, then whisk it until completely smooth. Add the 3 egg yolks one at a time, whisking well after each addition until you have a silky, pale yellow mixture with no lumps. Set this aside.
  3. 3In a clean bowl, beat your 3 egg whites with a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium-high speed until stiff peaks form — when you lift the beaters, the peaks should stand straight up and hold their shape. This is the step that makes the cake rise and get fluffy, so take your time here.
  4. 4Using a spatula, gently fold about a third of the whipped egg whites into the cream cheese mixture to lighten it up. Then add the remaining egg whites in two more additions, folding slowly and carefully each time. You want to keep as much air in there as possible — no aggressive stirring.
  5. 5Line a 6-inch round cake pan or a similar small baking dish with parchment paper and pour in your batter. Give the pan a gentle tap on the counter to release any big air bubbles.
  6. 6Bake on the middle rack for 25 minutes, or until the top is lightly golden and the cake has a gentle jiggle in the center when you nudge the pan. Turn off the oven, crack the door open slightly, and let the cheesecake cool inside the oven for another 15 minutes — this prevents it from deflating too fast.
  7. 7Remove from the oven and let it cool completely before slicing. It will settle a little as it cools and that is totally normal and perfect. Serve at room temperature or chilled. Dust with powdered sugar if you want to feel fancy.
💡 Improv Tip

Room temperature ingredients are non-negotiable here — cold cream cheese will give you lumps, and cold egg whites won't whip up properly. Pull everything out of the fridge at least 30 minutes before you start, and this little cake will reward you big time.
