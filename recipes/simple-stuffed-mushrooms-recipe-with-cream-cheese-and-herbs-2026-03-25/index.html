Simple Stuffed Mushrooms Recipe with Cream Cheese and Herbs

Published March 25, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple stuffed mushrooms recipe is one of those things that looks way fancier than the effort it takes — perfect for when you want to impress without losing your mind in the kitchen. We're talking creamy, herby filling packed into tender mushroom caps, ready in about 30 minutes with ingredients you probably already have. Classic Improv Oven energy: simple pantry stuff, big delicious results.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 16 oz baby bella or white button mushrooms (about 16-20 mushrooms)
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus extra for topping
  • 2 tablespoons plain breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped (or 1 tsp dried)
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional but recommended)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Cooking spray or a little extra olive oil for the pan

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 400°F. Line a baking sheet with foil or give it a light spray of cooking oil — mushrooms release a lot of liquid and you don't want a soggy situation.
  2. 2Clean your mushrooms with a damp paper towel (don't rinse them under water, they'll soak it up like a sponge). Pop the stems off and set them aside — don't throw those away, they're going in the filling.
  3. 3Finely chop the mushroom stems. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a small skillet over medium heat, add the chopped stems and minced garlic, and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until softened and most of the moisture has cooked off. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Let this cool for a couple minutes.
  4. 4In a bowl, mix together the softened cream cheese, Parmesan, breadcrumbs, parsley, onion powder, cumin, and red pepper flakes. Fold in the cooked mushroom stem mixture and stir until everything is well combined. Taste it — adjust salt, pepper, or spice to your liking.
  5. 5Arrange your mushroom caps hollow-side up on the prepared baking sheet. Drizzle or brush the remaining tablespoon of olive oil over the caps. Spoon the filling generously into each cap ��� don't be shy, pile it slightly above the rim. Finish with a sprinkle of extra Parmesan on top.
  6. 6Roast in the oven for 18-22 minutes until the mushrooms are tender and juicy and the tops are golden and a little crispy. Let them rest for 2-3 minutes before serving — the filling is molten hot straight out of the oven.
💡 Improv Tip

The number one enemy of stuffed mushrooms is a watery mess on your plate. Two things prevent that: don't skip cooking down the stems to release their moisture before mixing into the filling, and make sure your oven is fully preheated before the mushrooms go in. High heat = roasted, not steamed. Also, if your cream cheese is cold, it won't mix smoothly — pull it out of the fridge about 20 minutes before you start and thank yourself later.
