Sausage, Peppers, and Onions

Improv Oven Classic Recipe

This sausage, peppers, and onions situation is exactly what weeknight dinners should be—minimal prep, maximum flavor, ready in 30 minutes flat. We're using smoked sausage and a simple tomato base with fresh peppers and onions that gets better the longer it simmers. Serve it on a bun or over rice, either way it's a total win.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 14 oz skinless smoked sausage, slice into 1/2 inch pieces (we used Eckrich brand for this recipe)
  • 1 tbs vegetable oil
  • 1 green pepper, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 sweet onion, sliced
  • 28 oz tomato puree (we used Tuttorosso puree for the San Marzano tomatoes)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp ground pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbs sugar
  • 1 tbs garlic powder
  • 1 tbs basil
  • 1 tbs parsley
  • bread or buns for sandwiches

Instructions

  1. 1In a skillet on medium heat, brown sausage until cooked fully through. Remove and set aside.
  2. 2In the same skillet add vegetable oil and saut peppers and onions until they begin to brown, add salt and pepper.
  3. 3Pour tomato puree into skillet, add basil, parsley, garlic powder, sugar and water. Still until well combined.
  4. 4Cook on low heat for 15 minutes, stirring frequently.
  5. 5Stir in sausage pieces and cook for an additional 10 minutes. Serve hot.
💡 Improv Tip

Don't skip browning the sausage first—it builds flavor. And here's the move: slice everything uniform so it cooks evenly and looks restaurant-quality on the plate.
