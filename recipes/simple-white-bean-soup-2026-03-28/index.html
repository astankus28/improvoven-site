Simple White Bean Soup

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple white bean soup recipe is the kind of thing your abuela would throw together on a Tuesday without thinking twice — humble ingredients, big flavor, done in 30 minutes. We're talking creamy cannellini beans, a sofrito base, and a splash of lemon that ties everything together. It's cheap, it's filling, and it's exactly what your fridge has been trying to tell you it could make.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) cannellini or great northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, diced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for serving
  • Crusty bread or crackers for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Once it shimmers, add the diced onion, carrot, and celery. Cook for about 5 to 6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables have softened and the onion is translucent. This little sofrito base is where all your flavor starts, so don't rush it.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, and oregano. Stir everything together and let it cook for about 60 seconds until the garlic is fragrant. You'll know it's ready when your kitchen starts smelling incredible.
  3. 3Pour in the drained beans and the broth. Stir to combine, bring the soup to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low. Let it simmer uncovered for 12 to 15 minutes so the flavors can come together and the broth thickens slightly.
  4. 4Here's where you make it creamy without any cream: scoop out about one cup of the soup — beans and all — and either mash it with a fork or blend it quickly. Stir it back into the pot. This trick gives the broth that silky, thick body that makes people think you spent way more time on this than you did.
  5. 5Squeeze in the lemon juice, taste for salt and pepper, and adjust as needed. Ladle into bowls, hit it with a drizzle of good olive oil if you have it, and finish with fresh parsley or cilantro. Serve with crusty bread on the side for the full experience.
💡 Improv Tip

If your broth is looking too thick, just splash in a little more water or broth and stir — this soup loosens up beautifully. And if you want to stretch it further, throw in a handful of kale or spinach right at the end and let it wilt in. Suddenly you've got a completely different soup and zero extra dollars spent.
