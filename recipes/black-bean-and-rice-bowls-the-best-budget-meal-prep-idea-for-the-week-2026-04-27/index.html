Black Bean and Rice Bowls: The Best Budget Meal Prep Idea for the Week

Published April 27, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're looking for budget meal prep ideas for the week, these smoky black bean and rice bowls are about to become your new Sunday ritual — we're talking four nights of real dinner for under ten dollars total. Seasoned with cumin, garlic, and a hit of sazón, this is the kind of food that tastes like somebody's abuela made it, not like you were trying to save money. Load them up with whatever's hanging around in your fridge and you've got a different bowl every single night.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups water or low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 1 packet sazón con culantro y achiote (or 1 tsp cumin plus 1/2 tsp smoked paprika)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup water (for the beans)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro for topping (optional but worth it)
  • Toppings of your choice: sliced avocado, pickled jalapeños, hot sauce, shredded cheese, sour cream

Instructions

  1. 1Start your rice first so everything finishes around the same time. Combine the rice and water or broth in a medium saucepan, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for 18 minutes. Kill the heat and let it steam with the lid on for another 5 minutes — don't peek, let it do its thing.
  2. 2While the rice cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for about 4 minutes until it softens and goes translucent. Add the garlic and cook for another 60 seconds until fragrant — you'll know when you smell it.
  3. 3Add the sazón packet (or cumin and smoked paprika), cumin, oregano, and garlic powder to the onion and garlic. Stir everything together and let those spices bloom in the oil for about 30 seconds. This step is small but it makes a huge difference in flavor.
  4. 4Add the drained black beans and the half cup of water to the skillet. Stir to coat the beans in all that seasoning, then reduce heat to medium-low and let everything simmer together for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the liquid reduces and the beans get saucy and thick. Season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lime juice.
  5. 5Fluff your rice with a fork, taste it, and season with a pinch of salt if needed. Divide the rice evenly into four meal prep containers, spoon the black beans generously over the top, and add a handful of fresh cilantro if you have it. Let everything cool completely before sealing the containers.
  6. 6Store in the fridge for up to 4 days. To reheat, add a small splash of water to the container before microwaving so the rice doesn't dry out — about 2 minutes on high, stirring halfway through. Add your toppings fresh each night so nothing gets soggy.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon here is keeping your toppings separate all week — stash your avocado, hot sauce, and any other toppings on the side and add them right before you eat. That way Monday's bowl and Thursday's bowl both feel fresh and exciting, not like sad leftover duty. Also, if you want to stretch this even further, add a fried egg on top or some rotisserie chicken you grabbed on sale — this base plays well with everything.
