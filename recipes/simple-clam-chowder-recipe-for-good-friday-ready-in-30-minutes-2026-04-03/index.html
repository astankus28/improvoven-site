Simple Clam Chowder Recipe for Good Friday (Ready in 30 Minutes)

Published April 3, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple clam chowder recipe is exactly what you want on Good Friday — creamy, comforting, and made almost entirely from pantry staples you probably already have. No fresh clams required, no complicated technique, just a big bowl of something warm and satisfying that respects the day without emptying your wallet. It's the kind of soup your abuela would nod at approvingly.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (6.5 oz each) chopped clams in clam juice, do not drain
  • 3 medium russet potatoes, peeled and diced small (about 1/2 inch)
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 1 cup whole milk or whatever milk you have
  • 3 slices thick-cut bacon, chopped (optional but worth it)
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or scallions for garnish, optional
  • Oyster crackers or crusty bread for serving

Instructions

  1. 1If using bacon, cook the chopped bacon in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat until crispy, about 4 minutes. Remove bacon with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the drippings in the pot. If skipping bacon, melt your butter directly in the pot.
  2. 2Add the diced onion and celery to the pot and cook over medium heat for 4 to 5 minutes until softened and translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook one more minute until fragrant — your kitchen should smell amazing right now.
  3. 3Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir constantly for about 1 minute to cook out the raw flour taste. This is your thickener, so don't skip this step.
  4. 4Pour in the broth slowly while stirring to prevent lumps, then add the reserved clam juice from both cans. Add the diced potatoes, dried thyme, smoked paprika, and a good pinch of black pepper. Bring everything to a boil, then reduce heat to a steady simmer.
  5. 5Cook uncovered for about 10 to 12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are fork-tender. Test one — it should give easily when poked.
  6. 6Reduce heat to low and stir in the evaporated milk and regular milk. Add the chopped clams. Let everything warm through together for 3 to 4 minutes — do not let it boil hard once the dairy is in or it can curdle and break on you.
  7. 7Taste and adjust salt and pepper. Ladle into bowls, top with the reserved crispy bacon if using, a sprinkle of fresh parsley or scallions, and serve immediately with oyster crackers or thick slices of crusty bread.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon here is the evaporated milk — it gives you that rich, creamy chowder texture without needing heavy cream, which costs twice as much. Canned clams are 100% the move for this recipe; they're already cooked, already flavorful, and the juice in the can is liquid gold for your broth. Don't pour that down the drain.
