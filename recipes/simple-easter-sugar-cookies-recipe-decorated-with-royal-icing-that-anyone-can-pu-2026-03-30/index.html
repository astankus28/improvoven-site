Simple Easter Sugar Cookies Recipe Decorated with Royal Icing (That Anyone Can Pull Off)

Published March 30, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Easter sugar cookies recipe decorated with pastel royal icing is the kind of thing that looks like you spent a weekend on it but actually comes together in one chill afternoon. We keep it real here — no fancy equipment, no pastry school degree, just butter, flour, and a little patience. Think of these as your edible centerpiece that doubles as dessert after a big Easter dinner.

Prep
25 mins
Cook
12 mins
Total
37 mins
Serves
24 cookies

Ingredients

  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon fine salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated white sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract (optional but trust me)
  • 2 cups powdered sugar (for the icing)
  • 3 tablespoons milk (for the icing)
  • 1 tablespoon light corn syrup (for the icing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (for the icing)
  • Gel food coloring in pastel shades — pink, yellow, lavender, light blue
  • Sprinkles, sanding sugar, or candy pearls for decorating

Instructions

  1. 1In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Set it aside — that's your dry team.
  2. 2In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar together until it looks pale and fluffy, about 3 minutes. If you have a hand mixer, use it. If not, some elbow grease works fine — this is how abuela did it.
  3. 3Add the egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract (if using) to the butter mixture and beat until fully combined and smooth.
  4. 4Gradually add the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients, stirring or mixing on low until a soft dough forms. It should come together cleanly and not stick too much to your hands.
  5. 5Divide the dough in half, flatten each half into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap or a zip bag, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. This is non-negotiable — cold dough is what gives you those clean, sharp cookie shapes. Go watch something on Netflix.
  6. 6When you're ready to bake, preheat your oven to 375°F and line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
  7. 7Lightly flour your counter and roll the dough out to about 1/4 inch thickness. Use Easter cookie cutters — eggs, bunnies, chicks, whatever you've got — and cut out your shapes. Re-roll the scraps and keep going until the dough is used up.
  8. 8Place cookies on the prepared baking sheets about 1 inch apart and bake for 9 to 11 minutes, until the edges are just barely starting to turn golden. The centers might look slightly underdone — that's perfect. They firm up as they cool.
  9. 9Let the cookies cool on the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack and let them cool completely before decorating. Decorating warm cookies is a heartbreak you don't need.
  10. 10While the cookies cool, make your simple royal icing: whisk together the powdered sugar, milk, corn syrup, and vanilla extract until you get a smooth, slightly thick glaze. It should drizzle off your spoon in a ribbon but not be watery.
  11. 11Divide the icing into small bowls — one for each color you want. Add a tiny drop of gel food coloring to each bowl and stir until you hit your pastel shade. Gel coloring is way more vibrant than liquid, so start with just a toothpick dip of color.
  12. 12Decorate your cookies however feels right. For a clean look, spoon or pipe a base coat of icing onto each cookie and let it set for 15 minutes before adding details or sprinkles on top. You can use zip-lock bags with a tiny corner snipped off as piping bags — works like a charm.
  13. 13Let the decorated cookies sit at room temperature for at least 30 minutes until the icing is fully set and dry to the touch. Then they're ready to stack, gift, or just eat standing over the kitchen counter like the champion you are.
💡 Improv Tip

The single biggest game-changer for decorated sugar cookies is gel food coloring — the squeeze bottles of liquid coloring from the grocery store baking aisle will make your icing runny and your colors weak. Grab a small set of gel colors at a craft store or online for a couple dollars and your Easter cookies will look like they came from a bakery. Also, if your icing gets too thick while you're working, just stir in a few drops of milk to loosen it back up.
