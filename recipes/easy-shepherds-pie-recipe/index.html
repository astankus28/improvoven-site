Easy Shepherd's Pie Recipe

Improv Oven Classic · Originally published January 2015

If you're a meat and potatoes family, this one's for you. Ground beef, sweet peppers, corn, and cream of mushroom all go into one baking dish — topped with creamy mashed potatoes and baked until golden. It's been one of our most popular recipes since 2015 for a reason: it's filling, budget-friendly, and tastes like something you spent all day on.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
45 mins
Total
1 hour
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 lbs potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted
  • 2 lbs ground beef
  • 1 sweet red pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 Vidalia onion, chopped
  • 1 can whole kernel corn (15.25 oz), drained
  • 1 can cream of mushroom soup (10.75 oz)
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 2 tsp garlic salt
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped parsley to garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Place peeled, cubed potatoes in a large pot. Cover with cold water and add 1/2 to 1 tsp salt. Bring to a boil on high heat, then reduce to medium-low. Cover and cook until fork-tender, about 15–20 minutes.
  2. 2Drain potatoes and return to pot. Mash with sour cream and melted butter. Season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  3. 3In a large skillet over medium heat, cook ground beef, red pepper, and onion until beef is browned and vegetables are tender, about 10 minutes. Drain excess fat.
  4. 4Add corn, cream of mushroom soup, milk, and garlic salt to the skillet. Stir until well combined.
  5. 5Transfer beef mixture into an ungreased 3-quart baking dish (13x9 in). Spread mashed potatoes evenly over the top.
  6. 6Bake uncovered at 350°F for 30 minutes or until heated through and potato top is lightly golden.
  7. 7Garnish with chopped parsley if desired. Serve hot.
💡 Improv Tip

Don't skip draining the fat after browning the beef — it makes a big difference in how the filling sets up. You can also swap the ground beef for ground turkey to lighten it up, or add a handful of frozen peas to the filling for extra color and nutrition. Leftovers reheat beautifully the next day.
