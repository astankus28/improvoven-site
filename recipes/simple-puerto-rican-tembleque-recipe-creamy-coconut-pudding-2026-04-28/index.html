Simple Puerto Rican Tembleque Recipe (Creamy Coconut Pudding)

Published April 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Puerto Rican tembleque recipe is one of those magical desserts that looks like you spent all day in the kitchen but only asks for about 30 minutes and a handful of pantry staples. It's silky, coconut-forward, and that little wobble when you flip it out of the mold? Pure satisfaction. If you've got a can of coconut milk sitting in the back of your cabinet right now, you're already halfway there.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins plus 2 hours chilling
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (13.5 oz each) full-fat coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon for topping

Instructions

  1. 1Pour both cans of coconut milk into a medium saucepan. Before you turn on the heat, whisk in the cornstarch until it's fully dissolved with no lumps — this is your most important step, so take a minute here and do it right.
  2. 2Add the sugar and salt to the pan and give everything one more good whisk to combine.
  3. 3Turn the heat to medium and stir constantly with a wooden spoon or heat-safe spatula. Don't walk away — this stuff can stick to the bottom if you ignore it. Keep stirring until the mixture thickens noticeably, about 10 to 15 minutes. You'll know it's ready when it coats the back of your spoon and holds a line when you drag your finger through it.
  4. 4Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. 5Lightly grease your mold, ramekins, or a simple 8-inch round baking dish with a tiny bit of neutral oil or cooking spray. Pour the hot tembleque mixture in evenly.
  6. 6Let it cool on the counter for about 20 minutes, then cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours — overnight is even better if you can wait.
  7. 7When ready to serve, run a thin knife around the edges, place a plate on top of the mold, and flip it out in one confident move. Dust generously with ground cinnamon and serve cold.
💡 Improv Tip

Full-fat coconut milk is non-negotiable here — the light stuff will give you a sad, soupy pudding that won't hold its shape. And if your tembleque doesn't flip out perfectly clean, don't stress it. Just dust it with extra cinnamon and call it rustic. Nobody's leaving that table disappointed either way.
