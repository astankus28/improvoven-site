Easy Homemade Queso Dip Recipe (Better Than the Jar, Promise)

Published April 18, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy queso dip recipe homemade style is the one your friends will ask about every single time you make it — creamy, a little smoky, with just enough kick to keep things interesting. No processed cheese blocks required, just real ingredients you probably already have sitting in your kitchen. We're keeping it simple, budget-friendly, and absolutely dangerous near a bag of tortilla chips.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
15 mins
Total
25 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup whole milk (or whatever milk you have)
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth or water
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese (freshly shredded melts way better)
  • 1/2 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1 can (10 oz) diced tomatoes with green chiles, drained (Rotel or store brand)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1/4 cup pickled jalapeños, chopped
  • Optional: fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime to finish

Instructions

  1. 1In a medium saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter until it starts to foam just a little. Add the flour and whisk it together for about 1 minute — you're cooking out that raw flour taste, which matters. It'll look like a pale paste and that's exactly right.
  2. 2Slowly pour in the milk while whisking constantly, then add the chicken broth. Keep whisking until the mixture is smooth and starts to thicken, about 3 to 4 minutes. You want it to coat the back of a spoon before you add the cheese.
  3. 3Reduce the heat to low — this is important, don't skip it. High heat will make your cheese grainy and sad. Add the shredded cheddar and Monterey Jack in two or three small handfuls, stirring between each addition until fully melted before adding more.
  4. 4Once all the cheese is melted and silky, stir in the drained diced tomatoes with green chiles, the cumin, garlic powder, and smoked paprika. Let everything simmer together on low for 3 to 4 minutes so the flavors can get to know each other.
  5. 5Taste it. Adjust salt, pepper, or spice to your liking. If you want more heat, now's the time to add those pickled jalapeños. If the queso feels too thick, add a splash of milk and stir until you hit that perfect dippable consistency.
  6. 6Pour into your serving bowl, hit it with fresh cilantro and a little squeeze of lime if you're going that route, and get it to the table fast — this queso is best hot and fresh with tortilla chips, warm flour tortillas, or honestly just a spoon.
💡 Improv Tip

Shred your own cheese from a block — I know it's one extra step but pre-shredded cheese has anti-caking powder on it that makes the queso grainy instead of smooth. A block of cheddar at most stores is actually cheaper per ounce anyway, so you win twice.
