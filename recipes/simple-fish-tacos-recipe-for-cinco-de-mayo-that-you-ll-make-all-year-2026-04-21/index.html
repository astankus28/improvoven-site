Simple Fish Tacos Recipe for Cinco de Mayo (That You'll Make All Year)

Published April 21, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple fish tacos recipe is exactly what your Cinco de Mayo spread needs — crispy, flavorful, and ready in 30 minutes with ingredients you probably already have. We're talking flaky white fish, a zippy slaw, and a creamy sauce that ties it all together. No deep fryer, no fancy fish counter — just good food made easy, Miami style.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs tilapia or cod fillets (frozen works great, just thaw first)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 8 small flour or corn tortillas
  • 2 cups pre-shredded coleslaw mix
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp sour cream
  • 1 tbsp lime juice (plus extra limes for serving)
  • 1 tsp hot sauce (Tabasco or whatever you have)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • 1 avocado, sliced (optional but highly encouraged)

Instructions

  1. 1Pat your fish fillets completely dry with paper towels — this is the move that gives you a nice sear instead of sad steamed fish. Cut them into strips about 3 inches long.
  2. 2Mix your cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper together in a small bowl. Coat both sides of your fish strips generously with the spice mix.
  3. 3While the fish is sitting with the seasoning, make your creamy slaw. In a medium bowl, combine the coleslaw mix, mayonnaise, sour cream, lime juice, and hot sauce. Toss it all together and taste it — adjust lime or hot sauce to your liking. Set aside.
  4. 4Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. You want it hot but not smoking. Add the fish strips in a single layer — do not overcrowd the pan or they'll steam instead of sear. Cook in batches if needed.
  5. 5Cook fish for 3 to 4 minutes on the first side without touching it. Flip once and cook another 2 to 3 minutes until cooked through and slightly golden on the edges. The fish will flake easily when it's done.
  6. 6Warm your tortillas directly over a gas burner for 20 seconds per side for that charred flavor, or wrap them in a damp paper towel and microwave for 45 seconds if that's easier.
  7. 7Assemble your tacos: lay down a spoonful of creamy slaw, add a few pieces of fish, top with fresh cilantro, avocado slices, and a squeeze of lime. Serve immediately and watch them disappear.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen tilapia is genuinely your best friend here — it's usually under $5 a pound, it thaws fast under cold running water, and it takes seasoning beautifully. Don't sleep on it just because it's budget. The real secret though? Make the slaw first and let it sit while you cook the fish. Those 10 minutes let the lime juice soften the cabbage just enough so every bite has that perfect creamy crunch.
