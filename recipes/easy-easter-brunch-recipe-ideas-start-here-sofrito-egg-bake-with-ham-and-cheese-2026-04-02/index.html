Easy Easter Brunch Recipe Ideas Start Here: Sofrito Egg Bake with Ham and Cheese

Published April 2, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're hunting for easy Easter brunch recipe ideas that don't require a culinary degree or a second mortgage, this sofrito egg bake is your answer. It's got that Latin soul — think savory ham, melty cheese, and a hit of sofrito that makes the whole thing taste like your abuela made it — all baked in one dish in about 30 minutes. Perfect for feeding a crowd straight from the oven while you're still in your pajamas.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons sofrito (jarred is totally fine)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup diced ham (deli ham or leftover ham both work)
  • 1/2 cup diced bell pepper, any color
  • 1/4 cup diced white onion
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella or Mexican blend cheese, divided
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for topping (optional but worth it)
  • Hot sauce for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish or a large cast iron skillet with your olive oil or butter. You want every corner coated so nothing sticks.
  2. 2In a large mixing bowl, crack all 8 eggs and add the milk, sofrito, garlic powder, cumin, salt, and black pepper. Whisk everything together until it looks smooth and uniform — about 60 seconds of real whisking, not just a lazy stir.
  3. 3Add the diced ham, bell pepper, onion, and half of your shredded cheese directly into the egg mixture. Stir it all together so the good stuff is evenly distributed throughout.
  4. 4Pour the entire mixture into your greased baking dish. Spread it out gently so the fillings are even, then sprinkle the remaining cheese across the top in a nice even layer.
  5. 5Slide it into the oven and bake for 18 to 22 minutes. You're looking for the edges to be set, the center to be just barely jiggly, and the top to be lightly golden. A toothpick inserted in the middle should come out clean.
  6. 6Pull it out of the oven and let it rest for 5 minutes — this is non-negotiable, it helps everything firm up so you can slice it cleanly. Top with fresh cilantro or parsley, slice into squares, and serve with hot sauce on the side.
💡 Improv Tip

Sofrito is the secret weapon here — that little jar hiding in your fridge or pantry is doing about 80% of the flavor work so you don't have to. No sofrito? Blend up half an onion, half a green bell pepper, 3 garlic cloves, and a handful of cilantro with a splash of olive oil and use that instead. Same energy, zero regrets.
