Easy Meal Prep Ideas for the Week: Sofrito Rice Bowls with Black Beans and Roasted Veggies

Published April 27, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you've been searching for easy meal prep ideas for the week that actually taste good on day four, this is your answer. We're building a full week of lunches and dinners around one big pot of sofrito-scented rice, crispy roasted vegetables, and creamy black beans — all done in about 30 minutes. It's the kind of meal prep your future self is going to seriously thank you for.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
5 meal prep containers (5 lunches or dinners)

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 3 3/4 cups water or low-sodium chicken broth
  • 3 tablespoons sofrito (jarred is totally fine)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sazón seasoning (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 2 bell peppers (any color), sliced into strips
  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced into half moons
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (for roasting)
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • Optional toppings: sliced avocado, hot sauce, fresh cilantro, sour cream

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 425°F and line a large baking sheet with foil or parchment — we're not here to scrub pans on a Sunday.
  2. 2Toss the bell peppers, zucchini, and red onion on the baking sheet with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Spread them out in a single layer so they roast and get those golden edges — not steam. Slide them into the oven for 22 to 25 minutes, flipping once halfway through.
  3. 3While the veggies roast, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the sofrito and stir it around for about 2 minutes until it smells incredible and the raw edge is cooked off.
  4. 4Add the rice to the pot and stir it into the sofrito so every grain gets coated. Pour in the water or broth, add the sazón, and give it a good stir. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for 18 minutes. Remove from heat and let it steam covered for 5 more minutes, then fluff with a fork.
  5. 5While the rice finishes, warm the black beans in a small saucepan over medium-low heat with the cumin, smoked paprika, lime juice, and a pinch of salt. Stir occasionally for about 5 minutes until heated through and fragrant. You're not cooking them to death — just waking them up.
  6. 6Now build your containers. Divide the sofrito rice evenly between 5 meal prep containers. Add a scoop of seasoned black beans to each one, then pile on the roasted veggies. Let everything cool completely before sealing and refrigerating.
  7. 7Store in the fridge for up to 5 days. To reheat, microwave with a damp paper towel on top for about 2 minutes — it keeps the rice from drying out. Add your fresh toppings like avocado or cilantro right before eating, not before storing.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon here is sazón — that little orange packet from the Latin aisle costs almost nothing and makes your rice taste like it took all day. If you can't find it, mix together 1/2 teaspoon each of cumin, coriander, garlic powder, and a pinch of turmeric for the color. Also, swap the veggies for whatever is about to turn in your fridge — broccoli, sweet potato, even frozen corn thrown straight on the pan works. That's the whole Improv Oven philosophy right there.
