How to Make Guacamole

Improv Oven Classic · Originally published April 2017

Inspired by Chipotle's guacamole, this is the version we keep coming back to. The secret is using avocados that are just right — soft when you squeeze them but not mushy — and going easy on the onion. Fresh lime, good salt, and a little cilantro do the rest. Ready in under 10 minutes and better than anything from a jar.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
0 mins
Total
10 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup onion, finely diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced (optional)
  • 1 roma tomato, diced (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Cut avocados in half and remove the pit. Scoop the flesh into a medium bowl.
  2. 2Toss and coat avocado with lime juice immediately to prevent browning.
  3. 3Add salt and mash with a fork until you reach your desired consistency — smooth or slightly chunky.
  4. 4Fold in cilantro, onion, and jalapeño. Add tomato last if using.
  5. 5Taste and adjust salt and lime. Serve immediately with chips, on tacos, or as a side.
💡 Improv Tip

Go easy on the onion — you can always add more but you can't take it out. If your avocados aren't ripe yet, leave them on the counter overnight next to a banana to speed it up. To store leftovers, press plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the guacamole to keep air out and prevent browning.
