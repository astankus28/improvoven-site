Simple Sardine Pasta — A Pantry Recipe That Actually Slaps

Published April 13, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple sardine pasta pantry recipe is the kind of weeknight dinner that makes you feel like a genius for never throwing anything away. We're talking canned sardines, pasta, a few aromatics, and boom — you've got a bowl that tastes like something a Sicilian abuela and a Miami abuela cooked up together. Budget-friendly, ready in 30 minutes, and deeply satisfying in a way that'll make you wonder why you ever slept on sardines.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 12 oz spaghetti or linguine
  • 2 cans (3.75 oz each) sardines in olive oil, drained (oil reserved)
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (more if you like it spicy)
  • 1/2 cup pitted green or black olives, roughly chopped
  • 2 tablespoons capers, drained
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (plus the reserved sardine oil)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more for pasta water
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup pasta cooking water, reserved

Instructions

  1. 1Bring a big pot of heavily salted water to a boil — it should taste like the ocean, because honestly that's the whole vibe we're building here. Cook your pasta according to package directions until just al dente. Before you drain it, scoop out about 1/4 cup of that starchy pasta water and set it aside. Drain the pasta and set it aside too.
  2. 2While the pasta cooks, heat the 2 tablespoons of olive oil plus the reserved sardine oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes and let them sizzle gently for about 2 minutes — you want the garlic golden and fragrant, not burnt. Keep an eye on it, this is not the moment to check your phone.
  3. 3Add the drained diced tomatoes to the skillet and stir everything together. Let it cook for about 4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes soften and start to meld into the oil.
  4. 4Add the olives and capers and stir to combine. Let that cook another 2 minutes so all those briny, punchy flavors get friendly with each other.
  5. 5Now add the sardines. Use a spoon or fork to gently break them into chunky pieces — not a paste, we want some texture in there. Stir everything together and cook for 2 minutes just to warm the sardines through and let them mingle with the sauce.
  6. 6Add the drained pasta directly into the skillet. Toss everything together, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water to help the sauce coat every strand. If it looks dry, add a little more pasta water — that starchy liquid is liquid gold, don't skip saving it.
  7. 7Remove from heat and stir in the lemon zest, lemon juice, and fresh parsley. Taste it. Adjust salt, pepper, or red pepper flakes to your liking. Plate it up and eat immediately while it's hot and glossy and perfect.
💡 Improv Tip

Don't skip the lemon zest at the end — it's the move that lifts the whole dish and cuts through the richness of the sardines and olive oil. If you only have sardines packed in water instead of oil, no stress, just add an extra drizzle of olive oil and a tiny pinch more salt to compensate for that flavor the oil usually brings.
