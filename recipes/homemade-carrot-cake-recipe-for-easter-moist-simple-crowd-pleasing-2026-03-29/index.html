Homemade Carrot Cake Recipe for Easter (Moist, Simple, Crowd-Pleasing)

Published March 29, 2026 · Improv Oven

This homemade carrot cake recipe for Easter is the one you bring to the family gathering and suddenly everyone's asking for it — yes, even Tía who says she doesn't eat sweets. It's made with pantry staples you probably already have, comes together in one bowl, and that cream cheese frosting? Ridiculous in the best way. Whether you're feeding a crowd after Easter Mass or just want something special for a Sunday lunch spread, this cake earns its spot on the table.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
35 mins
Total
55 mins
Serves
12

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 cup vegetable oil (or any neutral oil)
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 cups freshly grated carrots (about 4 medium carrots)
  • 1/2 cup crushed pineapple, drained (trust me on this one)
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional but highly recommended)
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for frosting)
  • Pinch of salt (for frosting)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F. Grease two 9-inch round cake pans (or a 9x13 baking dish if you want to keep it simple — no judgment here) and line the bottoms with parchment paper. Set aside.
  2. 2In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger. Take a second to smell that — that's Easter right there.
  3. 3In a separate large bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, brown sugar, and eggs until the mixture is smooth and slightly lightened in color, about 1 minute. Stream in the oil while whisking, then add the vanilla and mix until combined.
  4. 4Fold the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients using a spatula until just combined — don't overwork it. A few streaks of flour are fine at this stage.
  5. 5Add the grated carrots, drained crushed pineapple, and nuts if using. Fold everything together gently. The batter will be thick and gorgeous and smell absolutely amazing.
  6. 6Divide the batter evenly between your prepared pans. Bake for 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean with just a few moist crumbs. If you're using a 9x13 pan, start checking at 35 minutes.
  7. 7Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then turn them out onto a wire rack. Do NOT frost until completely cool — this is non-negotiable. Warm cake and cream cheese frosting is a sad, sliding mess.
  8. 8While the cakes cool, make the frosting. Beat the softened cream cheese and butter together with a hand mixer or stand mixer on medium speed until completely smooth and fluffy, about 2 minutes. Add the sifted powdered sugar in two additions, mixing on low first so you don't wear it. Add the vanilla and pinch of salt, then beat on medium-high for another minute until light and creamy.
  9. 9Once the cakes are fully cooled, place one layer on your serving plate. Spread a generous layer of frosting on top, then set the second layer on top. Frost the top and sides with the remaining cream cheese frosting. If you want to get fancy for Easter, press some chopped walnuts along the sides or pipe little carrot shapes on top with orange and green food coloring — totally optional, always adorable.
  10. 10Slice, serve, and accept the compliments graciously. Leftovers (if there are any) keep covered in the fridge for up to 4 days.
💡 Improv Tip

Grate the carrots yourself — skip the pre-shredded bag. Fresh-grated carrots are moister and more tender, which is what makes this cake so soft it practically melts. Box grater, five minutes, totally worth it. And that crushed pineapple? Don't skip it. You won't taste pineapple, but it keeps the crumb insanely moist and adds a subtle sweetness that makes people say 'what IS that?' in the best way.
