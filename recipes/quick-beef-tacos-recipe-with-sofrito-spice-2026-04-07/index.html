Quick Beef Tacos Recipe With Sofrito Spice

Published April 7, 2026 · Improv Oven

This quick beef tacos recipe is the lunch move when you've got ground beef in the fridge and absolutely no patience — we're talking flavor-packed, ready in 30 minutes, and built the way we do it in Miami: with a little sofrito soul and a lot of lime. It's the kind of meal that tastes like you spent all afternoon cooking but actually comes together faster than finding parking on Calle Ocho. Keep this one in your back pocket for every midday taco craving.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef (80/20 is your friend here)
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1/2 white onion, finely diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, finely diced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (optional, add it)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1/2 cup shredded cabbage or iceberg lettuce
  • Fresh cilantro, a good handful roughly chopped
  • Hot sauce, for serving
  • Sour cream or Mexican crema, optional

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once it shimmers, add the diced onion and green bell pepper. Cook for about 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and the onion starts to go translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook another 30 seconds until fragrant — your kitchen should smell incredible right now.
  2. 2Add the ground beef to the pan, breaking it up with a wooden spoon or spatula. Season immediately with the cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, garlic powder, cayenne, salt, and black pepper. Cook over medium-high heat for 6-8 minutes, stirring and breaking up the meat, until fully browned with no pink remaining. Don't rush this step — those little crispy bits of beef are where the flavor lives.
  3. 3Drain any excess fat from the pan if needed, leaving just a little behind for richness. Add the drained diced tomatoes and stir everything together. Reduce the heat to medium and let it all simmer together for 5 minutes so the beef soaks up the tomato and the flavors get friendly. Squeeze in the lime juice, stir, and taste for seasoning — adjust salt or spice as needed.
  4. 4While the beef finishes, warm your corn tortillas. You can do this directly over a gas flame for 20-30 seconds per side using tongs until they get a little char, or dry-toast them in a separate pan over medium heat. Stack them under a clean kitchen towel to keep them soft and warm.
  5. 5Build your tacos: two tortillas per taco for structure, a good spoonful of the beef mixture, a pinch of shredded cabbage, fresh cilantro, a hit of hot sauce, and a small drizzle of crema if you're going there. Serve immediately with lime wedges on the side and something cold to drink.
💡 Improv Tip

If your ground beef is looking a little pale and steaming instead of browning, that means the pan isn't hot enough or it's too crowded — either crank the heat or cook in two batches. A proper sear on that beef is what separates a good taco from a great one, so don't skip that step even if you're in a hurry.
