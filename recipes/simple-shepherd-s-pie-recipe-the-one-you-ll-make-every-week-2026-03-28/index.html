Simple Shepherd's Pie Recipe (The One You'll Make Every Week)

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple shepherd's pie recipe is the kind of dinner that makes your whole place smell incredible and has everyone at the table before you even call them. It's hearty, it's cozy, and it comes together with stuff you probably already have sitting around — ground beef, potatoes, frozen veggies, done. Miami winters may be mild, but this dish hits different on any cool evening.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
35 mins
Total
50 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 lb ground beef (or ground turkey if that's what you've got)
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn — whatever blend)
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1/3 cup milk or cream
  • 1/2 cup beef broth (or water with a bouillon cube)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Optional: shredded cheddar cheese for the top

Instructions

  1. 1Start by boiling your cubed potatoes in salted water over medium-high heat. You want them fork-tender, about 15 to 18 minutes. While those are going, you'll handle everything else — this is a multitask situation.
  2. 2Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for about 3 minutes until it softens up and turns a little translucent. Add the garlic and cook another minute — your kitchen should be smelling real good right now.
  3. 3Add the ground beef to the skillet. Break it up as it cooks and season it with cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. That cumin is the Improv Oven twist — it adds a warmth that takes this thing to another level. Cook until browned, about 6 to 8 minutes, then drain any excess fat.
  4. 4Stir in the tomato paste and Worcestershire sauce, coating all that meat. Pour in the beef broth, add your frozen vegetables, and stir everything together. Let it simmer on medium-low for about 5 minutes until the liquid reduces and the filling looks saucy but not soupy. Pull it off the heat.
  5. 5Preheat your oven to 400°F. Drain your cooked potatoes and mash them with butter, milk, salt, and pepper until creamy and smooth. Taste them — they should be well seasoned on their own because they're the crown of this whole dish.
  6. 6Spread the mashed potatoes over the meat filling in the skillet, going all the way to the edges so everything is sealed in. Use a fork to create some texture on top — those little ridges get golden and crispy in the oven. If you're adding cheese, sprinkle it on now.
  7. 7Bake uncovered at 400°F for 20 to 25 minutes until the top is lightly golden and the edges are bubbling. Let it rest for 5 minutes before serving — it'll be lava hot straight out of the oven and you'll thank yourself for waiting.
💡 Improv Tip

If your skillet isn't oven-safe, no stress — just transfer the meat filling to a greased baking dish, spoon the mashed potatoes on top, and bake it the same way. Also, leftover shepherd's pie reheats beautifully the next day, and honestly? Some people say it tastes even better. Make extra on purpose.
