Easy Freezable Meal Prep Soup with Black Beans and Sofrito

Published April 24, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you've been searching for easy meal prep soup recipes that are actually freezable and don't taste like cardboard after reheating, this one's going to change your Sunday game. It's a hearty black bean and vegetable soup built on a sofrito base — that aromatic combo of onion, garlic, peppers, and tomato that makes everything taste like somebody's abuela was involved. Make a big pot, freeze it in portions, and weeknight dinners basically handle themselves.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes with juices
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro for serving (optional)
  • Cooked white rice or crusty bread for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Once it's shimmering, add the diced onion and green bell pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, for about 5 minutes until the onion is soft and translucent and everything smells incredible.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, and red pepper flakes if you're using them. Stir everything together and let it cook for about 60 seconds — you're blooming those spices right into the sofrito base and the smell alone will have your neighbors knocking.
  3. 3Pour in the diced tomatoes with all their juices and stir to combine, scraping up any bits stuck to the bottom of the pot. Let this cook for 2 minutes so the tomatoes cook down slightly and meld with the sofrito.
  4. 4Add both cans of black beans and pour in all 4 cups of broth. Stir well, raise the heat to medium-high, and bring the soup to a boil.
  5. 5Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium-low and add the diced zucchini and frozen corn. Simmer uncovered for 12 to 15 minutes until the zucchini is tender and the soup has thickened slightly. Taste it — this is your moment to adjust salt.
  6. 6Remove from heat and squeeze in the lime juice. Give it one final stir. Serve hot over white rice or with crusty bread, topped with fresh cilantro if you have it. To freeze, let the soup cool completely, then ladle into freezer-safe containers or zip-lock bags in single or double portions. It keeps beautifully for up to 3 months.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to this soup tasting even better from the freezer than fresh is slightly undercooking the zucchini the day you make it — like, pull it just before you think it's done. That way when you reheat it from frozen it hits the perfect tender texture instead of turning to mush. And always add a fresh squeeze of lime after reheating to wake everything back up.
