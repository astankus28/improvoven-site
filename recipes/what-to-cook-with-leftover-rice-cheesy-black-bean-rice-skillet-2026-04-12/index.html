What to Cook with Leftover Rice: Cheesy Black Bean Rice Skillet

Published April 12, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you're staring into your fridge wondering what to cook with leftover rice, this cheesy black bean skillet is your answer — and it comes together in about 30 minutes with stuff you probably already have. We're talking crispy rice edges, melty cheese, and that smoky-cumin vibe that hits like your tía made it. Budget-friendly, deeply satisfying, and honestly better than ordering out.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 3 cups leftover cooked white or brown rice (cold is actually better here)
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 cup shredded Mexican blend or cheddar cheese
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Juice of half a lime
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, hot sauce, sliced scallions, fresh cilantro, avocado

Instructions

  1. 1Pull your leftover rice out of the fridge and break up any clumps with a fork. Cold rice is actually perfect for this — it fries up crispier and holds its shape better than fresh rice would. Set it aside.
  2. 2Heat the oil in a large skillet or cast iron pan over medium-high heat. Once it shimmers, add the diced onion and green bell pepper. Cook for about 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they soften and start picking up a little color on the edges.
  3. 3Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds, just until fragrant. Don't walk away here — garlic goes from golden to burnt fast and burnt garlic will wreck the whole thing.
  4. 4Stir in the cumin, smoked paprika, chili powder, salt, and black pepper. Let the spices toast in the oil with the vegetables for about 60 seconds. You'll smell them bloom — that's exactly what you want.
  5. 5Add the drained diced tomatoes and stir everything together. Let it cook for 2 minutes so the tomatoes reduce slightly and the flavors start to marry.
  6. 6Add the black beans and stir to combine. Cook another 2 minutes until the beans are heated through.
  7. 7Now add your cold leftover rice. Spread it out evenly across the pan and press it down lightly with the back of your spatula. Let it sit undisturbed for 2-3 minutes so the bottom layer gets crispy and a little golden. This is the magic step — resist the urge to stir.
  8. 8Once you hear that satisfying sizzle and the bottom is crisping up, give everything a good stir to mix the crispy bits throughout. Taste and adjust salt if needed, then squeeze the lime juice over the whole skillet.
  9. 9Reduce heat to medium-low. Spread the shredded cheese evenly over the top and cover the pan with a lid or foil for 2-3 minutes, just until the cheese melts completely.
  10. 10Serve straight from the skillet with whatever toppings you've got — sour cream, hot sauce, scallions, cilantro, or sliced avocado all work beautifully here. Bring the whole pan to the table and let people dig in.
💡 Improv Tip

The single biggest mistake people make with leftover rice dishes is adding the rice too early or stirring too much. Let that rice sit and get crispy on the bottom before you touch it — those golden bits are where all the texture and flavor live. If your rice is sticking in a big clump, just drizzle about a teaspoon of water over it before you add it to the pan and break it up with your hands first.
