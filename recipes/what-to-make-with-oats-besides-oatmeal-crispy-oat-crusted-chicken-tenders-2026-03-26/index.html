What to Make With Oats Besides Oatmeal: Crispy Oat-Crusted Chicken Tenders

Published March 26, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you've been wondering what to make with oats besides oatmeal, these crispy chicken tenders are about to change your whole relationship with that canister in your pantry. Ground oats make a crust that's crunchier than breadcrumbs and way more flavorful — especially when you hit it with some garlic, cumin, and a little smoked paprika. This is weeknight dinner energy with zero stress and a grocery bill that won't hurt your feelings.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs chicken breast, cut into strips
  • 1.5 cups old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 0.5 teaspoon onion powder
  • 0.5 teaspoon salt
  • 0.25 teaspoon black pepper
  • 0.25 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons hot sauce (any kind you have)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or neutral oil for drizzling

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 425°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper or foil. If you have a wire rack, set it on top of the baking sheet — that's going to help the bottom of your tenders get crispy instead of soggy.
  2. 2Add the rolled oats to a blender or food processor and pulse about 8 to 10 times until you get a coarse, crumbly texture. You're not going for oat flour here — you want some texture left so the crust has real crunch. If you don't have a blender, put the oats in a zip-lock bag and crush them with a rolling pin or the bottom of a heavy pan.
  3. 3Pour the blitzed oats into a shallow bowl or plate and mix in the garlic powder, smoked paprika, cumin, onion powder, salt, black pepper, and cayenne if you're using it. Give it a good stir so everything is evenly distributed.
  4. 4In a separate shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs and hot sauce until combined. The hot sauce is not just for flavor — the acidity helps the coating stick. Don't skip it.
  5. 5Pat your chicken strips dry with a paper towel — this is important. Wet chicken won't hold the crust. Dip each strip into the egg wash first, let any excess drip off, then press it firmly into the oat mixture on both sides. Really press it in — don't be shy.
  6. 6Arrange the coated tenders on your prepared baking sheet in a single layer without crowding them. Drizzle or lightly spray with oil over the tops. This is what gives you that golden, crispy finish without deep frying.
  7. 7Bake for 18 to 22 minutes, flipping once halfway through, until the crust is deep golden brown and the chicken is cooked through to 165°F internal temperature. Let them rest for 2 minutes before serving — that crust will firm up even more as they cool slightly.
  8. 8Serve with your favorite dipping sauce. A simple mix of mayo, lime juice, and a little sriracha takes about 30 seconds to make and goes perfectly with these.
💡 Improv Tip

Old-fashioned rolled oats work best here — quick oats are too fine and steel-cut are too hard. If your oat crust is falling off during baking, it usually means the chicken was still wet when you coated it or you skipped pressing the coating on firmly. Dry chicken plus firm pressure equals a crust that stays put. Also, don't crowd the pan — if the tenders are touching each other, they'll steam instead of crisp, and nobody came here for steamed chicken.
