Easy Chicken Enchiladas Recipe for Cinco de Mayo (That'll Make Everyone Ask for the Recipe)

Published April 16, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy chicken enchiladas recipe for Cinco de Mayo is the move when you want to bring serious flavor to the table without spending your whole paycheck or your whole Saturday in the kitchen. We're talking tender shredded chicken, melty cheese, and that deep red sauce all rolled up tight — the kind of dish that disappears before you even sit down. Whether you're hosting a crowd or just treating yourself, this one's got you covered.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
25 mins
Total
40 mins
Serves
4-6

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked shredded chicken (rotisserie is your best friend here)
  • 8-10 medium flour or corn tortillas
  • 2 cans (10 oz each) red enchilada sauce
  • 2 cups shredded Mexican blend or Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1/2 cup diced white onion
  • 1 can (4 oz) diced green chiles
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons sour cream (optional, for filling)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Sliced jalapeños for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F and lightly grease a 9x13 baking dish. Pour about 1/2 cup of enchilada sauce on the bottom and spread it around — this keeps everything from sticking and adds flavor from the jump.
  2. 2In a bowl, mix your shredded chicken with the diced onion, green chiles, cumin, garlic powder, chili powder, salt, pepper, and about 1/4 cup of enchilada sauce. If you want a little creaminess in the filling, stir in the sour cream now. Taste it — that filling should already be delicious on its own.
  3. 3If you're using corn tortillas, warm them one at a time in a dry skillet or wrap them in a damp paper towel and microwave for 30 seconds. This step is non-negotiable — cold corn tortillas will crack and fall apart on you. Flour tortillas are more forgiving and can go straight from the bag.
  4. 4Spoon about 3 tablespoons of the chicken filling down the center of each tortilla, add a pinch of shredded cheese, then roll it up tight and place it seam-side down in the baking dish. Repeat until you've filled the dish — nestle them in close together so they hold each other up.
  5. 5Pour the remaining enchilada sauce evenly over all the rolled tortillas, making sure to cover the ends so they don't dry out. Then hit them with the rest of the shredded cheese across the top.
  6. 6Cover the dish with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Then uncover and bake another 5 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and starting to get those golden spots. Pull it out and let it rest for 5 minutes — I know it's hard but don't skip this part or everything falls apart when you serve it.
  7. 7Garnish with fresh cilantro, sliced jalapeños, and a dollop of sour cream if you like. Serve hot with rice, beans, or just a cold drink and good company.
💡 Improv Tip

The real secret to enchiladas that taste like somebody's abuela made them? Season your filling generously and taste it before it ever hits the tortilla. Bland filling wrapped in sauce is still bland filling — you want every component to have flavor on its own. Also, if you've got leftover enchilada sauce, warm it up and serve it on the side for dipping. People go crazy for that.
