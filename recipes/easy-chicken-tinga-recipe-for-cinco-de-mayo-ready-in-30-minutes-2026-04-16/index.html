Easy Chicken Tinga Recipe for Cinco de Mayo (Ready in 30 Minutes)

Published April 16, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy chicken tinga recipe is your Cinco de Mayo MVP — smoky, saucy, a little spicy, and absolutely built for a crowd. We're talking shredded chicken simmered in a chipotle-tomato sauce that tastes like it took all day but comes together in half an hour. Make it into tacos, tostadas, rice bowls, whatever your fridge is telling you to do.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs (or breasts — thighs stay juicier)
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) fire-roasted diced tomatoes
  • 2-3 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, plus 1 tablespoon of the adobo sauce
  • 1 medium white onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth or water
  • Corn or flour tortillas for serving
  • Optional toppings: sliced avocado, crumbled queso fresco, pickled red onions, fresh cilantro, lime wedges

Instructions

  1. 1Season your chicken thighs on both sides with salt, cumin, smoked paprika, and oregano. Don't overthink it — just get some flavor on there.
  2. 2Heat the oil in a large skillet or sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and sear for about 3-4 minutes per side until golden. The chicken doesn't need to be fully cooked through yet — we're just building flavor. Remove and set aside.
  3. 3In the same pan, drop the heat to medium and add the sliced onions. Cook for about 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they soften and start to get a little golden on the edges.
  4. 4Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant. Your kitchen should be smelling incredible right now.
  5. 5While the onions cook, add the fire-roasted tomatoes, chipotle peppers, and adobo sauce to a blender. Blend until smooth. If you don't have a blender, chop the chipotles fine and mash everything together — it'll be a little chunkier but still delicious.
  6. 6Pour the blended chipotle-tomato sauce into the pan with the onions and garlic. Add the chicken broth and stir everything together. Bring it to a simmer.
  7. 7Nestle the seared chicken thighs back into the sauce. Cover the pan and let everything simmer together on medium-low heat for about 12-15 minutes, until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  8. 8Pull the chicken out and shred it using two forks — it should fall apart easily. Return the shredded chicken to the pan and toss it all together in that smoky sauce. Let it simmer uncovered for 3-4 more minutes so the chicken absorbs everything and the sauce tightens up a little.
  9. 9Taste it. Adjust salt. Add more adobo if you want more smoke and heat. Now pile it onto warm tortillas or tostadas and hit it with whatever toppings you've got. This is your Cinco de Mayo moment.
💡 Improv Tip

Chipotles in adobo are where all the magic lives in this dish — but they can sneak up on you. Start with 2 peppers if your crew is spice-sensitive, taste the sauce before adding the chicken back, and go from there. The can keeps in the fridge for weeks, so whatever you don't use, freeze individual peppers in a zip-lock and pull them out next time you need a smoky punch in a sauce, soup, or marinade. That one can will carry you through at least four or five different meals.
