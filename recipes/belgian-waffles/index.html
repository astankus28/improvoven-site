Belgian Waffles Recipe

Improv Oven Classic Recipe

Crispy on the outside, fluffy inside—these Belgian waffles are easier than you think and way cheaper than brunch. We're talking melted butter, separated eggs for maximum lift, and whatever berries you've got in the kitchen. Drizzle with honey and you're done.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 2 large eggs, separated
  • 1.5 cups milk
  • 1 cup butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • handful of fresh blueberries. raspberries, strawberries, or blackberries
  • honey or syrup

Instructions

  1. 1Separate eggs placing yolks in one bowl and egg whites on another bowl. Beat egg whites until stiff peaks form.
  2. 2In a separate bowl, combine flour, sugar and baking powder.
  3. 3In the third bowl, lightly beat egg yolks. Add milk, melted butter and vanilla extract; mix well. Stir into dry ingredients just until combined. Fold into combined batter.
  4. 4Baked in preheated waffle iron according to manufacturer directions or until golden brown
  5. 5Fold in beaten egg whites.
💡 Improv Tip

Don't skip separating the eggs. Beating those whites until stiff peaks form is what makes these waffles fluffy instead of dense. Fold them in gently at the end to keep all that air intact.
