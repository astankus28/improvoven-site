Easy Chocolate Mug Cake Recipe (Ready in 2 Minutes!)

Published April 6, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy chocolate mug cake recipe is the one you pull out when the chocolate craving hits hard and you are not trying to wash a single dish. We are talking pantry staples, one mug, and two minutes in the microwave — that is it. It is the kind of dessert that makes you feel like you figured out a cheat code.

Prep
2 mins
Cook
2 mins
Total
4 mins
Serves
1

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 4 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 egg
  • 3 tablespoons milk
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil or melted butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • pinch of salt
  • 2 tablespoons chocolate chips (optional but highly recommended)

Instructions

  1. 1Grab your biggest mug — at least 12 oz — because this cake puffs up and you do not want a microwave situation on your hands.
  2. 2Add the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, and salt directly into the mug and give it a quick stir with a fork to combine the dry ingredients evenly.
  3. 3Crack in the egg, pour in the milk, oil, and vanilla extract, then mix everything together until smooth and no dry streaks remain. Scrape the bottom of the mug because the flour loves to hide down there.
  4. 4Fold in the chocolate chips if you are using them — and listen, use them. This is not the moment to hold back.
  5. 5Microwave on high for 60 to 90 seconds. Start checking at 60 seconds. The cake is done when the top looks set but still has a very slight glossy center — that is your fudgy sweet spot. Every microwave is different so if it looks wet at 60 seconds give it 15 more.
  6. 6Let it sit for 30 seconds before eating because it will be volcano hot. Top with a scoop of ice cream, a spoonful of peanut butter, or just eat it straight from the mug like the legend you are.
💡 Improv Tip

Do not overcook it — that is the number one mug cake mistake. A slightly underdone center will continue cooking from the residual heat and stay perfectly fudgy. If you microwave it until the top looks completely dry and set all the way through, you will end up with something closer to a chocolate hockey puck and we are not doing that to ourselves.
