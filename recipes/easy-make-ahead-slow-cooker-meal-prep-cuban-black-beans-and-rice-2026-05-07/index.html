Easy Make-Ahead Slow Cooker Meal Prep: Cuban Black Beans and Rice

Published May 7, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you've been sleeping on easy make-ahead slow cooker meal prep, this Cuban black bean recipe is about to change your whole week. We're talking a big, fragrant pot of sofrito-scented black beans that you set in the morning and forget about until dinner — then portion out for days of real, satisfying meals. This is the kind of cooking my abuela would approve of: cheap, filling, and way better than anything you're reheating from a box.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
8 hours
Total
8 hours 15 mins
Serves
8

Ingredients

  • 1 lb dried black beans, rinsed and soaked overnight (or 2 cans black beans, drained — see tips)
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar (added at the end)
  • Cooked white or brown rice, for serving
  • Optional toppings: sliced green onions, a squeeze of lime, hot sauce, a spoonful of sour cream

Instructions

  1. 1The night before, rinse your dried black beans and cover them with a few inches of cold water in a large bowl. Let them soak overnight for at least 8 hours. This cuts down cooking time and makes them way easier to digest. Drain and rinse them the next morning.
  2. 2In the morning, add the soaked and drained black beans to your slow cooker. Toss in the diced onion, green bell pepper, and minced garlic — that's your base sofrito right there, no extra pan required.
  3. 3Pour in the diced tomatoes (juice and all), the broth, and the water. Add the cumin, oregano, smoked paprika, bay leaves, olive oil, a generous pinch of salt, and some black pepper. Give everything a good stir so all those flavors get acquainted.
  4. 4Put the lid on, set your slow cooker to LOW, and walk away for 8 hours. Yes, really. Go to work, run your errands, or just live your life. By the time you come back, your kitchen is going to smell incredible.
  5. 5When the beans are completely tender and the broth has thickened into a rich, dark sauce, fish out the bay leaves and stir in the apple cider vinegar. This little splash at the end brightens up the whole pot — don't skip it. Taste and adjust salt if needed.
  6. 6Serve a big scoop over rice right away, or let the beans cool completely before dividing them into meal prep containers. They'll keep in the fridge for up to 5 days or in the freezer for up to 3 months. When you're ready to eat, just reheat with a splash of water or broth to loosen them up.
💡 Improv Tip

Short on time or forgot to soak beans? Use two cans of drained black beans instead — just reduce the water to 1 cup since canned beans don't need as much liquid, and cut the cook time to 4 hours on LOW or 2 hours on HIGH. The flavor won't be quite as deep as dried, but listen, a good meal on a Tuesday beats a perfect meal that never happens. Also, double this batch if your slow cooker fits it — these beans freeze beautifully in individual portions and future-you will be genuinely grateful.
