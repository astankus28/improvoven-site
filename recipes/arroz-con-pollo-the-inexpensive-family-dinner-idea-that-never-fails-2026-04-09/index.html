Arroz con Pollo: The Inexpensive Family Dinner Idea That Never Fails

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you need inexpensive family dinner ideas that actually get everyone to the table excited, arroz con pollo is your ride-or-die — one pot, a few pantry staples, and you're feeding four people for under ten bucks. This is the dish Miami grandmas built empires on, and once you make it, you'll understand why. It's humble, it's deeply flavorful, and it makes your whole kitchen smell like someone loves you.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
35 mins
Total
45 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs, bone-in skin-on (the cheapest cut and the most flavorful)
  • 1 1/2 cups long grain white rice
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 1/4 cups chicken broth (or water with 1 bouillon cube)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional but beautiful)

Instructions

  1. 1Pat your chicken thighs dry with a paper towel — this is the move for getting good color. Season them generously on both sides with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and smoked paprika. Don't be shy with it.
  2. 2Heat your oil in a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Once it shimmers, lay the chicken thighs skin-side down and don't touch them for 4-5 minutes. You want that golden-brown crust. Flip and cook another 3 minutes on the other side, then remove them to a plate. They're not fully cooked yet — that's fine, they're going back in.
  3. 3Turn the heat down to medium. In the same pot with all those gorgeous chicken drippings, add your diced onion and green bell pepper. Cook for about 3-4 minutes until softened, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom — that's pure flavor right there.
  4. 4Add the minced garlic, cumin, and oregano. Stir everything together and let it cook for about 60 seconds until fragrant. Your kitchen should be smelling incredible right now.
  5. 5Pour in the diced tomatoes (with their juices) and stir to combine. Let it cook for 2 minutes to meld everything together.
  6. 6Add the rice to the pot and stir it into the tomato and veggie mixture, coating every grain. Toast it for about a minute — this step adds a subtle nutty depth you don't want to skip.
  7. 7Pour in the chicken broth and stir everything together. Taste the liquid and adjust salt if needed — this is your only chance to season the rice as it cooks.
  8. 8Nestle the chicken thighs back into the pot, skin-side up, pressing them gently into the rice. Bring everything to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover tightly, and cook for 20-22 minutes. Do not lift that lid — you'll let the steam escape and end up with crunchy rice, and we're not doing that.
  9. 9After 20 minutes, check that the rice is cooked and the chicken is done (internal temp of 165°F or juices run clear). Scatter the frozen peas over the top, put the lid back on, and let everything sit off the heat for 5 minutes. The peas will warm through perfectly without getting mushy.
  10. 10Fluff the rice gently with a fork, garnish with fresh cilantro if you've got it, and bring the whole pot straight to the table. Serve it family-style — that's the whole point.
💡 Improv Tip

Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs are almost always the cheapest chicken at the store AND the most flavorful — the bone and fat do the work for you during cooking. If you only have boneless thighs or even drumsticks, you can absolutely use them, just adjust cook time slightly. And if your rice ever comes out a little wet, just leave the lid off for 3-4 minutes on low heat — it'll dry right out. You got this.
