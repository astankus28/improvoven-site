Grilled Steak Fajitas Recipe

Improv Oven Classic Recipe

These grilled steak fajitas prove you don't need a restaurant bill to eat well. A quick tenderizing trick makes budget-friendly flank steak tender and delicious, while a simple spice blend brings all the flavor. Dinner for four on the table in 35 minutes.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 whole flank steak
  • For the Steak Seasoning
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 tbs olive oil
  • For the Skillet
  • 1 tbs olive oil
  • 1 whole tomato, sliced
  • 1 half red onion, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 8-10 flour tortillas

Instructions

  1. 1In a separate bowl, mix together all seasoning ingredients and set aside
  2. 2On a butcher block or cutting board, beat the flank steak with a meat tenderizer or meat hammer lightly until meat begins to widen and soften. With a kitchen knife cut small incisions against the grain on the surface of the meat to break apart remaining membrane or fat. Most store bought cuts of flank a slightly tough so tenderizing the meat will make it easier to cook and easier to eat.
  3. 3Add steak to the seasoning bowl and make sure seasoning completely coats the steak with your hands. Set aside for a about 10 minutes.
  4. 4On a skillet on medium high heat, add olive oil until it begins to smoke, add tomato, peppers, and onions and cook until translucent. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. 5Add steak to the skillet cooking each side about 5 minutes.
  6. 6Remove steak from skillet and slice in strips.
  7. 7Serve together on tortillas
💡 Improv Tip

Tenderizing the flank steak and scoring against the grain makes a huge difference—it breaks down tough fibers so your meat cooks evenly and stays tender. Don't skip this step.
