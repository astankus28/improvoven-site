Easy Homemade Chili Chocolate Bark

Published March 28, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you've been searching for a homemade chili recipe easy enough to turn into dessert, this one's going to blow your mind — sweet, spicy, and completely pantry-friendly. It's that classic Mexican chocolate vibe, where chili powder and dark chocolate become best friends. This is the kind of treat you whip up in 20 minutes and people think you're some kind of genius.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
10 mins
Total
20 mins
Serves
8

Ingredients

  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips or chopped dark chocolate
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (less if you're sensitive to heat)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt or flaky salt for topping
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts or pepitas (optional but highly recommended)
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil or vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. 1Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set it aside. You want this ready before the chocolate because once it melts, things move fast.
  2. 2In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the chocolate chips and coconut oil. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between each one, until the chocolate is fully melted and smooth — usually takes about 90 seconds total. Don't rush this or you'll scorch it.
  3. 3Stir in the chili powder, cinnamon, cayenne, and vanilla extract directly into the melted chocolate. Give it a good mix so the spices are fully incorporated and evenly distributed.
  4. 4Pour the spiced chocolate mixture onto your prepared parchment-lined baking sheet and use a spatula to spread it out into a rough rectangle about 1/4 inch thick. It doesn't need to be perfect — rustic is charming.
  5. 5Immediately scatter the roasted peanuts or pepitas over the top while the chocolate is still wet, then finish with a generous pinch of flaky sea salt. The salt is not optional in my house — it makes everything pop.
  6. 6Let the bark set at room temperature for about 30 minutes if your kitchen is cool, or slide it into the refrigerator for 15 minutes to speed things up. Once it's fully hardened, break it into irregular pieces with your hands and serve.
💡 Improv Tip

Taste your chocolate mixture before you pour it out — seriously, just drag a spoon through it. The chili heat blooms more as the chocolate cools, so if it tastes perfectly spiced when it's warm, it'll be a little more intense once it sets. Start with less cayenne than you think you need, then build up next time once you know your crowd.
