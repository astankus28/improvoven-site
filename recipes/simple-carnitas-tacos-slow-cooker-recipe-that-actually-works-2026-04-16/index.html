Simple Carnitas Tacos (Slow Cooker Recipe That Actually Works)

Published April 16, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple carnitas tacos slow cooker recipe is the move when you want that crispy-edged, fall-apart pork without standing over a stove all day. Throw everything in the pot in the morning and by dinner time your whole kitchen smells like a taqueria. We finish them under the broiler for that golden crisp that makes people think you really did something special.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
8 hours
Total
8 hours 15 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs pork shoulder (bone-in or boneless, whatever's on sale)
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1.5 teaspoons salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 orange, juiced (about 1/3 cup)
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 small white onion, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth or water
  • 12 small corn tortillas
  • 1/2 white onion, finely diced (for serving)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped (for serving)
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges (for serving)
  • Your favorite salsa (for serving)

Instructions

  1. 1Mix together the cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, oregano, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Pat your pork shoulder dry with a paper towel — this helps the spices stick and the pork sear better later.
  2. 2Rub that spice mix all over the pork shoulder like you mean it. Get into every corner. Place it in the slow cooker fat-side up.
  3. 3Scatter the smashed garlic cloves and chopped onion around the pork. Pour the orange juice, lime juice, and chicken broth over everything. The liquid should come up about an inch on the sides — don't drown the pork.
  4. 4Cover and cook on LOW for 8 hours or HIGH for 5 hours. You want it so tender it basically falls apart when you look at it wrong.
  5. 5When the pork is done, pull it out onto a large baking sheet. Use two forks to shred it right there on the pan. Don't discard that liquid in the slow cooker — you'll need it.
  6. 6Ladle about half a cup of the cooking liquid over the shredded pork and toss it all together. Spread the pork into an even layer across the baking sheet.
  7. 7Set your oven to broil on HIGH. Slide the baking sheet under the broiler for 4 to 6 minutes, watching closely, until the edges of the pork get golden and a little crispy. This step is non-negotiable — it's what separates good carnitas from great carnitas.
  8. 8Warm your corn tortillas directly over a gas flame or in a dry skillet until they have a little char on them. Stack them two at a time for each taco.
  9. 9Pile on the crispy carnitas, top with diced white onion, fresh cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and whatever salsa you've got. That's it — you're done and you're a hero.
💡 Improv Tip

Pork shoulder is almost always cheaper per pound than pork loin, and it actually works better here because the fat melts down during the long cook and keeps everything juicy. If your grocery store has a markdown section for meat, pork shoulder is your best friend — it freezes beautifully and this recipe is the perfect destination for it. Also, don't skip the broiler step even if you're tired — that 5 minutes is the whole difference between taco Tuesday and taco legend.
