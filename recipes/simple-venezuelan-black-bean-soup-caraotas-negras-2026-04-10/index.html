Simple Venezuelan Black Bean Soup (Caraotas Negras)

Published April 10, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Venezuelan black bean soup — caraotas negras — is the kind of thing that makes you feel like someone's abuela is looking out for you. It comes together in about 30 minutes using pantry staples you probably already have, and it hits that deep, savory, slightly smoky note that makes a bowl of beans feel like a whole meal. Serve it with white rice and a squeeze of lime and you'll wonder why you ever ordered delivery.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
  • 1 small green bell pepper, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or neutral oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar or panela (the Venezuelan secret)
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar or red wine vinegar
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional but don't skip it)
  • White rice and lime wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the oil in a medium saucepan or pot over medium heat. Once it shimmers, add the diced onion and green pepper. Cook for 5 to 7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are soft and the onion is translucent — you want them sweet and tender, not browned.
  2. 2Add the minced garlic, cumin, oregano, and smoked paprika. Stir everything together and let it cook for about 60 seconds until the garlic is fragrant. This is your sofrito base and it's doing the heavy lifting flavor-wise, so don't rush it.
  3. 3Pour in one full can of black beans along with all the broth. Stir to combine and bring the mixture up to a gentle boil.
  4. 4While the soup is heating, use a fork or the back of a spoon to roughly mash the second can of drained beans in a small bowl — you want a chunky paste, not a smooth puree. Stir this into the pot. This technique is classic for Venezuelan caraotas and it gives the soup that thick, velvety texture without a blender.
  5. 5Add the brown sugar and vinegar, then stir well. The sugar balances the earthiness of the beans and the vinegar adds brightness — together they're what makes this taste distinctly Venezuelan rather than just any black bean soup. Season generously with salt and black pepper.
  6. 6Reduce heat to medium-low and let the soup simmer uncovered for 10 to 12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens to your liking. If it gets too thick, splash in a little more broth or water.
  7. 7Taste one more time for seasoning — adjust salt, vinegar, or sugar as needed. Ladle into bowls over white rice, hit it with a squeeze of fresh lime, and top with cilantro if you have it. Done.
💡 Improv Tip

The brown sugar and vinegar combo is non-negotiable — that sweet-sour balance is what separates Venezuelan caraotas from every other black bean soup out there. If you have panela (raw cane sugar) in your pantry, use it instead of brown sugar and it'll taste even more authentic. No vinegar? A little squeeze of lime stirred in at the end does the job beautifully.
