Homemade Marinara Sauce

Improv Oven Classic · Originally published September 2017

Once you make marinara from scratch, going back to the jar feels like a waste of money. This version uses just 7 pantry ingredients — the most important being a can of San Marzano tomato puree (we use Cento). Simmer it for 30 minutes for a quick weeknight sauce, or let it go for an hour and it gets deep and rich. Works for pasta, pizza, fried chicken dipping, or our arancini recipe.

Prep
5 mins
Cook
30 mins
Total
35 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 can (28 oz) San Marzano tomato puree
  • 1 cup water (use the empty can)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. 1Heat olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium-low heat. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant — don't let it brown.
  2. 2Pour in the tomato puree. Fill the empty can with 1 cup of water, swish to get all remaining puree, and add that to the pot too.
  3. 3Add basil, parsley, sugar, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together.
  4. 4Simmer on medium-low heat for 30–60 minutes, stirring occasionally. The longer it simmers the richer it gets.
  5. 5Taste and adjust seasoning. Use immediately or refrigerate for up to 5 days.
💡 Improv Tip

The can trick is key — adding water to the empty puree can and swishing it out means you're getting every last drop of flavor. Use Cento or any San Marzano brand for best results. This sauce freezes perfectly for up to 3 months, so make a double batch and thank yourself later.
