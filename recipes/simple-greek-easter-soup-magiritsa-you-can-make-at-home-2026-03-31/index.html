Simple Greek Easter Soup (Magiritsa) You Can Make at Home

Published March 31, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Greek Easter soup — called magiritsa — is the traditional lamb and egg-lemon broth that Greek families have been breaking their Lenten fast with for centuries, and once you try it you'll understand why. It's silky, bright, deeply savory, and surprisingly easy to pull off even if you've never cooked Greek food a day in your life. We're keeping it approachable and budget-friendly without losing any of that magic.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
45 mins
Total
1 hour
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb lamb shoulder or lamb stew meat, cut into small pieces
  • 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 3 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup long-grain white rice
  • 6 cups chicken broth or water with 2 bouillon cubes
  • 3 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (about 2 lemons)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried)
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: lamb liver or giblets, finely chopped, for a more traditional version

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the lamb pieces and season generously with salt and pepper. Brown them on all sides for about 5 minutes — you want some color on that meat because that's where the flavor starts.
  2. 2Toss in the yellow onion and garlic and cook for another 3 minutes, stirring, until the onion softens and everything smells incredible. Add the green onions and give it one more minute.
  3. 3Pour in the broth and bring it up to a boil. Once boiling, reduce to a steady simmer, cover the pot, and let the lamb cook for about 25 minutes until it's tender and the broth has gotten rich and fragrant.
  4. 4Stir in the rice and the fresh dill and parsley. Let it simmer uncovered for another 15 minutes until the rice is cooked through. Taste the broth and adjust salt if needed.
  5. 5Now here's the move that makes this soup iconic — the avgolemono finish. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and lemon juice until frothy and well combined. Slowly ladle about a cup of the hot broth into the egg-lemon mixture while whisking constantly. This tempers the eggs so they don't scramble.
  6. 6Pour that tempered egg-lemon mixture back into the pot while stirring gently. Turn the heat down to low and stir for about 2 minutes. Do not let it boil after this point — just let it warm through and thicken into that gorgeous, silky, lemony broth magiritsa is famous for.
  7. 7Serve immediately with crusty bread on the side and extra fresh dill on top if you have it.
💡 Improv Tip

The avgolemono step scares people but it's all about patience — ladle that hot broth into your eggs slowly and keep that whisk moving. If your soup sits and gets too thick, just add a splash of warm broth to loosen it back up. And honestly, if you can't find lamb, this works beautifully with bone-in chicken thighs too — not traditional, but delicious, and your wallet will thank you.
