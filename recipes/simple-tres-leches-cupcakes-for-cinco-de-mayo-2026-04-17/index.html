Simple Tres Leches Cupcakes for Cinco de Mayo

Published April 17, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple tres leches cupcakes recipe is your Cinco de Mayo secret weapon — individual little soaked cakes that are easier to make than a full sheet cake and way more fun to hand out. The tres leches magic is all there: fluffy vanilla cake drenched in a sweet milk trio, topped with a cloud of whipped cream. Make a batch the night before and watch them disappear before the afternoon is over.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
40 mins plus 2 hours chilling
Serves
12 cupcakes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large eggs, separated
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar, divided
  • 1/3 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup whole milk or heavy cream (for the soak)
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (for the whipped topping)
  • Ground cinnamon or a maraschino cherry for garnish (optional but festive)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F and line a standard 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners. Give the liners a quick spray with cooking spray — these cakes are moist and you don't want them sticking.
  2. 2In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set it aside.
  3. 3Separate your eggs into two bowls. Beat the yolks with 1/2 cup of the granulated sugar until the mixture is pale yellow and thick, about 2 minutes. Stir in the 1/3 cup of whole milk and vanilla extract.
  4. 4In a clean bowl (no yolk in there or it won't whip), beat the egg whites with a hand mixer on medium-high until foamy. Gradually add the remaining 1/4 cup sugar and keep beating until you get soft, glossy peaks — like shaving cream.
  5. 5Fold the flour mixture into the yolk mixture gently until just combined. Then fold in the egg whites in two additions, keeping as much air as you can. The batter will be light and a little fluffy.
  6. 6Divide the batter evenly among the 12 liners, filling each about two-thirds full. Bake for 18 to 20 minutes, until the tops are golden and a toothpick comes out clean. Let them cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer to a rimmed baking sheet — you'll need that rim for the soaking step.
  7. 7While the cupcakes cool, whisk together the evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and the 1/2 cup of whole milk or cream in a bowl or large measuring cup. This is your tres leches soak.
  8. 8Using a toothpick or a skewer, poke each cupcake 8 to 10 times all over the top. Don't be shy — you want those holes to let the milk mixture in. Slowly spoon about 2 to 3 tablespoons of the milk soak over each cupcake, letting it absorb before adding more. Some will pool on the baking sheet and that's fine.
  9. 9Cover the cupcakes loosely with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight if you're prepping for a party. The cold time is where the magic happens — the cake soaks up all that milky goodness and becomes incredibly tender.
  10. 10When you're ready to serve, beat the heavy whipping cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla together until you get firm, spreadable peaks. Dollop or pipe a generous swirl on top of each cupcake. Dust with a pinch of cinnamon or drop on a cherry if you want that classic tres leches look. Serve cold.
💡 Improv Tip

The soak is the whole soul of this recipe, so don't rush it — if you can make these the night before your Cinco de Mayo lunch spread, do it. They get better the longer they sit in the fridge. Also, if you only have one can of evaporated milk in the pantry, you can swap the 1/2 cup whole milk in the soak for a splash of half-and-half or even a little coconut milk for a Caribbean twist that honestly slaps.
