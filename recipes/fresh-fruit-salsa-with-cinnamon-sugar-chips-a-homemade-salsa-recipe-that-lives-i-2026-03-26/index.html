Fresh Fruit Salsa with Cinnamon Sugar Chips (A Homemade Salsa Recipe That Lives in Dessert Territory)

Published March 26, 2026 · Improv Oven

This homemade salsa recipe with fresh fruit is the move when you want something sweet, bright, and a little unexpected — think mango, strawberry, and a hit of lime all tumbling together in a bowl. It's the kind of thing you throw together in ten minutes and people act like you spent all day on it. Serve it with crispy cinnamon sugar tortilla chips and watch it disappear.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
8 mins
Total
18 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, diced small
  • 1 cup fresh or thawed frozen mango, diced small
  • 1 kiwi, peeled and diced small
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey or agave
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint or fresh basil, finely chopped
  • Pinch of salt
  • 6 small flour tortillas
  • 3 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Optional: pinch of cayenne for a little Miami heat

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 400°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This is your chip situation — do not skip this step, because the chips are half the magic.
  2. 2Mix the cinnamon and sugar together in a small bowl. Brush both sides of each tortilla with melted butter, then sprinkle the cinnamon sugar generously over the top side.
  3. 3Stack the tortillas and cut them like a pizza into 8 wedges each. Lay them out in a single layer on the baking sheet — no overlapping or they'll steam instead of crisp.
  4. 4Bake for 7 to 9 minutes until they're golden and crispy. They firm up even more as they cool, so don't panic if they feel slightly soft when you pull them out. Let them cool on the pan.
  5. 5While the chips are doing their thing, dice all your fruit into small, pretty pieces — uniformity matters here because every chip scoop should get a little bit of everything.
  6. 6Combine the strawberries, mango, and kiwi in a bowl. Add the lime juice, honey, fresh mint, and a small pinch of salt. Toss it gently and taste it — adjust the honey or lime until it sings to you.
  7. 7If you're feeling bold, add that pinch of cayenne. It sounds wild but it is absolutely correct. That sweet-heat combo is very Miami and very right.
  8. 8Let the fruit salsa sit for at least 5 minutes so the juices meld and everything gets friendly. Serve it alongside your cinnamon chips and watch people lose their minds over how simple this was.
💡 Improv Tip

Frozen mango works just as well as fresh here — just thaw it completely and pat it dry so your salsa doesn't get watery. This is the Improv Oven way: work with what you've got. No kiwi? Use pineapple, peaches, or even diced apple. The formula is one berry, one tropical fruit, lots of lime, and something fresh and green. You can't mess it up.
