Budget-Friendly Pernil Pork Shoulder Recipe (Crispy Skin and All)

Published April 11, 2026 · Improv Oven

This budget-friendly pernil pork shoulder recipe is the real deal — slow-roasted, deeply seasoned, and falling-apart tender with that crackling skin everybody fights over at the table. Pernil is one of those magical dishes that takes cheap ingredients and turns them into something that tastes like it cost way more than it did. Whether it's Sunday dinner or a special occasion, this one's going in your regular rotation.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
5 hrs
Total
5 hrs 20 mins (plus overnight marinade if you can)
Serves
8

Ingredients

  • 5-7 lb bone-in pork shoulder (ask the butcher for pernil cut — skin on)
  • 8 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 2 teaspoons black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons kosher salt
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil (or any neutral oil you have)
  • 3 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 1 packet Sazón con achiote (optional but highly recommended — about 50 cents at any Latin market)
  • 1 tablespoon adobo seasoning
  • 1 medium onion, sliced into rings
  • 1 cup water or chicken broth (for the roasting pan)

Instructions

  1. 1Make your marinade (sofrito paste): In a small bowl, mix together the garlic, oregano, cumin, black pepper, salt, olive oil, white vinegar, Sazón, and adobo until it forms a thick paste. This is the soul of your pernil — taste it and adjust salt if needed.
  2. 2Score the pork: Using a sharp knife, cut deep slits all over the pork shoulder — go about an inch deep. If the skin is on, score the skin in a crosshatch pattern without cutting into the meat. These pockets are where all that flavor lives.
  3. 3Stuff and coat: Use your fingers to push the garlic paste deep into every slit you made. Then rub the remaining paste all over the outside of the pork, getting under the skin wherever you can. Don't be shy — get in there.
  4. 4Marinate: Place the pork in a roasting pan or large zip-lock bag and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, but overnight is where the magic really happens. If you're doing it same-day, even 2 hours helps.
  5. 5Bring it to room temp: About 45 minutes before you're ready to cook, pull the pork out of the fridge and let it sit on the counter. Cold meat goes into the oven = uneven cooking. Don't skip this.
  6. 6Set up your roasting pan: Preheat your oven to 325°F. Scatter the sliced onions on the bottom of your roasting pan and pour in the water or broth. Place the pork skin-side up on top of the onions. Cover tightly with foil.
  7. 7Low and slow: Roast covered at 325°F for about 1 hour per pound. For a 6 lb shoulder, that's roughly 5.5 to 6 hours. You'll know it's ready when the meat is fall-apart tender and a fork slides in with zero resistance.
  8. 8Get that crackling skin (the best part): Once the meat is tender, crank the oven up to 450°F, remove the foil, and roast uncovered for another 20-30 minutes until the skin is deeply golden, blistered, and crackling. Watch it closely during this step — it goes from perfect to burnt fast.
  9. 9Rest before you cut: Pull it out and let it rest uncovered for at least 15-20 minutes. This keeps all those juices inside the meat where they belong. Use this time to make rice and beans, warn your family dinner is almost ready, and bask in the smell.
  10. 10Serve it up: Pull the pork apart with two forks or your hands (careful, it's hot). Serve with white rice, black beans, tostones, or just a pile of it on a plate with some hot sauce. Pour the pan drippings over the top — that's liquid gold right there.
💡 Improv Tip

The biggest secret to pernil that actually tastes like something? Don't rush the marinade. If you can season it the night before and let it sit in the fridge overnight, you will taste the difference — every single time. The salt and garlic penetrate the meat as it rests and you end up with flavor all the way through, not just on the surface. Also, bone-in is non-negotiable if you can help it — the bone adds richness to the meat and keeps it moist during that long roast.
