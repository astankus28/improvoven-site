Easy Healthy Meal Prep Breakfast Burritos You'll Actually Look Forward To

Published April 24, 2026 · Improv Oven

These easy healthy meal prep breakfast burritos are the cure for that sad 'nothing to eat' feeling on a Tuesday morning when you're already running late. We're loading them up with eggs, black beans, roasted veggies, and just enough pepper jack to make it exciting — then wrapping the whole thing tight so future-you has a real breakfast waiting in the freezer. Miami mornings move fast, and these burritos move with you.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 6 large flour tortillas (burrito size)
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup frozen diced peppers and onions (or 1 fresh bell pepper + half an onion, diced)
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • 1/2 cup shredded pepper jack or cheddar cheese
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or neutral cooking oil
  • Optional: hot sauce, salsa, or fresh cilantro for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add your frozen peppers and onions and the corn — don't stress if they're still frozen, they'll cook down fast. Season with cumin, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for about 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything is softened and starting to get a little golden on the edges.
  2. 2Add the drained black beans to the skillet and stir them in with the veggies. Cook for another 2 minutes just to warm them through and let them soak up all those spices. Transfer the whole veggie-bean mixture to a large bowl and set aside.
  3. 3Wipe the skillet out quickly, add the remaining tablespoon of oil, and reduce heat to medium. Crack all 8 eggs into a bowl, whisk them well with a pinch of salt, then pour them into the skillet. Scramble low and slow — we want soft, fluffy eggs that won't get rubbery after reheating. Pull them off the heat when they're just barely set, maybe even a tiny bit underdone, because they'll finish cooking when you reheat the burritos.
  4. 4Warm your tortillas one at a time in a dry skillet or microwave for 20 seconds so they're pliable and don't crack when you roll them. Lay one flat and add a scoop of the veggie-bean mixture, a portion of scrambled eggs, and a generous pinch of shredded cheese down the center. Don't overfill — leave about 2 inches on each side.
  5. 5Fold the sides of the tortilla in first, then roll from the bottom up, pulling it tight as you go. Place the burrito seam-side down. Repeat with all 6 tortillas.
  6. 6For meal prep: let the burritos cool completely, then wrap each one individually in aluminum foil. Store in the fridge for up to 4 days, or place wrapped burritos in a zip-top freezer bag and freeze for up to 2 months. To reheat from the fridge, microwave in the foil for 1.5-2 minutes. From frozen, thaw overnight in the fridge or microwave on defrost for 3-4 minutes, then full power for 1-2 minutes until hot all the way through.
💡 Improv Tip

The move that separates a sad soggy burrito from a great one is letting everything cool down before you wrap it — hot steam trapped inside is the enemy of good texture. Give your eggs and veggies even just 10 minutes on the counter before you start rolling. Also, that foil wrap isn't optional decoration: it holds the shape in the freezer and helps it reheat more evenly. If you want to go next level, do a quick toast on a dry skillet after reheating to crisp up the outside — game changer.
