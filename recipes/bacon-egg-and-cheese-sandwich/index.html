Bacon Egg and Cheese Sandwich

Improv Oven Classic · Originally published February 2018

Four ingredients. Ten minutes. This bacon egg and cheese on a croissant is the breakfast sandwich we make on repeat. Crispy bacon, soft scrambled eggs with melted cheddar, all tucked into a buttery croissant. Use fresh croissants from the bakery section — it makes a real difference. Everything else is negotiable, but the bacon is not.

Prep
5 mins
Cook
10 mins
Total
10 mins
Serves
2

Ingredients

  • 4 strips bacon, cut into pieces
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 fresh croissant, sliced in half
  • 1 slice cheddar cheese

Instructions

  1. 1Fry bacon in a skillet over medium heat until crispy, about 5–10 minutes. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  2. 2Beat eggs in a bowl. Pour into the same pan over medium heat. As eggs begin to set, gently pull and fold across the pan to form soft curds. Continue until no liquid remains, about 2 minutes — don't stir constantly, let them set between folds.
  3. 3Lay the cheddar slice on top of the eggs while still in the pan. Remove from heat and let the cheese melt from the residual heat.
  4. 4Place the bottom half of the croissant on a flat surface. Layer on the cheesy eggs and crispy bacon. Cap with the top half. Serve hot.
💡 Improv Tip

The fold-and-pull method for the eggs is the move — it gives you soft, creamy curds instead of rubbery scrambled eggs. Don't rush it over high heat. And if you want to level it up, add a smear of hot sauce or a thin slice of avocado before closing the sandwich.
