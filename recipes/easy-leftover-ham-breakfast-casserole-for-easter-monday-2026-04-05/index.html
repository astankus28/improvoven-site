Easy Leftover Ham Breakfast Casserole for Easter Monday

Published April 5, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy leftover ham breakfast casserole is exactly what Easter Monday was made for — you've got half a ham sitting in the fridge and zero motivation to cook something complicated, and honestly? That's the perfect setup. We're talking a cheesy, eggy, golden bake that uses up everything from yesterday's feast and has your whole family coming back for seconds before the coffee even finishes brewing.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
40 mins
Total
50 mins
Serves
6

Ingredients

  • 2 cups leftover ham, diced into small cubes
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk (or whatever milk you have)
  • 2 cups day-old bread, torn into chunks (rolls, sandwich bread, anything works)
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded cheese, divided (cheddar, colby jack, or a mix)
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter
  • Optional: hot sauce or sliced scallions for serving

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F and lightly grease a 9x13 baking dish or any similar oven-safe dish you have on hand.
  2. 2Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and bell pepper and cook for about 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and stir for another 30 seconds until fragrant. Remove from heat.
  3. 3In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, cumin, smoked paprika, a good pinch of salt, and several cracks of black pepper until everything is fully combined.
  4. 4Add the torn bread chunks to the egg mixture and press them down gently with a spoon so they start soaking up all that custard. Let it sit for 2 minutes — this is the move that makes the casserole rich instead of dry.
  5. 5Fold in the diced ham, the sautéed onion and pepper mixture, and 1 cup of the shredded cheese. Stir it all together until evenly distributed.
  6. 6Pour the entire mixture into your greased baking dish and spread it out evenly. Sprinkle the remaining 1/2 cup of cheese across the top.
  7. 7Bake uncovered for 35-40 minutes until the center is set, the edges are pulling away slightly, and the top is beautifully golden and bubbly. If you poke the center with a knife and it comes out clean, you're done.
  8. 8Let it rest for 5 minutes before slicing — I know it's hard to wait but it'll hold together so much better. Serve with hot sauce on the side and some sliced scallions if you're feeling fancy.
💡 Improv Tip

Listen, the bread situation is flexible — leftover dinner rolls from Easter dinner are absolutely incredible here because they're already a little rich and soft, but even a few slices of regular sandwich bread work perfectly. The key is that it's slightly stale or at least a day old so it absorbs the egg mixture without turning mushy. Fresh bread from this morning will make it a little soggy in the middle, so if that's all you've got, spread the chunks on a baking sheet and pop them in the oven at 300°F for about 8 minutes to dry them out first.
