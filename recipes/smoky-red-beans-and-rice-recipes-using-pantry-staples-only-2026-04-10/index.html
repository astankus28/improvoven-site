Smoky Red Beans and Rice — Recipes Using Pantry Staples Only

Published April 10, 2026 · Improv Oven

This one is for everyone who's ever stared into their cabinet wondering what to make — these recipes using pantry staples only prove you don't need a grocery run to eat really, really well. We're talking smoky, hearty red beans slow-simmered with canned tomatoes, dried spices, and rice until everything gets thick and saucy and deeply satisfying. It's the kind of meal your abuela would approve of, and your wallet definitely will too.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
30 mins
Total
40 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) red kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, with juices
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice, uncooked
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth (from a bouillon cube works great)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil or any neutral oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional but recommended)
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar or white vinegar

Instructions

  1. 1Heat the oil in a medium saucepan or deep skillet over medium heat. Once it shimmers, add the garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, and cayenne. Let those spices bloom in the oil for about 60 seconds, stirring constantly — you'll smell them open up and that's exactly what you want. This step is the difference between flat beans and flavor-packed beans.
  2. 2Pour in the canned diced tomatoes with all their juices and stir to combine with the spiced oil. Let it cook down for about 3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens slightly and the tomatoes start breaking apart.
  3. 3Add both cans of drained kidney beans and stir everything together so every bean gets coated in that smoky tomato base. Drop in the bay leaf, pour in 1 cup of the water or broth, and season with salt and black pepper. Stir, bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to low. Let the beans simmer uncovered for 15 to 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and clings to the beans beautifully.
  4. 4While the beans simmer, cook your rice. In a separate small saucepan, combine the rice with the remaining 1 cup of water or broth and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, cover, reduce to the lowest heat, and cook for 15 minutes. Remove from heat and let it steam, covered, for 5 more minutes. Fluff with a fork.
  5. 5Right before serving, fish out the bay leaf from the beans and stir in the vinegar. That little splash of acid at the end wakes everything up — don't skip it. Taste and adjust salt as needed.
  6. 6Serve the smoky red beans over a scoop of fluffy rice in a bowl. If you've got hot sauce or dried chili flakes sitting around, now's the time to use them.
💡 Improv Tip

If your canned tomatoes taste a little flat or tinny, let them cook down a full extra 5 minutes in step two before adding the beans — that extra time caramelizes the sugars and knocks out that metallic edge. Also, a bouillon cube dissolved in hot water is genuinely a pantry MVP for this recipe. Chicken, beef, or veggie all work, and each one takes the dish in a slightly different direction.
