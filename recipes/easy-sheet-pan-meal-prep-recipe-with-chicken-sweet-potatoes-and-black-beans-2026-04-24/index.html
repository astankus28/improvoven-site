Easy Sheet Pan Meal Prep Recipe with Chicken, Sweet Potatoes, and Black Beans

Published April 24, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy sheet pan meal prep recipe is your new Sunday secret weapon — one pan, one oven, zero stress, and four days of real meals handled. We're talking juicy spiced chicken thighs, caramelized sweet potatoes, and crispy black beans all roasting together while you do literally anything else. Very Miami, very practical, very delicious.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
35 mins
Total
45 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, cubed into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow onion, sliced into wedges
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro for serving (optional but come on, do it)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 425°F and line a large sheet pan with foil or parchment paper. You want room for everything to breathe — crowded pan equals steamed food, and we're not doing that.
  2. 2In a small bowl, mix together the garlic powder, cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, onion powder, a generous pinch of salt, and black pepper. This is your spice blend and it smells incredible.
  3. 3Pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels — this is the move for getting crispy skin. Drizzle them with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, then rub the spice blend all over them, getting under the skin if you can.
  4. 4Toss the sweet potato cubes, bell pepper, and onion wedges in the remaining 2 tablespoons of olive oil with a pinch of salt and pepper. Spread them out on one side of the sheet pan.
  5. 5Nestle the chicken thighs on the other side of the pan, skin side up. They need their own space to get golden — don't let them hide under the vegetables.
  6. 6Scatter the drained black beans around the vegetables. They'll get a little crispy on the outside and creamy inside. Trust the process.
  7. 7Roast everything at 425°F for 35 minutes, or until the chicken skin is deep golden brown and a thermometer reads 165°F in the thickest part. No need to flip anything.
  8. 8Pull the pan out, squeeze that lime juice over everything while it's still hot, and let it rest for five minutes. Finish with fresh cilantro if you have it. Divide into four containers and your whole week just got easier.
💡 Improv Tip

If your sheet pan is on the smaller side, use two pans instead of crowding everything onto one — a crowded pan steams instead of roasts and you'll miss out on all that beautiful caramelization. Also, boneless thighs work great here and cut cook time down to about 25 minutes if you're in a hurry.
