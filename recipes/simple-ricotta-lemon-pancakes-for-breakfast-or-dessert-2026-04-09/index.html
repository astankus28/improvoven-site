Simple Ricotta Lemon Pancakes for Breakfast or Dessert

Published April 9, 2026 · Improv Oven

These simple ricotta lemon pancakes started as a breakfast flex and turned into the dessert everyone asks me to bring to the party — fluffy, creamy, with that bright citrus pop that makes you close your eyes for a second. Think of them like a cross between a pancake and a cheesecake that decided to keep it casual. Stack them high, drizzle with honey or fresh berries, and watch people lose their minds.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole milk ricotta cheese
  • 2 large eggs, separated
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice (about half a lemon)
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter or neutral oil for the pan
  • Powdered sugar for dusting
  • Honey or fresh berries for serving

Instructions

  1. 1In a large bowl, combine the ricotta, egg yolks, sugar, lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract. Stir everything together until it looks smooth and a little dreamy — that ricotta is going to make these pancakes incredibly tender.
  2. 2Add the flour, baking powder, and salt to the ricotta mixture and fold gently until just combined. Don't overwork it — a few lumps are your friends here.
  3. 3In a separate clean bowl, beat the egg whites with a hand mixer or a whisk until stiff peaks form. This is the secret step that makes these pancakes impossibly fluffy instead of dense. Take the extra three minutes, trust me.
  4. 4Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the ricotta batter in two additions, using a rubber spatula and a light hand. You want to keep all that air you just worked so hard to build.
  5. 5Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-low heat and add a small pat of butter. When it foams and settles, you're ready to go.
  6. 6Scoop about 3 tablespoons of batter per pancake onto the skillet. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes until the edges look set and the bottom is golden, then flip carefully and cook another 1 to 2 minutes. These are more delicate than regular pancakes so keep the heat moderate and be patient.
  7. 7Serve warm in a stack dusted with powdered sugar, drizzled with honey, and topped with fresh berries if you've got them. For full dessert mode, a scoop of vanilla ice cream on top is absolutely not wrong.
💡 Improv Tip

If your ricotta looks watery straight out of the container, drain it for 10 minutes in a fine mesh strainer or through a paper towel — excess moisture will make your batter too loose and the pancakes will spread flat instead of puffing up nice. This one little move makes all the difference between good pancakes and great ones.
