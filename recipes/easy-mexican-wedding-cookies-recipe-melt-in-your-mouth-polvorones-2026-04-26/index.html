Easy Mexican Wedding Cookies Recipe (Melt-in-Your-Mouth Polvorones)

Published April 26, 2026 · Improv Oven

This easy Mexican wedding cookies recipe is the one you'll make every single time someone says 'bring something homemade' — because they look fancy but they're secretly effortless. We're talking buttery, nutty little snowballs rolled in powdered sugar that disappear off the plate before they even cool down. Abuela energy, pantry ingredients, zero stress.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
18 mins
Total
33 mins
Serves
24 cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar, plus 1 cup more for rolling
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup finely chopped pecans or walnuts
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon (optional but highly recommended)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone mat. If you don't have either, just leave the pan ungreased — these cookies have enough butter to handle themselves.
  2. 2In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and 1/2 cup of powdered sugar together until it's light, fluffy, and looks almost whipped — about 2 minutes with a hand mixer or 3-4 minutes if you're going old school with a wooden spoon and some muscle.
  3. 3Mix in the vanilla extract, then add the flour and salt. Stir until a soft dough forms. It'll look crumbly at first — keep going, it comes together. Fold in the chopped nuts and cinnamon if using.
  4. 4Roll the dough into 1-inch balls — roughly the size of a large grape. Place them about an inch apart on your prepared baking sheet. They don't spread much, so no need to leave a ton of space.
  5. 5Bake for 16 to 18 minutes, until the bottoms are just barely golden and the tops look set but still pale. Don't wait for them to brown on top — that means they're overdone and you'll lose that melt-in-your-mouth magic.
  6. 6Let the cookies cool for just 5 minutes — you want them warm but not scalding. While they're still warm, roll each cookie generously in the extra powdered sugar. Set them on a rack to finish cooling, then roll them in powdered sugar one more time for that beautiful snowy coating. That second roll is the move — don't skip it.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret weapon here is toasting your pecans or walnuts in a dry skillet for 3-4 minutes before chopping them. Just medium heat, keep them moving, pull them off when they smell nutty and amazing. It takes five extra minutes and it makes the whole cookie taste more expensive. Also — butter temperature actually matters here. Cold butter will fight you, melted butter will make the dough greasy. Room temp, soft enough to leave an indent with your finger, that's the sweet spot.
