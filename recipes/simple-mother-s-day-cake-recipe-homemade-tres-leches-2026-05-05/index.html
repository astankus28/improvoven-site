Simple Mother's Day Cake Recipe (Homemade Tres Leches)

Published May 5, 2026 · Improv Oven

This simple Mother's Day cake recipe homemade from scratch is the one that's going to make her cry happy tears — and honestly, you deserve that moment. Tres leches is basically a Miami abuela's love language: a fluffy sponge cake soaked in three kinds of milk until it's impossibly moist, then piled high with fresh whipped cream. It sounds fancy, it looks stunning, and the ingredients are all stuff you probably already have in your pantry.

Prep
20 mins
Cook
30 mins
Total
50 mins plus 2 hours chilling
Serves
12

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 5 large eggs, separated
  • 1 cup granulated sugar, divided
  • 1/3 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 1 cup whole milk (for the three-milk soak)
  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 3 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (for the whipped cream)
  • Ground cinnamon for dusting
  • Fresh strawberries or maraschino cherries for topping (optional but do it)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 350°F and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish — butter it up real good, every corner.
  2. 2In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set that aside.
  3. 3Separate your eggs into two large bowls. To the yolks, add 3/4 cup of the granulated sugar and beat with a hand mixer on medium-high for about 2 minutes until the mixture is pale yellow and thick. Mix in the 1/3 cup whole milk and the teaspoon of vanilla.
  4. 4Gently fold the flour mixture into the yolk mixture using a spatula until just combined. Don't overwork it — lumps are okay, tough cake is not.
  5. 5In the other bowl, beat your egg whites with clean beaters until soft peaks form. Gradually add the remaining 1/4 cup sugar and keep beating until you get stiff, glossy peaks — like a meringue.
  6. 6Fold the egg whites into the batter in three additions, being gentle and using big swooping motions from the bottom of the bowl up. This is what makes your cake fluffy. Take your time here.
  7. 7Pour the batter into your prepared dish and bake for 25 to 30 minutes, until the top is golden and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. 8While the cake bakes, whisk together the sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and 1 cup whole milk in a bowl or large measuring cup. This is your tres leches soak — it smells incredible.
  9. 9Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then use a fork or skewer to poke holes all over the top — go crazy, every inch of that cake needs holes.
  10. 10Slowly pour the three-milk mixture over the warm cake. It's going to look like a lot. Pour it all. Let it soak in, pour more, let it keep soaking. The cake is thirsty and it will absorb every drop. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight if you can wait.
  11. 11When you're ready to serve, make the whipped cream: beat the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla together until soft peaks form. Spread it all over the top of the cold cake in big swoops.
  12. 12Dust lightly with ground cinnamon and decorate with fresh strawberries or cherries. Slice into squares and watch everyone go quiet at the table — that's how you know you did it.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret move here is making this cake the night before Mother's Day. Overnight in the fridge means the milk soak fully absorbs and the flavors come together in a way that a 2-hour chill just can't match. Add the whipped cream the morning of, right before you present it. She will think you are a professional pastry chef and you should let her believe that.
