Budget Tamale Casserole Skillet

Published April 10, 2026 · Improv Oven

This budget tamale casserole skillet recipe is what happens when you raid the pantry and accidentally make something your whole family asks for again the next week. We're talking all the cozy, corny, spiced-up goodness of tamales without the two-day process — just one skillet and about 30 minutes. It's hearty, it's satisfying, and it costs less than a fast food run.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
20 mins
Total
30 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef or ground turkey
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes with green chiles, undrained
  • 1 cup frozen or canned corn, drained
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt (for cornbread layer)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar or Mexican blend cheese
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, sliced jalapeños, chopped cilantro, hot sauce

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 400°F. Pull out a 10 or 12-inch oven-safe skillet — cast iron is ideal but any oven-safe pan works.
  2. 2Set the skillet over medium-high heat and add the ground beef or turkey. Break it apart with a spoon and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. Drain off excess fat if needed.
  3. 3Add the diced onion to the skillet and cook with the meat for 2 to 3 minutes until it softens up. Then stir in the garlic and cook another 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. 4Add the black beans, diced tomatoes with chiles, corn, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Stir everything together and let it simmer for 3 to 4 minutes so the flavors come together. Taste it and adjust the seasoning — you're in charge here.
  5. 5While the filling simmers, make your cornbread topping. In a bowl, whisk together the cornmeal, milk, egg, butter, baking powder, and salt until just combined. Don't overmix — a few lumps are totally fine.
  6. 6Sprinkle half the cheese directly over the meat and bean filling in the skillet. Then pour the cornbread batter evenly over the top, spreading it gently with a spoon or spatula to cover.
  7. 7Scatter the remaining cheese over the cornbread batter and transfer the skillet to the oven. Bake for 18 to 22 minutes until the cornbread is set in the center, golden on top, and pulling away slightly from the edges.
  8. 8Let it rest for 5 minutes before serving — this helps everything hold together when you scoop it. Dish it out straight from the skillet and pile on your toppings.
💡 Improv Tip

If you only have a box of Jiffy corn muffin mix in the pantry, use that instead of making the cornbread layer from scratch — just mix it according to the box and spoon it right on top. Nobody's judging you, and honestly it comes out great. That's the whole spirit of Improv Oven.
