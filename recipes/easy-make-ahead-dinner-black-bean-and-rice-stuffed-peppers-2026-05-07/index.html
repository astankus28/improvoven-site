Easy Make-Ahead Dinner: Black Bean and Rice Stuffed Peppers

Published May 7, 2026 · Improv Oven

If you've been searching for easy make-ahead dinner recipes that actually taste good the next day — maybe even better — these stuffed peppers are about to become your new weeknight secret weapon. We're loading up bell peppers with seasoned black beans, fluffy rice, cumin, sofrito, and a little melted cheese on top, all from stuff you probably already have sitting around. Make a big batch Sunday, and Tuesday-you is going to be very, very grateful.

Prep
15 mins
Cook
40 mins
Total
55 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers, any color, tops cut off and seeds removed
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice, uncooked
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 2 tablespoons sofrito (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a blend)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1Preheat your oven to 375°F. While it heats up, bring the water or broth to a boil in a medium saucepan. Add the rice, stir once, drop the heat to low, cover, and let it cook for 18 minutes until fluffy. Pull it off the heat and let it sit covered for 5 more minutes — don't peek.
  2. 2While the rice does its thing, heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the sofrito and let it sizzle for about 2 minutes until it smells incredible. Add the drained black beans, diced tomatoes, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together and cook for 5 minutes until the flavors come together and most of the liquid cooks off.
  3. 3Fold the cooked rice into the bean mixture in the skillet. Taste it — this is your moment. Adjust salt, add a pinch more cumin if you want, make it yours. This filling is the soul of the whole dish.
  4. 4Stand the hollowed bell peppers upright in a baking dish. If they're wobbly, trim just a tiny sliver off the bottom so they sit flat — don't cut a hole through them. Spoon the filling generously into each pepper, pressing it down gently so you can pack in as much as possible.
  5. 5Top each stuffed pepper with a good handful of shredded cheese. Cover the baking dish tightly with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then remove the foil and bake another 10 to 15 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden and the peppers are tender when you poke them with a fork.
  6. 6Let them rest for 5 minutes before serving — they'll be lava hot inside. Scatter some fresh cilantro on top if you've got it. That's it. Dinner is done.
💡 Improv Tip

This is the make-ahead magic: assemble the stuffed peppers completely, cover the dish with foil, and stash them in the fridge for up to 3 days before baking. When you're ready, just pull them out, let them sit on the counter for 15 minutes to take the chill off, then bake as directed. You can also freeze the filling separately for up to 2 months — just thaw, stuff, and bake whenever life calls for it.
