Simple Michelada Recipe for Cinco de Mayo Brunch — With Spicy Huevos Rancheros

Published April 18, 2026 · Improv Oven

A simple michelada recipe for Cinco de Mayo is basically mandatory if you're doing brunch right, and pairing it with crispy huevos rancheros makes it a full morning meal worth waking up for. Think cold, tangy, spicy beer in a chili-salt rimmed glass next to runny eggs soaking into warm tortillas — that's the kind of breakfast that makes your neighbors jealous. This is the Cinco de Mayo brunch combo you didn't know you needed, built from stuff you probably already have.

Prep
10 mins
Cook
15 mins
Total
25 mins
Serves
4

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 corn tortillas
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 small white onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeño, diced (seeds in if you want the heat)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for topping (optional but do it)
  • 4 cold Mexican lagers (Modelo, Corona, or whatever's on sale)
  • 4 tablespoons hot sauce (Valentina or Cholula preferred)
  • 4 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 4 tablespoons fresh lime juice (about 3 limes)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Maggi seasoning (or more soy sauce if you don't have it)
  • Chili powder and coarse salt for rimming the glasses
  • Ice cubes
  • Lime wedges and cucumber slices for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1Start your micheladas first so they're cold and waiting for you when the food is done — that's the move. Run a lime wedge around the rim of four tall glasses, then dip each rim into a small plate mixed with equal parts chili powder and coarse salt. Set the glasses aside.
  2. 2In each glass, combine 1 tablespoon hot sauce, 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce, 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice, a splash of soy sauce, and a few drops of Maggi if you have it. Stir that up, drop in some ice, then slowly pour in your cold lager at an angle so it doesn't foam over. Give it a gentle stir, garnish with a lime wedge and a cucumber slice, and park it in the fridge while you cook.
  3. 3Now get your ranchero sauce going. Heat the oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for about 3 minutes until softened and translucent. Add the garlic and jalapeño and cook another minute until fragrant — your kitchen should smell incredible right now.
  4. 4Add the diced tomatoes, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together and let it simmer on medium-low for about 8 minutes until the sauce thickens up and the flavors come together. Taste it and adjust — more hot sauce, more salt, you're the boss.
  5. 5Add the drained black beans to the sauce and stir to combine. Let them warm through for about 2 minutes. This is your base — rich, spicy, and deeply savory.
  6. 6While the sauce simmers, warm your corn tortillas directly on a gas burner flame for about 30 seconds per side for that charred edge, or in a dry skillet over high heat. Stack them under a clean kitchen towel to keep them soft and warm.
  7. 7Create four small wells in the sauce using the back of a spoon. Crack one egg into each well, then cover the skillet with a lid and cook on medium-low heat for about 3 to 4 minutes for runny yolks or 5 to 6 minutes if you want them set. Watch them — overcooked eggs in huevos rancheros is a heartbreak.
  8. 8Place a warm tortilla on each plate. Spoon out an egg with a generous scoop of the bean and ranchero sauce on top of each tortilla. Hit it with fresh cilantro and an extra squeeze of lime. Pull those micheladas out of the fridge and serve everything together immediately.
💡 Improv Tip

The secret to a great michelada is cold everything — cold glass, cold beer, cold base. Mix your michelada base the night before Cinco de Mayo and keep it in a jar in the fridge. When your guests show up, all you're doing is rimming glasses and pouring beer. Nobody needs to know how easy it was.
